Humidity Experiments

(Q5000 SA Only)

 

Templates are available to help guide a new Q5000 SA user in setting up common humidity experiments. Those templates (shown below) can also be accessed through the Wizard to further simplify the process.

  • Setting Up a Humidity Ramp Procedure:  This experiment measures the amount of moisture adsorbed while ramping relative humidity at a constant rate and holding at a constant temperature.

  • Setting Up a Isohume Procedure:  This experiment maintains relative humidity constant while either holding temperature constant or increasing temperature in a stepped fashion.

  • Setting Up a Sorption Isotherm Procedure:  This experiment increases the relative humidity in a stepped fashion while holding the sample at a constant temperature.

  • Setting Up a Adsorption Desorption Procedure:  This experiment measures the amount of moisture adsorbed and desorbed over a range of relative humidity as the humidity is increased and then decreased in a stepped fashion while holding the sample at constant temperature.

  • See also: Guidelines for Performing Humidity Experiments below.

Guidelines for Performing Humidity Experiments

When performing Q5000 SA humidity experiments at the temperature and humidity extremes for the instrument, there are several considerations that are important:

  • Experiment will be performed at temperatures > 60°C.

The Q5000 SA humidity chamber can be used over the temperature range 5 to 85°C. However, the lifetime of the sample and reference humidity sensors will be adversely affected if the sensors are used above 60°C, particularly at higher humidities. Therefore, the sensors should be removed before operating in that region. Removal and replacement of those sensors is an easy task and is described in Replacing the Q5000 SA Humidity Sensor.  

NOTE: The humidity in the Q5000 SA humidity chamber is achieved and maintained by precise control of  the two mass flow controllers which regulate the ratio of dry and wet gas mixed to achieve the requested humidity. Removal of these humidity sensors will not affect overall performance of the system.

  • Experiment will be performed at a temperature > 60°C and a humidity > 80 %RH.

Moisture condensation can occur inside the humidity chamber, particularly on the hang-down wire and close-out disk, as the requested humidity approaches 98 %RH. This effect is more prevalent at higher temperatures (>60°C) and humidities above 80 %RH where the dew point temperature approaches the method temperature, and becomes more likely the longer the dwell times at those conditions. Condensation generally appears as a sudden weight change and can be either a gain or loss in weight depending on whether the condensation occurs on the sample or reference side of the chamber.  If condensation occurs, it can usually be removed (dried out) by closing the chamber and holding the system at a low humidity for a period of time.

  • Experiment will be performed at temperatures less than 25°C.

The Q5000 SA humidity chamber covers the temperature range of 5 to 85°C. However, it is likely that moisture condensation will occur on the outside of the chamber when operating for long periods at 5°C under laboratory humidity conditions that exceed 25 %RH at 25°C. At a chamber temperature of 10°C, the lab humidity at 25°C should not exceed 35 %RH.  

NOTE: Condensation on the outside of the chamber is largely a cosmetic issue and will not affect experimental results or damage the instrument.
