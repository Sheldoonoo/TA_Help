Monitoring Real-Time Signals

 

Real-time instrument signals may be monitored from either the instrument touch screen or from the controller Signal Display pane. If you choose to use the touch screen, you can access either the Status or Display options. See also: Display Menu Touch Screen.

Controller Signal Display Pane

The Signal Display pane (shown to the right) is a customizable instrument control window that allows the user to select the real time signals they want displayed during an experiment. These same signals are also available for plotting in the RealTime Plot window.

See also:  Overview of the Instrument Control Window

 

Signal Display Pop-up Menu

The TA Instruments TMA displays a certain set of information on the Signal Display window. Using the pop-up menu (accessed by right clicking), you can customize the signal display by selecting Customize Signal Display.  When the Customize Signal Display window appears, select or clear the signals then click OK to close the signal selection window and display the new signals. You can reorganize the order of the signals by clicking on a signal name to display a drop-down list of signals to select from.

TMA Instrument Signals

Listed below are some of the possible real-time TMA signals. The available signals are dependent on instrument mode and options installed. These signals are available for display within the Signal Display pane, as RealTime plot signals, and they are displayed on the instrument touch screen, if available. A subset of these signals are available as stored signals within the data file.

For a detailed explanation of the signals shown on the window, see the table below:

 

Signal

Description

Creep Compliance

The ratio of time-dependent strain to stress in the creep phase of a creep test.

Dimension Change

The dimension change signal is displayed when running TMA experiments.  It is the change in sample dimension registered by the probe as it moves up or down.

Force

The force signal is the amount of force, in Newtons, currently being exerted by the probe on the sample.

Force Amplitude

 

Back to top

Force amplitude is the magnitude of the force sine wave being applied to the sample (measured zero to peak).

Frequency (of oscillation)

The number of oscillation cycles completed in one second.

Heater Power

The amount of electrical power being applied to the heater.

Heater Temperature

The current temperature of the heater.

Length

The measured length of the sample.

Length Amplitude

Length Amplitude is the magnitude of the displacement sine wave measured at the sample after it is corrected for the oscillatory compliance of the probe.

Length Amplitude Uncalibrated

Length Amplitude Uncalibrated is the magnitude of the displacement sine wave measured at the sample.  This is a raw signal used for calibration.  Amplitude is measured zero to peak.

Loss

 

Back to top

 

The resistance of the sample to the oscillatory force (Stiffness) is expressed as two components. The stiffness that is in-phase with the input force is called Storage.  The stiffness that is 90 degrees out of phase with the input force is called Loss.

Loss Modulus

 

The calculated ability of a material to dissipate mechanical energy by converting it into heat.

Method Time

The total time that the current method has been running.

Modulated Dimension Change

The measured periodic sample dimension change in response to the modulated temperature

Modulated Dimension Change Amplitude

The amplitude of the measured periodic sample dimension change.

Modulated Temperature

 

Back to top

The modulated temperature signal is displayed when running MTMA experiments.  It is the measured sample temperature. This signal is selectable and can be included in the data file.

Modulated Temperature Amplitude

The temperature amplitude signal is displayed when running MTMA experiments. It is the amplitude of the measured periodic temperature. This signal is selectable and can be included in the data file.

Nonreversing Alpha

The portion of the coefficient of expansion (alpha) due to processes that are not reversible within one period.

Nonreversing Dimension Change

The portion of the dimension change due to processes that are not reversible within one period.

Oscillating Force

The amount of oscillating force, in Newtons, currently being exerted by the probe on the sample.

Oscillating Length

Oscillating length is the instantaneous length of the sample as it is exposed to the sinusoidal force.  This is a diagnostic signal.

Phase

Phase is the lag of the delta-L response sine wave relative to the input force sine wave, expressed as an angle. For a purely elastic material the phase would be zero. For a purely viscous material the phase would be 90 degrees.

Phase Uncalibrated

Phase uncalibrated is the phase measurement before it is corrected for measurement delays and system effects.  This is a raw signal used for calibration.

Recovery

 

Back to top

Recovery is the percentage of displacement strain that has gone away since the removal of the creep force. 100% recovery means that no creep displacement remains.

Recoverable Compliance

The ratio of time-dependent strain to stress in the recovery phase of a creep recovery test.

Relaxation Modulus

 

Also called stress relaxation modulus, this is the ratio of time-dependent stress to strain in a stress relaxation experiment.

Remaining Run Time

The estimated total time remaining until completion of experiment.

Reversing Alpha

The portion of the coefficient of expansion (alpha) due to processes reversible within one period.

Reversing Dimension Change

The portion of the dimension change due to processes reversible within one period.

Sample Purge Flow

The flow rate of the purge gas being supplied to the sample.

Secant Modulus

Secant Modulus is the slope of a line from the zero stress, zero strain point to the current point on the stress vs. strain curve. The startup value of Secant Modulus (in the sample's linearly-elastic zone) is known as "Young's Modulus."  Secant Modulus is available whenever both Stress and Strain are being calculated.

Segment Time

The total time that the current segment has been running.

Set Point Temperature

The temperature that the furnace is requested to attain.

Stiffness

In TMA, the sample stiffness is defined as the force applied to the sample divided by the amplitude of deformation. The stiffness of a material is dependent on its geometry (physical dimensions).

Storage

 

Back to top

The resistance of the sample to the oscillatory force (Stiffness) is expressed as two components.  The stiffness that is in-phase with the input force is called Storage.  The stiffness that is 90 degrees out of phase with the input force is called Loss.

Storage Modulus

The calculated ability of a material to store mechanical energy as determined by its stiffness or rigidity.

Strain

The measurement of deformation, relative to a reference configuration of length, area, or volume. Also called relative deformation.

Strain Setpoint

When strain control is active, the target level of strain to be maintained is called Strain Setpoint.  This signal is only valid when strain control is active.  At other times it holds the last value set.

Stress

A force per unit area.

Tan Delta

Tan Delta is the ratio of loss modulus to storage modulus (E''/E' or G''/G'). Tan Delta is a useful index of material viscoelasticity since it is a ratio of viscous and elastic moduli.

Tangent Modulus

Tangent Modulus is the instantaneous slope of the stress vs. strain curve during a stress (force) or strain (displacement) ramp. The Tangent Modulus is undefined until the force or strain ramp is established.  

Temperature

The current temperature of the sample.

Total Alpha

A TMA signal.

Uncalibrated Temperature

The temperature of the sample before calibration is applied.
