Message 1022

The combination of temperature and humidity selected may result in moisture condensation.

Problem:

You have selected a temperature and humidity that fall outside of the recommended range:

  • Experiments should be performed at a temperature > 60°C and a humidity > 80 %RH.

Moisture condensation can occur inside the humidity chamber, particularly on the hang-down wire and close-out disk, as the requested humidity approaches 98 %RH. This effect is more prevalent at higher temperatures (>60°C) and humidities above 80 %RH where the dew point temperature approaches the method temperature, and becomes more likely the longer the dwell times at those conditions. Condensation generally appears as a sudden weight change and can be either a gain or loss in weight depending on whether the condensation occurs on the sample or reference side of the chamber.

Solution:

Change the experiment to use a temperature below 60°C and a humidity below 80 %RH or remove the humidity sensors. Follow the instructions in the Q5000 online help to remove and reinstall the humidity sensors.

If condensation occurs, it can usually be removed (dried out) by closing the chamber and holding the system at a low humidity for a period of time.
