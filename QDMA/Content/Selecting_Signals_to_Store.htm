Selecting DMA Signals to Store

 

Every analytical instrument produces distinctive signals that may or may not be stored in a data file. To select the signals that you want to be saved in data files produced during this experiment, access the Summary Page in Experimental View. Click the button. Check the signals to be stored from the list shown. Then click the OK button. The signals selected apply only to the current run.

This list will vary depending on the mode chosen.

Some of the signals for the DMA are listed below. See Monitoring Real-Time Signals for a complete listing.

  • Method Time (min) = Time since run start.

  • Temperature (°C) = Sample temperature.

  • Storage Modulus (MPa)/Loss Modulus (MPa) = Type of modulus (Young's, bending, compression, or shear) depends on the clamp type.

  • Tan Delta = Ratio of loss modulus to storage modulus.

  • Frequency (Hz) = (of oscillation) The number of oscillation cycles completed in one second.

  • Oscillation Amplitude (mm) = sample dynamic amplitude.

  • Position (mm) = The position of the clamp relative to the slide zero position.

  • Displacement (mm) = position of the tensioning clamp relative to a zero point.

  • Static Force (N)  = applied static force to tensioning clamps.

  • Stress (Pa) = A force per unit area.

  • Strain (%) = The measurement of deformation relative to a reference configuration of length, area, or volume.  Also called relative deformation.

  • Creep Compliance (µm²/N or 1/Pa) = The ratio of time-dependent strain to stress in the creep phase of a creep recovery test.

  • Strain Recovery (%) = The percentage of strain recovered in the recovery phase of a creep recovery test.

  • Relaxation modulus (Pa) = Also called stress relaxation modulus.  The ratio of time-dependent stress to strain in a stress relaxation experiment.

  • Decay time (min) = the time in a stress relaxation experiment over which the relaxation of stress is measured.
