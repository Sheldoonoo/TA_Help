Measuring System Overview

 

The measuring system is defined as those parts that are in direct contact with the sample or material. A measuring system consists of two parts:

  • One is the fixed member (or Stator), which is either the Peltier plate or a system attached to the Peltier plate (in the case of concentric cylinders or when the Extended Temperature Module is in use.).
  • The second part (the geometry) is attached to the driving motor spindle where it is locked in position by means of the draw rod. The draw rod is detachable and passes through the hole bored in the spindle. The geometry constitutes the moving member (the Rotor).

There are several types of measuring systems available. The most common ones are listed below:

  • Cone and Plate
  • Parallel Plate
  • Concentric Cylinders
  • Double Concentric Cylinders

The entire range of measuring geometries are available in a variety of materials, including stainless steel, acrylic, aluminium and titanium. All geometries greater than 25 mm are of the Solvent trap type, (used to restrict solvent evaporation during an experiment.) Also available are 'cross-hatched' systems, which can be used if slippage at the sample-geometry interface is a problem.
