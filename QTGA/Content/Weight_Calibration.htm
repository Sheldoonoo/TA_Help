Calibrating the TGA Weight

 

Weight calibration should be performed on the TGA at least once a month. Because the TGA has two weight ranges, taring is done for both ranges. The tare weight is stored by the instrument for the appropriate weight range.

Select Calibrate/Weight from the menu or click on located on the tool bar to perform weight calibration.

The weight calibration functions guide you through the calibration procedure step-by-step. You will need to obtain the following items for this procedure:

  • two (2) empty sample pans

  • calibration weights

  • brass tweezers

NOTE: Always handle the calibration weights with brass tweezers, not with your fingers. The oils and salt from your skin can change the calibration weight.

Scroll through the steps for more detailed information on weight calibration or, if you want help for a particular step in the calibration, select the step number below:

Step 1: Electronic Balance Tare

Follow the instructions appropriate to the model instrument you are using:

Current QTGA Model

  1. Turn on the instrument.

  2. Press the FURNACE key to lower the furnace.

  3. Using the ball driver supplied in your TGA accessory kit, loosen the six captive screws securing the balance chamber faceplate to the instrument and remove the faceplate.

  4. Loosen and remove the thumbscrew holding the balance cover on the sample (left) side of the balance mechanism and take off the cover.

  5. Loosen and remove the thumbscrew holding the balance cover on the tare (right) side of the balance mechanism and take off the cover.

  6. Locate the tare hang-down wire. See the figure to the right.

  7. Hang an empty sample pan of the same type and size as your experimental sample pan on the tare hook. This is your tare pan.

  8. Place an empty sample pan that you plan to use in your experiment on the sample platform.

  9. Press the Load Pan key to load the pan onto the sample hook and close the furnace. Closing the furnace prevent air currents from affecting the weight reading.

  10. Press Next to begin the electronic tare.

Previous QTGA Model (with Tare Tube)

  1. Unscrew the tare tube in a clockwise direction, then remove it.

  2. Hang an empty sample pan of the same type and size as your experimental sample pan on the tare hook. This is your tare pan.

  3. Replace the tare tube by turning it in a counterclockwise direction to screw it back into the instrument. This will prevent air currents from affecting the weight measurement.

  4. Place an empty sample pan that you plan to use in your experiment on the sample platform.

  5. Press the Load Pan key to load the pan onto the sample hook and close the furnace. Closing the furnace prevent air currents from affecting the weight reading.

  6. Press Next to begin the electronic tare.

Step 2: Zero the 200 mg Range

The instrument is measuring the weight in the 200 mg range. The tare weight is stored by the instrument for this range.

  1. Select Next to go on with the calibration procedure.

  2. Once the weight is stabilized, select Accept. (Press Back to stop the weight stabilization and return to the beginning of this step, if desired.)

Once complete, the calibration procedure will automatically advance to the next step.

Step 3: Calibrate the 200 mg Range

This step in the procedure calibrates the 200 mg weight range for the TGA instrument.

  1. Click Unload Pan to unload the sample pan.

  2. Obtain the 100 mg class 1 standard weight from the TGA Accessory Kit.

  3. Place the 100 mg weight in the sample pan.

  4. Select the Load Pan key to load the pan onto the sample hook and close the furnace. Closing the furnace prevent air currents from affecting the weight reading.

  5. Enter the exact mass of the standard (default value = 200.0 mg), then select Next. The system will begin measuring the combined weight of the standard and the pan.

  6. Once the weight is stabilized, select Accept. (Press Back to stop the weight stabilization and return to the beginning of this step, if desired.)

NOTE:  After pressing Accept, the results are sent to the instrument. This may take a minute to complete. Wait until the Next button is enabled to proceed.

Step 4: Zero the 1000 mg Range

The instrument is measuring the weight in the 1000 mg range. The tare weight is stored by the instrument for the 1000 mg range.

  1. Click Unload Pan to unload the sample pan. Remove the previous weight, but leave the pan in place.

  2. Select the Load Pan key to load the emptied pan and close the furnace.

  3. Select Next to go on with the calibration procedure.

  4. Once the weight is stabilized, select Accept. (Press Back to stop the weight stabilization and return to the beginning of this step, if desired.)

  5. Wait until the calibration information is saved. Once complete, the calibration procedure will automatically advance to the next step.

NOTE:  After pressing Accept, the results are sent to the instrument. This may take a minute to complete. Wait until the Next button is enabled to proceed.

Step 5: Calibrate the 1000 mg Range

This step in the procedure calibrates the 1000 mg weight range for the TGA instrument.

  1. Obtain the 1000 mg class 1 standard weight from the TGA Accessory Kit.

  2. Click Unload Pan to unload the sample pan.

  3. Place the 1000 mg weight on the empty sample pan.

  4. Select the Load Pan key to load the pan and close the furnace. Closing the furnace prevent air currents from affecting the weight reading.

  5. Enter the exact mass of the standard in the appropriate field of the TGA Mass Calibration window  (default value = 1000.0 mg), then select Next. The system will begin measuring the combined weight of the standard and the pan.

  6. Once the weight is stabilized, select Accept. (Press Back to stop the weight stabilization and return to the beginning of this step, if desired.)

  7. Wait until the calibration information is saved. Once complete the calibration procedure will automatically advance to the next step.

  8. Press Finish to unload the sample pan and exit weight calibration.
