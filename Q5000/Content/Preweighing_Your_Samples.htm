Preweighing Your Samples

 

When conducting experiments on the TGA you may wish to weigh the samples before the experiment begins in order to find out how much weight is lost by the samples while they are sitting on the sample tray waiting for the run to begin. To do this follow the steps below (it will take approximately two minutes to preweigh each pan):

  1. Load the samples into empty, tared sample pans.

  2. Select Calibrate/Preweigh Samples from the menu. The Autosampler Preweigh Utility window is displayed.

  3. Click on the desired pan number(s) from the list. You can also click the Select All button to choose all pan numbers for preweighing or click Deselect All to clear your selections.

  4. Select the Preweigh button to weigh each sample. The sample weight before the run, date, and time will be included in the Parameter Block for the data file for future recall.

NOTE: Choose the Abort button to stop this function.

  1. Click Close when finished.

  2. Readjust the pan placement on the sample tray, if necessary to correct for any alignment issues.

NOTE (Q5000 SA Only):  Some materials readily build up static electricity charges that can create problems during loading. This phenomenon is most likely to occur in experiments where the ambient humidity is low and/or when using uncoated quartz sample pans. Metal-coated quartz pans are available, which eliminate this problem by completing a grounding path to the balance housing.

NOTE:  Samples that might gain or lose weight, (based on adsorbing moisture or losing volatiles respectively) while sitting in the Autosampler tray awaiting analysis, are best analyzed using sealed aluminum pans which are punched just prior to analysis.
