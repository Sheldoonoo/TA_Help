Glossary of Terms

Introduction

This glossary is based mainly on the British Standard Glossary of Rheological Terms (BS 5168:1975). Most of the common terms used throughout this manual are included and brief descriptions are given. The symbols and units for the common terms are shown in the "Symbols and Units" section.

See also:Variables

A

Anti-thixotropy

An increase of the apparent viscosity constant shear stress or shear rate followed by a gradual recovery when the stress or shear rate is removed. The effect is time-dependent. (Also known as negative thixotropy or rheopexy ).

Apparent Viscosity

The shear stress divided by the rate of shear when this quotient is dependent on rate of shear. (Also called viscosity or shear viscosity ).

 

 

B

Bingham Model

A model with the behavior of an elastic solid up to the yield stress; above the yield stress, the differential viscosity is independent of the shear rate

Back to top

C

Complex Compliance

The mathematical representation of a compliance as the sum of real and imaginary part. The real part is sometimes called the storage compliance and the imaginary part, the loss compliance.

Complex Modulus

The mathematical representation of a modulus as the sum of a real and imaginary part. The real part is called the storage modulus and the imaginary part the loss modulus.

Complex Viscosity

The mathematical representation of a viscosity as the sum of a real part and an imaginary part. The real part is usually called the dynamic viscosity and the imaginary part is related to the real part of the complex shear modulus.

Compliance

The strain divided by the corresponding stress.

Consistency

A general term for the property of a material by which it resists permanent change of shape.

Couette Flow (circular)

Simple shear flow in the annulus between two co-axial cylinders in relative rotation.

Couette Flow

Simple flow between parallel plates in relative motion in their own plane.

Creep

The slow deformation of a material; usually measured under constant stress.

Back to top

D

Dashpot

A model for Newtonian viscous flow, typically represented by a piston moving in a cylinder of liquid.

Deborah Number

The ratio of a characteristic (relaxation) time of a material to a characteristic time of the relevant deformation process.

Deformation

A change of shape or volume or both.

Die Swell

A post-extrusion swelling.

Differential Viscosity

The derivative of shear stress with respect to shear rate.

Dilatancy

An increase in volume caused by deformation or shear thickening.

Dynamic Modulus

Synonym of complex modulus .

Dynamic Viscosity

The part of the stress in phase with the rate of strain divided by the rate of strain, under sinusoidal conditions.

Back to top

E

Elasticity

A reversible stress or strain behavior.

Elastic Energy

Synonym of strain energy .

Elastic Modulus

A stress divided by the corresponding elastic strain.

Elastico-viscous

A descriptive term for a liquid having both viscous and elastic properties.

Elongational Viscosity

Synonym of extensional viscosity .

Extensional Viscosity

The extensional stress divided by the rate of extension. (Also known as the Trouton viscosity.)

Back to top

F

Flow

A deformation, of which at least part is non-recoverable.

Flow Curve


A curve relating stress to rate of shear.

 

measurement system factors

H

Hooke Model

A model representing Hooke's Law of elasticity (e.g., a spring).

 

I

Instrinsic Viscosity

The limiting value of the reduced viscosity as the concentration approaches zero.

K

Kelvin Model

A mechanical model consisting of a Hooke model and a Newtonian fluid model in parallel. (Also called a Voigt unit .)

Kinematic Viscosity

The shear viscosity divided by the density of the material.

Back to top

L

Linear Viscoelasticity

Viscoelasticity characterised by a linear relationship between stress and strain.

Loss angle

The phase difference between the stress and strain in an oscillatory deformation.

Loss Compliance

The imaginary part of the complex compliance.

Loss Modulus

The imaginary part of the complex modulus.

Back to top

M

Maxwell Model

A mechanical model consisting of a Hooke model and a Newtonian fluid model in series.

Memory Fluid

Synonym of elastic liquid .

Modulus

The ratio of a component of stress to a component of strain.

 

N

Negative Thixotropy

Synonym of anti-thixotropy.

Back to top

Non-Newtonian Fluid

Any fluid whose behavior is dependent upon shear rate.

Normal Stress

The component of stress at right angles to the plane or vector under consideration.

P

Power Law Behavior

Behaviour characterised by a linear relationship between the logarithm of the shear stress and the logarithm of the rate of shear in simple shear flow.

Pseudoplasticity

Synonym of shear thinning.

Back to top

Procedure

A rheological test method (such as a viscometry test) that consists of the required parameters to operate the rheometer.

Procedures can be stored as Flow, Oscillation and Creep files.

R

Relaxation Time

The time taken for the shear stress of a fluid that obeys the Maxwell model to reduce to 1/e of its original equilibrium value on the cessation of steady shear flow.

Retardation Time

The time taken for the strain in a material that obeys the Kelvin model to reduce to 1/e of its original equilibrium value after the removal of the stress.

Back to top

Reynolds Number

The product of a typical apparatus length and a typical fluid speed divided by the kinematic viscosity of the fluid. It expresses the ratio of the inertia forces to the viscous forces.

Rheogram

A graph of a rheological relationship.

Rheology

The science of the deformation and flow of matter.

Rheometer

An instrument for measuring rheological properties.

Rheopexy

Synonym of anti-thixotropy .

Rigidity Modulus

Synonym of shear modulus .

Back to top

S

Shear

The movement of a layer of material relative to parallel adjacent layers.

Shear Compliance

The elastic shear strain divided by the corresponding shear stress.

Shear Modulus

The shear stress divided by the corresponding elastic shear strain.

Shear Rate

The change in shear strain per unit time.

Shear Strain

Relative deformation in shear.

Back to top

Shear Stress

The component of stress parallel to the area considered.

Shear Thickening

The increase of viscosity with increasing rate of shear in a steady shear flow.

Shear Thinning

The reduction of viscosity with increasing rate of shear in a steady shear flow.

Shear Viscosity

Synonym of apparent viscosity .

Steady flow

A flow in which the velocity at every point does not vary with time

Storage Compliance

That part of the shear strain that is in phase with the shear stress divided by the stress under sinusoidal conditions.

Storage Modulus

That part of the shear stress that is in phase with the shear strain divided by the strain under sinusoidal conditions.

Stored Energy

Synonym of strain energy .

Back to top

Strain

The measurement of deformation relative to a reference configuration of length, area or volume.

Strain Energy

The energy stored in a material by elastic strain.

Stress

Force per unit area.

Stress Relaxation

The decrease of shear stress on the cessation of steady shear flow, usually when the stress in the original steady shear flow has reached equilibrium.

 

T

Thixotropy

A decrease of the apparent viscosity under constant shear stress or shear rate, followed by a gradual recovery when the stress or shear rate is removed. The effect is time-dependent.

Time-Temperature Superposition

The scaling of the results of shear strain experiments carried out at different temperatures to fit onto a single curve.

Trouton Ratio

The ratio of extensional to shear viscosity.

Viscoelasticity

Having both viscous and elastic properties.

Viscometer

An instrument for the measurement of viscosity.

Viscosity

The property of a material to resist deformation increasingly with increasing rate of deformation. Mathematically it is defined as the shear stress divided by the rate of shear in simple shear flow.

Voigt Unit

Synonym of Kelvin model.

Young's Modulus
