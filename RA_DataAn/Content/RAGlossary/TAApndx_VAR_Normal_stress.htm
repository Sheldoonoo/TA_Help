Normal Stress

Description

The stress acting at right angles to the plane of shear, calculated from the normal force and the measuring system normal force factor. For a cone and plate, the normal stress is the first normal stress difference N1. For a parallel plate, the normal stress is the first normal stress difference, N1 minus the second normal stress difference, N2. For this type of geometry, the normal stress is calculated using the assumption that it is proportional to the shear rate experienced by the sample. The exact normal stress can be obtained by using the Stress correction transformation in the Rheology Advantage Data Module.
