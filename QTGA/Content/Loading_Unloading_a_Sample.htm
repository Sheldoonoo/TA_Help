Loading/Unloading a Sample

 

After the sample has been tared, you can load the sample into the TGA furnace as follows:

  1. Place the sample in the sample pan and position the pan on the sample platform. The wire on the bottom of the sample pan should align with the groove in the pan hole so that the sample pan can be picked up by the sample hang-down wire. NOTE:  Always use brass tweezers to handle the sample pans.

CAUTION:  Manually loading the sample pan onto the hang-down wire my damage the balance mechanism. It is best to allow the instrument to load the pan.

  1. Select Start and the pan will be automatically loaded onto the balance and the furnace will close or you can follow the individual steps below to perform the same function.

    1. Select Control/Sample/Load or press the LOAD key on the instrument touch screen/keypad. The TGA will automatically load the sample pan onto the balance.

    2. Position the thermocouple at the edge of the sample pan, rather than in the middle for the best results. The position of the thermocouple should be the same as it was during temperature calibration.

    3. Select Control/Furnace/Closed or press the FURNACE key on the instrument touch screen/keypad to close the furnace around the sample.

Loading a Sample Using the TGA Autosampler

Follow these instructions to load a sample using the Autosampler:

  1. Place each sample into its specific pan number as specified in the run sequence.

  2. Start the sequence. The sequence will begin with the scheduled run and continue until the end of the sequence. During the sequence, the specified pan number will be automatically loaded and the furnace closed until the end of the experiment when the current pan is unloaded and the pan number for the next run loaded. This sequence is repeated until the end of the run sequence.
