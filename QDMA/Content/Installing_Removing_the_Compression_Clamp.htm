Installing/Removing the Compression Clamp

 

The procedures that follow explain the installation and removal of the compression clamp, which is an optional DMA clamp fixture. The compression clamp is used to measure the properties of low to medium modulus materials.

Installing the Clamp

To install the compression clamp on the DMA, follow these steps, referring to the figures as needed:

  1. Remove any existing clamp assembly as instructed in the help topics for that clamp.

  2. Select the Mode and Test on the Summary Page.

  3. Press the CLAMP key on the touch screen to raise the drive shaft.

  4. Press the DRIVE Float/Lock key to lock the drive shaft.

  5. Verify that the sample and reference thermocouples do not interfere with the slide motion of the clamp and adjust the thermocouples, if necessary.

  6. Using the hex wrench, loosen the setscrew and slide the dovetail of the yoke into the dovetail holder on the drive shaft. See the figure below. Tighten the setscrew.

  1. Install the top bar and compression disk (15 mm or 40 mm) assembly onto the yoke. Leave the Allen (hex) screws slightly loose. This assembly makes up the moveable clamp. See the figure below.

  1. Click on the DMA Calibration button on the tool bar and then select the clamp type "Compression with All Calibrations."

  2. Calibrate the clamp mass. Click here for more information Calibrating DMA Clamps. After successfully completing the clamp mass calibration, click the Next button.

  3. Install the fixed clamp firmly onto all four of the mounting posts and tighten the screws with a hex wrench. Do not over tighten the screws and be careful not to damage the thermocouples.

  4. Screw the lower compression disk (15 mm or 40 mm) onto the fixed clamp as seen in the figure to the right. The clamp is now fully assembled.

  5. Using a Gauge Block Length of zero, calibrate the Clamp Zero. After successfully completing the Clamp Zero procedure, click the Next button.

  6. Calibrate the Clamp Compliance. Click here for more information Calibrating DMA Clamps.

  7. After successful completion of the Clamp Compliance Calibration, check the moveable clamp for alignment (front-to-rear parallelism, left-to-right parallelism, and concentricity).  Parallelism can be checked either visually or by pulling a piece of paper between the compression disks.  If they are properly aligned, the paper should drag evenly when pulled.  If the clamp needs to be realigned, use the procedures in the next section.

Aligning the Clamp

To align the compression clamp follow these steps:

NOTE:  The clamp in the figure below is shown rotated 90 degrees from the actual mounting configuration on the instrument; this is to allow you to more easily compare the adjustments to the instructions.

  1. Adjust the left-to-right parallelism as follows:

    1. Loosen the center setscrew.  If the upper compression disk is high on the left side, turn the right dovetail setscrew clockwise to make the adjustment.  If the disk is high on the right side, turn the left dovetail setscrew clockwise to make the adjustment.

    2. Tighten the center setscrew again and recheck the left-to-right parallelism either visually or by pulling a piece of paper between the compression disks.  If they are properly aligned, the paper should drag evenly when pulled.  Repeat steps 1a and 1b, if needed, or adjust the front-to-rear parallelism (see step 2).

  1.  Adjust the front-to-rear parallelism as follows:

    1. Remove the Allen screws from the upper section of the moveable clamp.

    2. Insert the appropriate number of shims needed to adjust the parallelism correctly.  If the upper clamp is high in the back, place shims in the front, and vice versa. See the figure below.

  1. Adjust the upper disk for concentricity as follows:  Loosen the upper moveable clamp Allen (hex) screws.  Manually move the disks into alignment.  The screw holes in the upper part of the moveable clamp are “oversized” to allow this adjustment.

  2. Tighten all Allen (hex) screws and setscrews and recheck the parallelism and concentricity.  Repeat any of the steps above, if needed.

  3. Make sure that the appropriate clamp type and mode are selected on the controller.

  4. Calibrate the clamp mass, offset, and compliance (see Calibrating the DMA).

  5. Position the thermocouple so that it is close to, but not touching the sample.

Removing the Clamp

To remove the compression clamp from the DMA, follow these steps:

  1. Unscrew and remove the lower compression disk from the fixed clamp.

  2. Press the CLAMP key on the instrument to raise the moveable clamp.

  3. Loosen, but do not remove, the four Allen (hex) screws holding the fixed clamp, then remove the fixed clamp.

  4. Loosen the setscrew holding the moveable clamp on the drive shaft.

  5. Slide the moveable clamp off the dovetail holder.
