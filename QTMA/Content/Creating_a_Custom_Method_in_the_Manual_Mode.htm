Preload Force:  There are two types of force that are used when you evaluate materials with the TMA. Preload force is the force initially applied either to ensure good contact between the measurement probe (e.g., expansion, penetration) and the sample, or to remove slack in the sample (e.g., film/fiber probe), before measuring its length. Although the amount of preload force depends on the material being evaluated, it is recommended that the force used be limited to the smallest amount needed to accurately measure initial sample length. (0.005 N is a good default value for tension type probes and softer materials.) Otherwise, the preload force could affect results (e.g., creep) particularly with softer materials. (This is true even though the experimental applied force is automatically adjusted to account for the preload force.)

If no other force is specified in the test, this force will be applied throughout the experiment.

A value between -1.0 and 1.0 may be entered.

NOTE:  Low force levels may accentuate vibration-induced signal noise.
