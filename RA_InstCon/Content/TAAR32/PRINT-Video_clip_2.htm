Script - Installing a Measuring Geometry

  1. Before working on the rheometer, you should always ensure that the air supply is on and that you can hear the air passing through the bearing.
  2. To remove the air bearing clamp hold the clamp firmly with one hand while turning the knob on the draw rod anti-clockwise.
  3. Lower the clamp from the rheometer and place to one side.
  4. To attach a geometry, hold the draw rod firmly with one hand and push the required geometry onto the spindle.
  5. Turn the knob on the draw rod to screw it into the geometry.
  6. To ensure the geometry is firmly attached, gently apply a lifting pressure to make sure there is no vertical movement.
  7. Having attached the geometry to the rheometer, you must now set it up in the Rheology Advantage™ software.
  8. Open the geometry file that corresponds to the one you have just attached.
  9. You should consider a re-zero of the instrument each time you change the measuring geometry, so answer yes to the question.
  10. For the most accurate data, you should perform an instrument mapping for the installed geometry.
  11. Click on the 'Zero gap' button to start the automatic zeroing procedure.
  12. Use the buttons to move the head down, then click on the 'Continue' button.
