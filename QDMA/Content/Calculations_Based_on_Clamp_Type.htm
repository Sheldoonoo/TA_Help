Calculations Based on Clamp Type

 

Since a material’s modulus is independent of its geometry, equations relating the sample stiffness to the modulus depend on the type of clamps used, the sample shape, and the mode of deformation.  This help topic contains stiffness calculations for the different clamp types along with comments on appropriate correction factors. Also included are stress and strain equations, which can be used as a general guideline for calculations made from the force and amplitude of deformation. The equations for stress and strain assume linear viscoelastic behavior. Modulus calculations based on the equations may not agree with values computed by the DMA program, especially if the sample stretches or shrinks significantly during an experiment.  

The DMA determines the modulus (stiffness) of a material differently depending upon the clamp type installed on the instrument and selected using the instrument control software.  This section provides the equations used for calculations, based on the type of clamp. See also:  Sample Stiffness and Modulus Calculations.

Geometry Factors:

The parameter block contains three normalizing factors related to sample size and geometry.  The factors allow easy conversion between sample properties and material properties.  They appear in this order in the parameter block:

  • Geometry Factor – Multiplying stiffness by the geometry factor converts from stiffness to modulus.

  • Strain Factor – Multiplying amplitude by the strain factor converts from amplitude to strain.

  • Stress Factor – Multiplying force by the stress factor converts from force to stress.
