Experimental Checklist

 

This window in the experimental Wizard provides additional comments on proper experimental setup.

Purge Gas

A purge gas is recommended for TMA experiments.  The recommended purge rate is 100ml/minute.  Helium is recommended as a purge when performing experiments below 0°C.  Because of the significant volume of air trapped around the sample when closing the furnace, let the purge gas flow for 3-5 minutes before adding coolant or starting a run.

Cooling Capability

A source of external cooling is required for TMA experiments below ambient temperature and may be necessary when performing Modulated TMA experiments.  The TMA furnace assembly contains an external reservoir which can be filled with liquid nitrogen or other coolant to attain temperatures as low as -150°C.  A mechanical cooling accessory with a remote heat exchanger which fits into the TMA furnace reservoir can be used to -70°C.  Because of the thermal mass of the TMA furnace, most subambient studies are performed by ballistically cooling to the lowest temperature of interest and then heating at a controlled rate.

Zeroing the Probe

Before loading the sample material, the probe must be "zeroed."  This is accomplished for the standard mode probes (expansion, macroexpansion, penetration, hemispherical) by removing any previous samples or residue from the quartz stage so that the probe tip is in direct contact with the stage. Then press the Zero Length button on this window.

Loading the Sample

For expansion, macroexpansion, penetration, and hemispherical probes:

The sample is loaded by pressing the PROBE UP key on the instrument touch screen twice to allow clearance for loading.  Insert the sample and press the PROBE DOWN key on the instrument touch screen. Once the sample is in place, close the furnace by pressing the FURNACE key located on the TMA instrument touch screen.  Measure sample length by pressing the MEASURE key on the TMA instrument touch screen (the preload force will be applied). The length is automatically measured and the value entered in the Sample Size block of the Sample Information screen.  [Return to the previous screen by clicking the Back button to check this entry.]

For film/fiber probes:

A similar process for loading is followed when using the flexure or film/fiber probe.  However, the process is slightly more complicated. For more information see the following:

  • Preparing Film and Fiber Samples

  • Preparing Flexure Accessory Samples

Positioning the Sample Thermocouple

Regardless of the size or shape of the sample, when using a standard mode probe excluding flexure, position the sample thermocouple so that the tip of the thermocouple is against the stage and angled toward the sample location.  

For flexure probes, the thermocouple is placed so that the tip of the thermocouple is close to, but not touching the edge of the sample where it rests on the "knife edge."

NOTE:  Reproducible thermocouple placement is critical for obtaining good results.

For film/fiber probes, position the tip of the thermocouple at approximately one-half the length of the samples you will be measuring. The tip should be angled toward the sample location.

Measuring the Sample Length

After you have your sample in place and the sample thermocouple properly positioned, press the FURNACE key on the touch screen to rotate the furnace into place above the sample and close it. Click the Measure Length button on this window. This measures your sample’s length automatically;  use this key before  beginning the run.  The measured length is transferred to the instrument control software and the value is recorded in the Sample Size field.

NOTE:  Measure is not applicable with the flexure probe.

Starting the Experiment

Once the sample is mounted, close the furnace by pressing the FURNACE button located on the touch screen. Review the Real-Time Signals on the Signal Display Pane for any problems.  

After you have completed the steps needed in the wizard, you can:

  • Click Append Run to add this run to the end of a previous run.

  • Click the Start Run button to close the wizard and begin this run immediately.

or

  • Select Finish to close the wizard, storing the information you have entered. You may start this experiment later by selecting Control/Start, pressing the START on the tool bar, or pressing the START button located on the instrument touch screen or keypad.
