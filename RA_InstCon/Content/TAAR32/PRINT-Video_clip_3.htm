Script - Loading and Trimming Samples

 

  • Ensuring that the sample is loaded correctly and the gap is properly filled is one of the key aspects to consider in any rheological experiment.
  • If the gap is not correctly filled, certain types of error can occur.
  • If the gap is overfilled, there is the likelihood of some of the excess sample migrating to sit on top of the geometry.
  • If the gap is under filled, you are effectively altering the diameter of the geometry. This will introduce large errors to the measured data.
  • Most people find that the best sample loading method involves overloading the sample plate and then trimming when the geometry is at the correct operating position.
  • The speed of gap closure can also have effect.
  • You can adjust the closure parameters from within the Rheology Advantage™ software.
  • Use the on-line help for more information on this feature.
  • Loading the sample correctly is a skill that is best learned by practicing loading and re-loading a sample.
