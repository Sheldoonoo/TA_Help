Static Testing

In an Isostrain test, a specific known strain is applied to the material.  The temperature of the material is then ramped, and the amount of stress (force) required to maintain that strain is monitored.  Results obtained provide insights into shrinkage stresses and other built-in processing effects in films and fibers.

In a Stress/Strain test, the material is held isothermally and exposed to ramping (linearly increasing) stress and the resultant strain is monitored with time.  The resultant stress/strain curve permits evaluation of material properties such as the linear viscoelastic region (where stress/strain defines the material modulus), yield, and ultimate strength.
