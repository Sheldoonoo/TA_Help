Experimental Checklist

 

Additional comments on proper experimental setup.

Purge Gas:

The Q5000 instruments are operated with two purge gas flows. In both models one purge gas flows through the balance housing. In the Q5000 IR the second purge gas flows through the furnace/sample area. In the Q5000 SA the second purge gas flows through the humidity chamber.

Click here for more information Q5000 IR Purge Gases and Flow Rates or Q5000 SA Purge Gases and Flow Rates

  • The balance purge, which is always an inert gas like nitrogen, is used to protect the balance assembly from back diffusion of gaseous decomposition products from the furnace area.

  • The second purge is the actual purge gas "interacting" with the sample material. This gas can be either inert or reactive and is often switched during the experiment.

Humidity Chamber (Q5000 SA Only):

Check to make sure that you have enough water in the reservoir for your experiments. The status of the reservoir should be displayed here. If you need to fill the reservoir, click here for the instructions Filling the Humidity Chamber.

Sample Loading:

When comparing TGA results between materials, it is important to keep sample surface area, as well as size, consistent.

Taring the Pan:

When you are ready to proceed, make sure that you place a clean, empty sample pan on the tray platform, then click the Tare button on the window. The Tare Utility window allows you to tare the corresponding pan number or the entire tray. Click here for more information Taring the Pan(s).

When the taring process has been completed, load your sample into the pan. Make sure that the pan is properly aligned with the groove in the platform.

Starting the Experiment:

After you have completed the steps needed in the wizard, you can:

  • Press the START RUN button to close the wizard and begin this run immediately.

or

  • Select Finish to close the wizard, storing the information you have entered. You may start this experiment later by selecting Control/Start, pressing the START on the tool bar, or pressing the START button located on the instrument touch screen or keypad.
