Calibrating Inertia

 

In an ideal world, whenever a stress was applied by a rheometer, it would act solely upon the loaded sample and nothing else. In practice, however, the non-zero moments of inertia of the rheometer spindle and measurement geometry mean that some of the applied torque is being used to accelerate or decelerate these mechanical components (until steady state is reached.) A correction needs to be made to the stress value to reflect more accurately the conditions that the sample is undergoing. The amount of correction applied is based upon the calibrated values of the instrument and geometry inertia.

In the oscillation mode, the correction is made automatically. In the flow mode, however, the correction can be toggled on or off as necessary. Flow inertia correction is more likely to be needed when low viscosity materials are being measured using fast ramps over a wide shear range.

Calibrating Instrument Inertia

Under ideal circumstances, the inertia value of the rheometer should not change with time. Running a calibration check on this value therefore gives a good indication of the performance of the rheometer.

If you notice a continual drift of this value, check the quality of the air used as it could be indicative of a poor quality supply.

Follow the steps below to calibrate your rheometer's inertia:

  1. Select Options from the menu.
  2. Click on Instrument...
  3. Click on the Inertia tab. (NOTE:  You can type in the value manually to restore to a known calibration state, if desired.)
  4. Ensure that there is no measuring system attached to the rheometer.
  5. Click on the Calibrate button.
  6. Follow the steps in the calibration wizard.

Calibrating the Measuring System Inertia

Follow the steps below to calibrate the measuring system inertia of the instrument:

  1. Select Geometry from the menu.
  2. Click on View.
  3. Click on the Settings tab.
  4. Ensure that the correct measuring system is attached to the rheometer.
  5. Click on the Calibrate button (Geometry inertia).
  6. Follow the steps given by the wizard. If you are using solid samples, calibrate the Compliance.

NOTE: You can also type the value in manually if required (to restore to a known calibration state for example) at stage 3.
