Choosing a Pan

 

The Q5000 instruments have the following pans available.  Choose a pan based on the desired  temperature range and application. The pan size and material are specified through the instrument control program on the Summary Page.  Proper selection is important to ensure optimum performance is achieved and pan punching is initiated (only when a sealed pan is selected).

General Guidelines

 The criteria for choosing a sample pan are as follows:

Q5000 IR Pans

  • For most TGA experiments, platinum is the desirable choice. It is easy to clean and does not react with most organics and polymers. However, for applications above 700°C, platinum-HT pans are recommended. (When using a platinum-HT pan on the tare side, you can use either platinum or platinum-HT sample pans on the Autosampler tray.)

  • Alumina (ceramic) pans (shown in the figure to the right) are more porous and therefore more easily contaminated. Use alumina pans for samples that might alloy or react with platinum (e.g., metals, corrosives, inorganics).

  • Aluminum pans (seen below) are meant for one-time use in experiments that do not go above 600°C and for samples that do not react with aluminum (e.g., corrosives, acidic materials, or anything that would alloy with aluminum). The aluminum pans sit inside a bail, which provides the handle that is used when the instrument picks up the pan. Aluminum pans may be used open or closed with a lid.

Sealed aluminum pans are punched before the experiment (as seen in the figure to the right). This allows any gas that has accumulated to escape and provides exposure to the atmosphere. See Preparing Sealed TGA Pans for information on how to enclose your sample in an aluminum pan sealed with a lid.

  • If your sample will melt during the experiment, use a pan that is deep enough to prevent spilling (the deepest pan is the 250 µL alumina pan).

  • Platinum and ceramic pans are reusable. To clean pans between experiments, use a Bunsen burner or a propane torch, or run the pan through a hot thermal program in the TGA to burn out any residue. Aluminum pans are disposable, do not attempt to clean and reuse them. DO NOT use an acid wash to clean the Platinum HT pans. For information on cleaning the various pans, click here: Cleaning TGA Sample Pans.

  • There are two available sample trays for the Q5000. Verify that the proper sample tray is selected by accessing the Instrument Preferences/TGA Page from the Tools menu.

NOTE:  Some materials readily build up static electricity changes that can create problems during loading. The Q5000 IR is designed with a unique hang-down wire assembly that provides a direct path to ground when metal pans are used. Hence, no static effects will be seen with those pans. When using ceramic, uncoated quartz, or platinum-HT pans, however, it may be necessary to periodically discharge static build-up using an "anti-static pistol." Cleaning the furnace tube will also help discharge static buildup.

Once you have selected the proper sample pan, put the same type and size pan on the tare hook and tare the pan. See also: Taring the Pan(s).

Q5000 SA Pans

  • Hemispherical metal-coated quartz pans are primarily used for Q5000 SA sorption analysis. You must install the ten-sample Autosampler platform when using the quartz pans.

  • Some materials readily build up static electricity charges that can create problems during loading. This phenomenon is most likely to occur in experiments where the ambient humidity is low. Metal-coated (metalized) quartz pans eliminate this problem by completing a grounding path to the balance housing.

  • There are two available sample trays for the Q5000. Verify that the proper sample tray is selected by accessing the Instrument Preferences/TGA Page from the Tools menu.

Once you have selected the proper sample pan, put the same type and size pan on the tare hook and tare the pan. See also: Taring the Pan(s).

For information on cleaning the various pans, click here: Cleaning TGA Sample Pans.
