Cross

To obtain a model for a general flow curve, over a wide range of shears, requires a model with at least four parameters. The Cross model is a good example of this type of equation.

It predicts behavior consisting of low and high shear plateau’s, with a shear thinning region in-between. The Cross model uses shear rate as the independent variable. The Ellis model can be used as an equivalent using shear stress.

An alternative model is the Carreau model.
