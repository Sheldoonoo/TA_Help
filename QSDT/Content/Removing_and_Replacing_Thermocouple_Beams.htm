Removing and Replacing Thermocouple Beams

 

CAUTION:  Extreme care must be taken when changing the thermocouple beams, the wires are very small (.002 in.) and fragile.  They can be easily broken.  

The following directions tell you how to remove existing SDT thermocouple beams and then replace them with new beams.  The procedures can be performed under the following conditions:

  • The furnace must be fully open.

  • The SDT power switch must be in the off position.

  • Both thermocouple beams must be replaced, even if only one is damaged, as they are made as a matched set.  If you replace only one beam, the instrument's specifications will be voided.

Thermocouple  Beam  Installation

Each thermocouple beam is specifically made for either the sample side or reference side.  You can identify this by the way the mounting tab is secured to the beam.  The beam will only mount in its correct position.  The sample thermocouple beam is mounted towards the front of the module and the reference thermocouple beam is mounted to the rear of the SDT.

The following beam installation procedures assume that you have already removed the previous set of beams.  Please remember that both thermocouple beams must be replaced at the same time, as they are a matched set.

  1. Hold the thermocouple beam in your right hand with the wires pointing to the right and the sample/reference platform to the left.

  2. Insert the circular-shaped sample/reference platform through from the right side of the housing as shown in the figure below. Make sure that the wires follow and do not get caught in the thermocouple spring plate.

  1. Secure the thermocouple beam with your left hand and carefully pull the remaining beam through the opening until the bracket can be slid under the beam-mounting bracket (#0-80) screw.

  2. Align the thermocouple beam so that it will be parallel with the other beam and tighten the beam-mounting bracket (#0-80) screw.

  3. When viewed from above, make sure both beams are parallel to each other and parallel to the center line of the furnace.  If the beams need adjusting, turn on the power switch, loosen the (#0-80) screw slightly, and carefully shift the beams in the appropriate direction, then re-tighten the (#0-80) screw.  

  4. When viewed from the front of the instrument, both beams should be horizontal and parallel to the top of the hand rest.  If the beams need adjusting to make them parallel to the top of the hand rest, turn on the power switch, then loosen the position sensor board mounting screws and turn the adjusting screws using a 5/64 Allen wrench (see the figure below). When the beams are horizontal and parallel, tighten down the position sensor board mounting screws. With large ceramic cups on the platform there should be adequate clearance all around the mouth of the metal bellows.

  1. Place the copper thermocouple block in position and using a 5/64 Allen wrench, secure the two (2) screws.  This will clamp the thermocouple wires tightly. Do not overtighten the Allen screws.

  2. Form each wire alternately in either a convex or concave fashion beginning with the front wire. This can be done using the tweezers or Allen wrench to gently lift or depress each wire, taking care not to place excessive pressure on the wires.

  3. Replace the three (3) blue twisted Delta-T wires in their positions through the copper thermocouple block.  Make sure they are fully seated.  (See the figure above.)

  4. Verify that the wires are hooked up properly by performing the following simple test:

    1. Access the Q600 Signal Display touch screen. Locate the Delta T and temperature signals.

    2. Using your thumb and forefinger, gently squeeze the sample beam platform.  Both the sample temperature and the Delta T/temperature difference should increase slightly. Remove your fingers.

    3. Using your thumb and forefinger again, now gently squeeze the reference beam platform. The Delta T/temperature difference only should decrease slightly. Remove your fingers.

If steps b and c did not result in the desired effect, check the orientation of your wires by repeating steps 8 through 11.
