Message 861

Submersion clamps for use with aqueous or buffer solutions below 80°C only.

Problem:

You cannot use the submersion compression or submersion film/fiber clamps with aqueous solutions above 80°C. The clamps are primarily designed to evaluate polymeric materials in aqueous or buffer solutions over the temperature range -50°C (but above freezing point) to 80°C. Because of the volume of fluid (and its associated heat capacity), these submersion measurements are usually performed under isothermal conditions ramping at 2°C/min or less. The clamps are designed for aqueous or buffer solutions and should not be used with fluids whose vapor pressure results in volatility at the temperature of usage.

Solution:

Do not use temperatures below ambient or above 80°C in your experiments when either submersion clamp is installed. See the guidelines above.
