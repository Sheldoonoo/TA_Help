Transporting the Q5000 IR

 

Important:  If you need to ship this instrument, you will need to contact TA Instruments' Service to properly package the instrument for shipment. Improper packaging will result in damage to the instrument.

If, however, you need to transport the TGA Q5000 IR instrument yourself by vehicle, follow these instructions to pack up the instrument safely. It is very important to properly protect this precision instrument to guard against potential problems when the unit is unpacked and used again. These instructions are not needed when moving the instrument on a cart within the same building.

WARNING:  Have an assistant help you pack this unit.  Do not attempt to do this alone.

CAUTION:  Follow the directions on the packing instructions carefully when installing the shipping material.

Removing the Water from the System

Before the TGA Q5000 can be shipped, the water should be removed from the instrument as follows:

  1. Press the metal release clip and pull out the connector to disconnect the "RETURN" line from the heat exchanger.

  2. Unscrew the nut to release the black fitting on the end of the hose and pull the fitting off.

  3. Place the "RETURN" line in a bucket.

  4. Press down on the release clip and pull the connector to disconnect the "SUPPLY" line from the heat exchanger.

  5. Unscrew the nut to release the black fitting on the end of the hose and pull the fitting off.

  1. Introduce air into the "SUPPLY" line to force all of the water through the lines and into the bucket. You can disconnect the Air Cool line from the instrument and use it to blow air through the system.

  2. When water no longer empties into the bucket, the water should be removed from the system.

Removing the Lower Furnace Assembly

The lower furnace assembly must be removed before the sample side hang-down wire can be removed. Follow the steps below to remove the sample lower furnace assembly:

  1. Close the furnace completely.

  2. Remove the lower furnace assembly by unscrewing the thumbscrew as shown in the figure to the right.

  3. Remove the lower furnace assembly from the furnace by pulling it straight down until the entire assembly is clear of the instrument.  DO NOT TWIST THE LOWER FURNACE.

  4. Unscrew the knurled nut at the other end of the lower furnace assembly (shown in the figure to the right) to release it from the instrument cabinet.

  5. Disconnect the tube from the air cool inlet.

  6. Place the lower furnace assembly in a well-padded box, preferably its original shipping box, for transporting.

Removing the Hang-Down Wires and Pans

During removal, take care not to bend the hang-down wires or damage the hang-down loops.

  1. Press the UNLOAD button, and remove any sample pans from the balance.

  2. Remove the balance housing cover by removing the two screws in the back with a screwdriver. (See the figure to the right.) Retain the screws. Lift the cover off.

  3. Remove the six screws on the inner balance faceplate and remove the plate.

  4. Turn the instrument power switch to ON, if it is not already powered up.

CAUTION:  During this procedure, take care not to bend the hang-down wires or damage the hang-down loops.

Installing the Q5000 IR Shipping Bracket

The shipping bracket is used to stabilize the furnace during transport. It was removed when the instrument was unpacked and should be reinstalled as follows:

  1. Close the furnace completely using the furnace up button .

  2. Use a 1/2-inch wrench to loosen the nut on the furnace shipping bracket and raise the foot.

  3. Place the bracket under the furnace as shown in the figure to the right.

  4. Lower the foot until it rests on the platform surface as shown.

  5. Tighten the nut to hold the bracket in place.

  6. Turn off the instrument's power switch, which is located on the back of the instrument and remove the power cord.

  7. Disconnect all other cables and lines attached to the back of the instrument. Place the cables and lines in a box to be shipped with the instrument.

Replacing the Shipping Pallet

  1. If you have retained the shipping pallet, follow the next steps to mount the instrument. Have an assistant hold the front up while you remove the two mounting feet. Then move to the back and remove the stabilizer bar.

  2. Place the pallet on the floor. Using two people, lift the instrument onto the pallet.

  3. Use a 7/16-inch socket wrench to install the five hold-down bolts and washers on the bottom of the shipping pallet.

  4. If desired, you can wrap the instrument in a large plastic bag. Place the original box over the instrument, if it is available. The instrument is now ready for transport.
