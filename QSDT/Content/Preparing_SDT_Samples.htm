Preparing SDT Samples

 

The SDT can be used to analyze virtually any material that can be put into a SDT sample cup and that will not react with the cup material. This includes the following sample types:

  • Films

  • Fibers

  • Powders

  • Solutions

  • Composites

When making quantitative measurements or verifying reproducibility, it is important to ensure good thermal contact between the sample and sample cup.  The physical characteristics of the sample affect the quality of this contact.

When using powdered or granular samples, spread them evenly across the bottom of the cup to minimize thermal gradients. For solid samples, select the side of your sample with the flattest surface for contact with the cup.  

NOTE:  The oils on your fingers can affect the results of thermal analysis experiments. Always use tweezers when handling sample pans and sample material.

If you overfill a cup, the heating or cooling procedures may cause the sample to boil out, buckle, or explode the cup. Therefore, you should be reasonably familiar with the effects of heating and cooling procedures on your samples and cups.
