Experimental Checklist

 

Additional comments on proper experimental setup.

Air Bearing Gas

A purge gas is always used in the DMA.  Unlike the other thermal analysis modules, however, the purge in DMA is essentially a purge "by default."  That's because the purge is the gas used to "float" the air bearing slide.  Therefore, air at about 2 liters/minute (either from an in-house air line, a gas cylinder, or Air Compressor Accessory) is the most common purge gas.  Other "inert" gases like nitrogen can also be used.  

NOTE:  Dryness of the purge gas is important.  Moisture condensation in the air bearing will adversely affect performance.

Cooling Capability

Many materials evaluated by the DMA have events of interest below ambient temperature (nom. 25°C).  Hence, a source of external coolant is required.  The gas cooling accessory (GCA) is recommended.  Because of the thermal mass of the DMA furnace and clamps, most subambient studies are performed by ballistically cooling to the lowest temperature of interest ensuring that equilibration has occurred and then heating at a controlled rate (usually 2-5°C/minute).

External air cooling can be connected to cool the furnace between experiments at high temperatures (only is the GCA is not being used). See also:  Starting Subambient Experiments

Choosing Sample Size/Loading the Sample

The general procedure to be followed when choosing the best sample conditions [clamp and geometry (sample dimensions)] is illustrated below for the single/dual cantilever clamps.  For complete details on all the clamps, see the help topic for that clamp. See also:  Available DMA Clamps

The single/dual cantilever clamps can be used for relatively weak to moderately stiff materials.  The samples can range from supported thermosetting resins, to elastomers, amorphous, or lightly-filled thermoplastic materials.  Because samples are rigidly clamped and do not need to provide a "restoring" force to the instrument, highly damped materials can be measured in the cantilever clamps.  Dual cantilever clamps are better used for weak elastomers and supported resins.  The single cantilever clamps must be used for measuring the properties of amorphous polymers and elastomers through the glass transition, and for analyzing materials with high thermal expansion.

The single/dual cantilever clamps will accommodate samples with lengths (4, 10 or 17.5mm / 8, 20, or 35mm), widths (up to 15mm), and thicknesses (up to 5mm). The modulus range which can be covered varies based on the actual sample geometry. Therefore, an appropriate sample size can be chosen by calculating the "geometry factor" based on sample dimensions and using a figure such as the one below to verify that the modulus range available is acceptable.  In this case, the figure shows that thinner samples are necessary when evaluating higher modulus materials, and vice versa.

Starting the Experiment

Once the sample is mounted, close the furnace by pressing the FURNACE button located on the touch screen. Press the MEASURE key on the DMA if you are using the 3-point bending, compression, tension fiber, or tension film clamps.  The DMA will apply the preload force specified in your experiment, measure the sample length (tension film and tension fiber) or thickness (3-point bending, compression), enter that dimension into the experimental parameters, and start the motor in the desired mode.  Review the Real-Time Signals on the Signal Display Pane for any problems.  

After you have completed the steps needed in the wizard, you can:

  • Press the START RUN button to close the wizard and begin this run immediately.

or

  • Select Finish to close the wizard, storing the information you have entered. You may start this experiment later by selecting Control/Start, pressing the START on the tool bar, or pressing the START button located on the instrument touch screen or keypad.
