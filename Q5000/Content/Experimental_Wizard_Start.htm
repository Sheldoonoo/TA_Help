Q5000 IR Experimental Wizard

Thermogravimetric Analysis (TGA) is a thermal analysis technique designed to measure the amount or rate of weight change in a material as a function of temperature or time. The weight changes measured are generally weight losses associated with phenomena such as decomposition or volatilization and the results provide information about thermal stability and compositional analysis. However, weight gains associated with the adsorption of moisture or a gas (e.g., oxidation) can also be measured.

The following calibration procedures should be conducted before using this wizard:

  • Weight Calibration

  • Temperature Calibration (Q5000 IR only)

Most TGA Q5000 IR experiments are performed while heating the material at a constant rate from ambient to a temperature above the point of total decomposition. Additional information about the material can sometimes be obtained by performing more sophisticated High Resolution TGA™ (Hi-Res™ TGA) or Modulated TGA™ (MTGA™) experiments. However, when evaluating a material for the first time, a Conventional TGA experiment is recommended.

  • Conventional TGA Experiments

  • Modulated TGA Experiments

Click the radio button for the type of experiment desired, then select Next to proceed or select Back to return to the previous window.

Q5000 SA Experimental Wizard

The Sorption Analyzer, the TA Instruments Q5000 SA, is used to determine the amount of  moisture a material can adsorb or desorb as a function of its mass, temperature, time, and relative humidity.  This instrument is primarily concerned with applications where changing levels of humidity can influence or significantly alter important properties or uses of a material (e.g., physical properties (Tg), stability, shelf life, bioavailability).

When you select Experimental/Wizard from the menu, the opening window will display the following options:

For more information regarding these tests, choose from the following Help topics:

  • Salt Deliquescence Experiment

  • Adsorption/Desorption Procedure

  • Humidity Ramp Procedure

  • Isohume Procedure

  • Sorption Isotherm

Click the radio button for the type of experiment desired, then select Next to proceed or select Back to return to the previous window.
