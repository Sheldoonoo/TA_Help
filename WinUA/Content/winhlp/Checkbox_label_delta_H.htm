Delta H

Check this box to display the Delta H value in kJ/mol. This is the change in the heat of fusion.

 

Heat of Fusion is defined as the amount of heat required to melt one gram of solid at its melting point with no change in temperature. Usually expressed in J/g. The molar heat of fusion is the amount of heat required to melt one mole of a solid at its melting point with no change in temperature and is usually expressed in kJ/mol.

 

Depending on whether this value is determined by the data, determined and adjusted according to the area correction factor, or a known value is used, this value is labeled with (uncorrected), (corrected), or (entered). Use the Purity Analysis Options - Purity Calculation Page to enter a known value.
