Preparing Solid Samples

The TGA has high sensitivity to weight changes. Therefore, there is no advantage to using large samples. 10 to 50 milligram samples are recommended. However, when evaluating solids or powders, make sure that the sample is prepared and introduced into the pan so that it evenly covers the bottom of the pan. This is important because surface area (and the associated interaction with the purge gas) can affect results.

Preparing Liquid Samples

Liquid samples can be evaluated in an open pan or a sealed aluminum pan. The sealed pan is recommended. Care must be taken when introducing the sample into the pan to avoid "wicking" of liquid onto the sealing surface (lip) of the pan.

Preparing Sorption Analysis Samples

The adsorption and desorption of moisture by materials can be significantly affected by factors such as surface area, sample weight, and flow rate of the humidified gas. Hence, it is advantageous to use powdered samples and to spread the sample so that it evenly covers the bottom of the pan.

Sample Size

The Q5000 SA balance is very sensitive to small weight changes. Therefore, it is not necessary to use large samples, particularly if the sample gains more than10% of its original weight during exposure to humidity. A sample size of 5 – 10 mg is sufficient. A slightly larger sample size (25 – 30 mg) is recommended if the total weight gain is less than 0.5%. Generally smaller samples will allow more rapid equilibration with the humidity being used. Smaller samples are also recommended when performing ramped humidity or ramped temperature experiments.
