Peltier System

 

The Peltier system is an integral part of the TA Instruments rheometers that is important for accurate temperature control.

Understanding the Peltier System

Temperature control in the standard configuration is via a plate that uses the Peltier effect to rapidly and accurately control heating and cooling. The Peltier system uses a thermoelectric effect. This functions as a heat pump system with no moving parts, and is ideally suited to rheological measurements. By controlling the magnitude and direction of electric current, the Peltier system can provide any desired level of active heating or cooling directly in the plate.

  • Standard Peltier System range: 10°C to 99°C
  • Extended Peltier System range: -10°C to 180°C (extended use above 150°C is not recommended))

This provides very fast temperature control, however, it is unable to control the temperature of very large sample masses such as large concentric cylinder systems. A schematic of the Peltier plate is shown in below.

 



Since the Peltier system operates as a heat pump, it is necessary to have a heat sink available. The heat sink removes unwanted waste heat from the plate. This heat sink is normally in the form of a reservoir, containing a few liters of water, plus a small pump which can provide a sufficient flow rate through the Peltier heat exchanger jacket built into the plate.

If you are working in the temperature range of 20°C below and 60°C above ambient, the water bath need only be at room temperature. For optimum performance at lower or higher temperatures, the water bath temperature needs to be altered accordingly. (i.e., the Peltier plate will work most efficiently at a temperature range 15°C above and below the water bath temperature.)
The flow rate through the Peltier plate does not need to be high. A flow rate of at least 0.5 liter min-1 is adequate. If this flow rate is not maintained, the temperature control system will lose control and the plate will heat or cool continuously.

 

CAUTION:  The Peltier Plate may be damaged by operating the instrument without a flow of water through the Peltier system. (A Peltier Overheat protection device will activate if no flow is detected. A ‘Peltier Overheat’ message will be displayed on the computer monitor and the LCD of the electronics control box.

Configuring the Peltier System

The Peltier system is the standard method of temperature control for the rheometer. The following procedure should be used to ensure that the software is configured to use the Peltier system.

  1. Select Options from the menu.
  2. Click on Instrument.
  3. Click on the Temperature tab.
  4. Ensure that the items Temperature control and Temperature read are set to show Peltier.
  5. Press the OK button.
