Taring the Pan(s)

 

The Tare function ensures that the weight measured by the balance reflects the weight of the sample only.

When a pan is tared, the instrument reads the weight of the empty sample pan and then stores the weight as an offset, which is subtracted from subsequent weight measurements. For optimum accuracy, the weight reading must be stable before it is accepted as an offset. The instrument determines when the weight reading is sufficiently stable.

You should tare the sample pan before each experiment, even if you use the same pan in consecutive experiments. In addition, please verify that the same type of pan is used on the tare side and the sample side of the balance. When using a platinum-HT pan on the tare side, you can use either platinum or platinum-HT sample pans on the Autosampler tray.

Taring may be performed from the instrument control program or from the instrument touch screen.

Setting Up Pans for Taring

To properly tare your sample pans, follow the instructions below depending upon the sample tray and type of pans in use.

25-Position Tray

  • Platinum pans:  Align wire on the bottom of the sample pan with the groove in the pan recess.

  • Ceramic pans:  Align bail with groove in the pan recess.

  • Aluminum:  Assemble bail/pan/lids (but do not seal until after sample is added) and align the notch in the bail with the notch in the pan recess.

10-Position Tray

  • Quartz pans:  Align pan bail with etching on the sample tray.

Using the Instrument Control Program to Tare Pans

The Tare Utility window is displayed when Tare is selected from the instrument control software (see details below). Use this window to tare a specific pan number or choose from multiple pan numbers.

  1. Place the empty sample pan(s) on the Autosampler tray.  (If you are using sealed aluminum pans, then you must tare the lid along with the empty pan.)

  2. Select Calibrate/Tare or click on the button. (You could also select TARE TRAY from the touch screen. This option tares all the pans installed on the tray.)

  3. Select the desired pan number from the list.

  4. Click on the desired pan number to select one pan or hold down the Ctrl key and click to select multiple pan numbers from the list. You can also click the Select All button to choose all pan numbers for taring or click Deselect All to clear your selections.

  5. Click Tare when finished selecting the tray number and pan(s).

  6. Click Close when completed all taring has been completed.

Taring is recorded in the Instrument Log. View this log, if desired. The instrument will automatically load and close the furnace/humidity chamber when Tare is selected.

Using the Touch Screen to Tare Pans

For detailed information on the touch screen, click here Introducing the Touch Screen. Follow the directions below to tare your sample pans:

  1. Place the empty sample pan(s) on the Autosampler tray.  (If you are using sealed aluminum pans, then you must tare the lid along with the empty pan.)

  2. Select the Control key on the touch screen.

  3. Press the icon or select "Tare" from the Control Command list.

  4. Select the desired pan number(s).

NOTE: You can tare all of the pans loaded on your sample tray by selecting "Tare All" from the Control Command list.

  1. Press Apply to start the tare process.

Taring is recorded in the Instrument Log. View this log, if desired. The instrument will automatically load and close the furnace/humidity chamber when Tare is selected.
