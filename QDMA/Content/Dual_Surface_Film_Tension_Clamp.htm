Dual Surface Film Tension Clamp

 

The dual surface film tension clamp can be used for film samples that are up to 2 mm thick. This clamp can be used for thin films of all types and for both single and bundled fibers. (0.02 to 250 mm in diameter)

Clamp Faces

The clamp consists of an upper fixed clamp and lower movable clamp. Both the top and bottom clamp have rotating dual surfaces clamp faces as seen in the figures below.

 

  • The smooth (or flat) surface is intended for clamping of low modulus or soft samples.

  • The second clamp face has two thin ridged surfaces at the top and bottom. This ridged clamp face is intended for clamping stiffer, high modulus materials.

Spring Washer and Screw Assembly

Each clamp face is fixed against the sample using a hex head screw and spring washer assembly.  Once tightened, the curved washers push against the clamp face and the fixing screw. This ensures the clamp face maintains in contact with the surface of the sample being tested should any shrinkage occur during heating or cooling.

Please make sure to note the correct orientation of the spring washer assembly and be sure to maintain the proper arrangement, with the curved surfaces oriented as seen in the figure below. The spring washer assembly is shown to the right.

NOTE:  Be careful not to over torque the sample, flattening the washers in the assembly.

Mounting Samples

After the film tension clamp has been installed on the DMA, you can mount the sample using the following steps:

The clamping of film samples is critical to achieving accurate and reproducible results.

  1. Press the DRIVE Float/Lock key on the instrument to release (float) the clamp.

  2. Move the moveable clamp to the approximate position of the desired specimen length.  The sample length can be directly observed as a signal output on the instrument display.

  3. Press the DRIVE Float/Lock key to lock the clamp in position.  (The actual specimen length can be measured by the DMA by using the MEASURE key after the specimen is mounted.)

  4. Loosen the clamping screws on the moveable and fixed clamps (see the figure to the right for the location of the clamping screws).

  5. Select the side of the clamp face desired and turn it toward the sample.

  6. Slide the sample in from the side of the fixed clamp, then lower it down into the moveable clamp.

  7. Push the moveable clamp shut using your finger, then adjust the sample so that it is centered and aligned vertically on the clamp.  

  8. Please make sure to note the correct orientation of the spring washer assembly and be sure to maintain the proper arrangement. Make sure that there is no sample underneath the clamping screw. Finger tighten the clamping screw.

  9. Using the torque wrench, tighten the bottom clamp screw on the moveable clamp to the appropriate clamping torque [2 to 3 in-lbs (20 to 40 cm-N) for rigid materials]. Use of excessive torque will bend the stage, causing permanent damage.  Use the torque wrench as directed and do not exceed specifications.

  10. Carefully align the film so that it is vertical and evenly tensioned across the sample width. Tighten the clamping screw on the fixed clamp.  Trim any excess sample.

  11. Position the thermocouple so that it is approximately halfway between the two clamps and close to, but not touching, the sample.

  12. Place the thermal shield over the clamps, if you are using a heating rate of 2°C/min or higher.  NOTE:  Do not use the thermal shield when using heating rates less than 2°C/min or when performing a step and hold experiment.

Running an Experiment

When you use the film tension clamp for experiments, you will need to set up the experimental parameters using the instrument control software.

Follow these general guidelines when using the film clamp:

  1. Set up the experimental parameters.  Note that these clamps are tensioning clamps; therefore, force track and preload force values must be selected. The recommended values are 0.005 to 1 N for preload force and 115 to 200 percent for force track, if appropriate.

  2. Press the MEASURE key on the DMA.  This will apply the preload force, measure the sample length, enter the length into the experimental parameters, and then start the motor in the desired operation mode.

  3. View the signals. Pay special attention to these signals:

    • Amplitude:  This signal should achieve and maintain the value programmed.  If running a multistrain experiment, the amplitude will cycle through the values programmed.

    • Stiffness:  The stiffness should be within the instrument’s measurable range of 100 N/m to 10,000,000 N/m.  

    • Drive Force:  The drive force should be between 0.0001 and 18 N.  If the drive force is not within this range, either increase the programmed amplitude to increase the drive force, or increase the stiffness of the sample by changing the physical dimensions of the sample.  Since the length is fixed in the film tension clamp, the width and thickness will need to be increased in order to increase the sample stiffness.

    • Preload Force:  If force track is used, the preload force will automatically be adjusted to remain a set percentage greater than the force required to drive the sample at the programmed amplitude.  If force track is not used, the preload force should read the value set in the instrument parameters.  For more information regarding preload force, see Understanding the DMA Forces.

If the initial conditions are acceptable, (i.e., smooth oscillation and good modulus values), start the experiment.  

If the values are not acceptable, then change conditions appropriately.

Removing Samples

After you have finished your experiment, you can remove the sample from the film tension clamp as follows:

  1. Loosen the two clamping screws and open the clamp faces.

  2. Remove the sample.

  3. Use a knife or razor blade to gently scrape all clamp faces to remove any residue.  Be careful not to scratch the clamp faces.
