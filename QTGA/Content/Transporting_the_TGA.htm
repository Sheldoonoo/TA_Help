Transporting the TGA

 

Important:  If you need to ship this instrument, you will need to contact TA Instruments' Service to properly package the instrument for shipment. Improper packaging will result in damage to the instrument.

If, however, you need to transport the TGA Q series instrument yourself by vehicle, follow these instructions to pack up the instrument safely. It is very important to properly protect this precision instrument to guard against potential problems when the unit is unpacked and used again. These instructions are not needed when moving the instrument on a cart within the same building.

WARNING:  Have an assistant help you pack this unit.  Do not attempt to do this alone.

CAUTION:  Follow the directions on the packing instructions carefully when installing the shipping material.

There are several basic steps to be performed when packing up the TGA for shipment/transporting:

  • Removing the Water from the System

  • Installing the Shipping Foam in the Balance

  • Installing the Shipping Bracket

  • Replacing the Shipping Pallet

Removing the Water from the System

Before the TGA can be shipped, the water should be removed from the instrument as follows:

  1. Press the metal release clip and pull out the connector to disconnect the "RETURN" line from the heat exchanger.

  2. Unscrew the nut to release the black fitting on the end of the hose and pull the fitting off.

  3. Place the "RETURN" line in a bucket.

  4. Press down on the release clip and pull the connector to disconnect the "SUPPLY" line from the heat exchanger.

  5. Unscrew the nut to release the black fitting on the end of the hose and pull the fitting off.

  1. Introduce air into the "SUPPLY" line to force all of the water through the lines and into the bucket. You can disconnect the Air Cool line from the instrument and use it to blow air through the system.

  2. When water no longer empties into the bucket, the water should be removed from the system.

Installing the Shipping Foam in the Balance

CAUTION:  When repacking the balance, be careful not to damage the balance arm or hang-down loops.

  1. Using the 7/64-inch ball driver supplied in your TGA accessory kit, loosen and remove the six screws securing the balance chamber faceplate to the instrument.

  2. Take off the faceplate.

  3. Loosen and remove the thumbscrew holding the balance cover on the sample (left) side of the balance mechanism (shown here), and take off the cover.

  4. Using tweezers, insert the foam around the screw hole (see figure below) as follows:

  1. Gently compress the foam with the tweezers, being careful not to touch the balance.

  2. Place the foam insert in the balance chamber as shown in the figure below.

  1. Replace the sample side cover and screw.

  2. Repeat the procedure to install the foam insert in the tare (right) side of the balance and replace the tare side cover and screw.

Installing the Shipping Bracket

The shipping bracket is used to stabilize the furnace during transport. It was removed when the instrument was unpacked and should be reinstalled as follows:

  1. Use a 1/2-inch wrench to loosen the nut on the furnace shipping bracket and raise the foot.

  2. Place the bracket under the furnace as shown in the figure to the right.

  3. Lower the foot until it rests on the platform surface as shown.

  4. Tighten the nut to hold the bracket in place.

  5. Turn off the instrument's power switch, which is located on the back of the instrument and remove the power cord.

  6. Disconnect all other cables and lines attached to the back of the instrument. Place the cables and lines in a box to be shipped with the instrument.

Replacing the Shipping Pallet

  1. If you have retained the shipping pallet, follow the next steps to mount the instrument. Have an assistant hold one side up while you remove the two mounting feet on one side. Then move to the other side and remove the mounting feet.

  2. Place the pallet on the floor. Using two people, lift the instrument onto the pallet.

  3. Use a 7/16-inch socket wrench to install the five hold-down bolts and washers on the bottom of the shipping pallet.

  4. If desired, you can wrap the instrument in a large plastic bag as shown below. Place the original box over the instrument, if it is available.  The instrument is now ready for transport.
