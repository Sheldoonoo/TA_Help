Selecting a TMA Operating Mode

 

There are several TMA operating modes available for the Q Seriesâ„¢ instruments. The Q400 Thermomechanical analyzer (TMA) measures dimensional changes in material as a function of temperature or time in a controlled atmosphere.  The Enhanced TMA option adds additional capabilities to simultaneously characterize material viscoelastic properties.

 

Mode

Description

Standard

Standard mode provides data on dimension change with experimental control over force and temperature.   It can also be used to detect transitions in materials (e.g., glass transition temperature, softening and flow information, delamination temperature, melting phenomena etc).

Calibration

Calibration mode provides uncorrected temperature, delta-length, and force data.  This mode is used only to calibrate temperature and cell constant and provides no material characterization capabilities.

Advanced

(Applies to Advanced TMA EM Option Only)

Advanced mode provides all the capabilities of Standard mode and adds the ability to input sample geometry and thereby collect normalized stress and strain data (via isostrain and strain ramps).

In addition, the capability of experimentally controlling strain is provided.  Strain control can be either Isostrain where strain is held constant while temperature is varied or Strain Ramp where strain is linearly ramped while temperature is held constant.

Creep

(Applies to Advanced TMA EM Option Only)

Creep mode provides compliance and recovery data with controlled stress application and recovery. In a creep test, a stress level is instantaneously applied to the sample and held constant for a period of time. At the end of that period, the stress is removed instantaneously. The resulting changes in strain are measured as a function of time. The material properties obtained from this experiment are creep compliance, recoverable compliance, and strain recovery.

Stress Relaxation

(Applies to Advanced TMA EM Option Only)

The Stress Relaxation mode adds recovery, stiffness, and relaxation modulus data with controlled displacement and recovery. In a stress relaxation test, a strain is instantaneously applied to the sample, and the stress required to maintain that strain is measured as a function of time. The stress relaxation modulus is calculated as the time-dependent stress divided by the constant strain. The sample recovery can also be monitored versus time upon release of the strain.

Modulated TMA

(Applies to Advanced TMA EM Option Only)

The Modulated mode separates dimension change into reversible and non-reversible components by the application of a sinusoidal temperature profile. In a modulated temperature test the expansion/contraction of the material is measured while the temperature profile varied sinusoidally versus time.  When the underlying temperature profile is a temperature ramp this permits the separation of the thermal expansion into reversing and non-reversing components.

Dynamic TMA

(Applies to Advanced TMA EM Option Only)

The Dynamic TMA mode provides Storage Modulus, Loss Modulus, and Tan Delta data by the application of a sinusoidal force profile. In a modulated force test the sample is subjected to a sinusoidal stress profile, while the temperature is either ramped linearly or held constant in time.  The sample dimension change is resolved into in-phase stiffness and out-of-phase damping components.  The stiffness and damping are normalized based on sample geometry to produce Storage Modulus and Loss Modulus, respectively.  The ratio of Storage to Loss, called Tan Delta, provides a measure of the material energy dissipation properties.

Manual Mode

(Applies to Advanced TMA EM Option Only)

Provides a super set of the capabilities of all the individual modes to enable complex multimode experiments.

Once you have selected a mode, you will need to enter your choice using the instrument control program.  

  • If you are using the Experimental Wizard, select the radio button for the desired mode, then click the Next button.

  • If you are creating your experiment without the wizard, select the desired mode from the drop-down list on the Summary Page.
