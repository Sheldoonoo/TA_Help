Rheological Units and Factors

Further information on the factors discussed here and their derivation, is given in, for example, K. Walters "Rheometry", Wiley, 1975, and A.A. Collyer and D.W. Clegg, editors, "Rheological Measurement", 2nd edition, Chapman and Hall, 1998.

The physical quantities, or variables, that are either directly controlled or directly measured on a rotational rheometer, are the torque, the angular velocity (or, for some experimental procedures, the angular displacement) and the normal force. How these are related to the shear stress, shear rate (or shear strain) and normal stress experienced by the sample, will depend on the system geometry. Each geometry therefore has a set of factors used in the conversion from instrument variables to sample variables.

Thus    s = Fs M   

     = FW

            N = FN . Fz

Fm = Fs / F

where s is the shear stress, M is the torque, is the shear rate, W is the angular velocity, N is the normal stress and Fz is the normal force. Fs , F are FN the shear stress, shear rate and normal force factors respectively. The measurement system factor, Fm, is defined as the ratio of the shear stress factor to the shear rate factor. It is unnecessary to define an additional factor, Fg for the conversion of angular displacement to strain through the expression:

g = Fg f

where g is the shear strain and f is the angular displacement, since Fg º F.

The fluid density factor, Fr, is used in the correction for sample inertia in oscillatory measurements.
