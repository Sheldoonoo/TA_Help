Choosing the Q5000 SA Purge Gas

You can control the sample atmosphere during experiments by connecting a purge gas to the system. Purge gas is distributed separately to the humidity chamber sample and reference sides.

  • Recommended Q5000 SA Purge Gas: Nitrogen (dry)

  • Purge gas can be obtained from a pressurized cylinder or an in-house supply source. Gas supplied from an in-house source should be passed through a sieve dryer to remove any trace of moisture before it enters the instrument.

NOTE: If you are using laboratory purge gas, rather than bottle purge gas, you will need to install an external drier.

WARNING:  Do not use hydrogen or any other explosive gas.

WARNING:  Do not use any liquid in the purge lines.

Setting the Flow Rates

The gas and flow rates for the balance chamber and the sample measurement of the humidity chamber are adjusted using the Instrument Preferences/Humidity Page. The software assumes that nitrogen gas is always used as the balance and humidity chamber purge gas. The default flow rates are 200 mL/min for the humidity chamber and 10 mL/min for the balance. Flow rates as low as 100 mL/min can be used for the humidity chamber, however, the time for samples to come to equilibrium with the humidified purge becomes longer.

Before you start the purge gas, make sure that the gas source is connected to the purge ports. (Click here for information Connecting Purge Gas Lines.) In addition, ensure that all lines are clear and that your supply of purge gas is sufficient for the experiment.

NOTE: Purge gases are never changed during a Q5000 SA experiment.
