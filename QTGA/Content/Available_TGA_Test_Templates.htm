TGA Ramp Experiments

Most TGA experiments are performed by heating the material at a constant rate from ambient temperature to a temperature above the point of total decomposition. The purge gas that surrounds the material either remains the same throughout the experiment (TGA Ramp) or is switched at an elevated temperature (TGA Ramp with Gas Switch) to cause additional weight changes to occur. A variety of inert and “reactive” purge gases can be used, including nitrogen, argon, helium (inert) and air, oxygen (reactive). Gas switching experiments generally involve switching from an inert to reactive gas at a specific temperature. For example, carbon black determinations are achieved by switching from nitrogen to oxygen at 600°C.

TGA Heat and Hold Experiments

Heat and Hold experiments are used to assess the thermal stability of materials over time at elevated temperatures. They are also used to assess the rate of volatiles (water, solvent) evolution/drying at a specific temperature, as well as the adsorption/desorption of gases in applications such as catalyst reactivity, porosity of ceramics and oxidative stability. There are two types of Heat and Hold experiments: TGA Heat and Hold – where the material is heated rapidly and held at an elevated temperature or TGA Stepwise Isothermal – where the material is heated rapidly and then held at a series of successively higher temperatures.
