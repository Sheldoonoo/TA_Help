R Squared

R² (R-squared) is a mathematical term describing how much error is attributable to the regression model. R² is defined as one minus a ratio of sums. The ratio numerator is the sum of the squared differences between the actual data and the model. The ratio denominator is the sum of the squared differences between the actual data and the mean.

 

R² = 1  Sum((data[i] - regression[i])²) / Sum((data[i] - datamean)²)

 

R² approaches unity as the regression model approaches a perfect fit to the data. R² values of 0.999 or better are indicative of good fits to the data. R² values of 0.950 or less typically indicate poor fits.
