Determining the Compensation Values

Determining the thermal compensation value consists of performing a sequence of rheometer zeroing at some temperature, then changing the temperature in small increments and re-zeroing each time. This process is quite time consuming and in most instances, the approximate values given below will suffice.

Should you wish to determine the values, you can use the wizard supplied in the Rheology Advantage software.

NOTE:  If you have the normal force option, you can obtain the compensation value by performing a temperature ramp under normal force control (compression) and then fitting a straight line to the graph of Gap vs. Temperature.

Why Use Thermal Compensation?

One of the features of the "Automatic Gap Setting" (AGS) facility is the ability to compensate for changes in the gap due to temperature changes during the measurement. The compensation is an active correction made to the gap—the rheometer physically moves the rheometer head to ensure that the actual gap remains constant.

The effects of expansion/contraction of the metallic parts of the rheometer can be eliminated by calibrating and then activating the thermal gap compensation factor in the software. For the most accurate compensation, you must obtain a value for each different measuring geometry and temperature module used.
