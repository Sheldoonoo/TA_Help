Calibrating the DMA

 

Each DMA must be calibrated for accuracy, when certain operations are performed. This help topic describes the types of calibration available and when to perform those calibrations. Click on the links provided to obtain further details on a particular calibration procedure.

Types of Calibration

The following types of calibration are available for the DMA Q800:

Clamp Calibration

Clamp Calibration involves up to three steps (mass, zero, and compliance) depending on the clamp type installed. This calibration is performed each time the clamp is changed.

  • Clamp Mass calibration is performed to allow the instrument to compensate for the mass of a specific clamp.  

  • Clamp Zero calibration is needed to determine the point of zero sample length for automated sample length (tension) or thickness (compression and penetration) measurement.

  • Compliance Calibration is used to measure the flexibility of a clamp and calibrates the instrument to that flexibility.

Position Calibration

Position Calibration is used to calibrate the absolute position of the drive shaft (and slide) as read by the optical encoder.

Electronics Calibration

Electronics Calibration automatically removes the air to lock the slide (drive shaft) and applies a static force to the motor as a calibration reference.

Force Calibration

Force Calibration (balance and weight) is used to adjust the force exerted by the clamp on the sample and the force registered by the instrument as the experiment proceeds.

Dynamic Calibration

Dynamic Calibration (mass, compliance, and analyze) is used to characterize the dynamic performance of the instrument by measuring several samples of known stiffness and loss.

Calibration Report

You can obtain a DMA calibration report containing information regarding all of the calibrations above by selecting Calibration Report from the Calibration menu.

Temperature Calibration

Temperature Calibration is useful for experiments in which precise transition temperatures are essential.

Use the Calibration menu to select the types of calibration listed above.

Determining When to Calibrate

Each DMA must be calibrated for accuracy, using the following basic guidelines.  

  • You need to perform Clamp Calibration under the following circumstances:

  1. When you change from one clamp type to another.

  2. If the clamp does not float properly.  This can be caused by residue on the clamps or by changes in ambient conditions.  If you do not properly calibrate the clamp, it may affect the ability to make measurements as well as modulus values, particularly for very weak samples.

  3. If you suspect incorrect sample length measurements.

  4. If you have improper clamping or have entered an incorrect sample length, it may result in a high value for the compliance calculation.  (e.g., High compliance calculation can lead to modulus values that are too high for stiff materials).

  5. After loading new instrument software.

  6. You need to perform the Position Calibration under the following circumstances:

  1. When the DMA is moved or at least once a month.  The purpose of this calibration is to calibrate the absolute position of the drive shaft (and slide) as read by the optical encoder.

  2. If the probe will not float after you have performed a Clamp Calibration.

  3. If you turned off the instrument without locking the drive shaft first.

  4. You need to perform the Instrument Calibrations under the following circumstances:

  1. When the instrument is moved.

  2. If the force is not linear over the entire traveling distance of the moveable clamp, you may need to perform an Instrument Force Calibration and a Clamp Calibration.  For example, the clamp floats normally in some positions, but not in other positions.

  3. If the modulus and tan delta have unusual responses to changes in frequency (e.g., there is a spread in the modulus in a non-transition zone), you may need to perform the Instrument Dynamic and Electronics Calibrations.

  4. If the feed hose for the air cool or GCA is either removed or installed.

  5. On a regular (monthly) basis.  You will need to perform all of the Instrument Calibrations.

The calibration report shows the current calibration status and when the calibrations were last performed.
