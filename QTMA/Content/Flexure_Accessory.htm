Flexure Accessory

 

The Flexure Accessory can be used on the TMA for three-point bending studies to measure the flexibility and strength of a variety of materials, such as composites, plastics, and PC boards. It employs a knife-edged probe and either of two types of fixtures, quartz or low-friction aluminum, depending upon the type of experiment desired.

Quartz Fixture

The quartz fixture is the standard three-point platform with two knife edges spaced 5.08 mm (0.2 in) apart. This accessory permits the determination of deflection temperature under load.

Low-Friction Aluminum Fixture

The low-friction aluminum fixture consists of an aluminum three-point bend platform with two rollers spaced 10.16 mm (0.4 in) apart. It is generally used for experiments such as modulated and dynamic experiments. It is not recommended for use in thermal expansion experiments.

Equation

Required loading is calculated using the following equation:

 

P =  2 Sbd2        

3 L

Where:

P = calculated load (N)

S = maximum fiber stress (Pa)  (1 psi = 6.895 x 103 Pa)

b = sample width (m)

d = sample depth (m)

L = width of span between the knife edge supports of the flexure platform (5.08 x 10-3 m).

Installing the Flexure Accessory

The flexure accessory uses a special flexure probe and fixture and the standard stage.

  1. Remove the existing probe as follows:
    1. Raise the furnace and rotate it clockwise to move it off to the side.
    2. Grasp the top of the probe with one hand. Using the other hand, locate and hold the probe-locking lever, found behind the door that covers the weight tray.
    3. Unscrew the locking lever by turning it counterclockwise approximately one turn.
    4. Raise the probe gently and twist slightly to aid its removal from the stage opening.
  1. Install the standard stage, if it is not already in place. For instructions to install the stage click here Installing the Stage.
  2. Install the flexure probe as follows:
    1. Raise the furnace and rotate it clockwise to move it off to the side.
    2. Insert the core end of the probe carefully into the opening in the TMA stage.
    3. Loosen the probe-locking lever, which is the knurled post found behind the weight tray door, by turning it counterclockwise.  Hold the probe-locking lever in the up position and continue lowering the probe into the stage until you can feel it seat in the locking mechanism.
    4. Tighten the probe-locking lever by turning it clockwise.
    5. Calibrate the newly installed probe as directed. See Probe Calibration.

Preparing Samples

Samples used with the flexure accessory should have the following specifications:

  • No wider than the fixture being used, quartz or low-friction aluminum
  • Uniform width and thickness
  • Thick enough to be rigid when placed on flexure platform
  • 7 to 10 mm long.

Loading Samples

To load your flexure samples on the TMA, follow these steps. See the figures below.

  1. Raise the furnace, and rotate it clockwise to move it off to the side.
  2. Remove any previously run samples from the stage, and ensure that no residue remains.
  3. Open the probe.
  4. Place the fixture on top of the stage. (For the aluminum low-friction fixture make sure the rollers are in place.)
  5. For the low-friction aluminum fixture:  Gently pull the probe down using the probe locking lever to align the V-shaped groove in the fixture with the knife-edge probe face as shown in the figure below.
  6. Place the sample on the fixture so that it is centered across the points.
  7. Position the sample and platform on the stage under the flexure probe tip so that the probe knife edge is parallel to the platform points and is centered between them as shown in the figure to the right.
  8. Press ZERO LENGTH on the touch screen or select Control/Length/Zero from the instrument control menu to provide a zero reference point.
  9. If the TMA is operating in any of the enhanced modes (any mode other than Standard and Calibration), enter the additional sample shape dimensions.  NOTE:  In the Standard mode the size refers to the sample thickness.
  10. Adjust the thermocouple position so that it is in close proximity to the sample.
  11. Move the furnace into position and lower it.

Experimental Guidelines for Dynamic Experiments

  • Maintain a minimum length to width ratio of at least 4:1
  • Use a dynamic force of 0.05 to 0.2N, recommend starting with 0.1N
  • Use a static force of 0.1 to 0.3N
  • Use a frequency of 0.05 to 0.5Hz, recommend starting with 0.5Hz
