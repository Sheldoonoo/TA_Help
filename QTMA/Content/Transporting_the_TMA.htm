Transporting the TMA

 

Commercially Shipping the Instrument

Important:  If you need to ship this instrument, you will need to contact TA Instruments' Service to properly package the instrument for shipment. The instrument must be properly packed up as explained in this section and the voltage configuration unit must be removed. Improper packaging will result in damage to the instrument. See: TA Instruments Offices to contact service.

Transporting the Instrument by Vehicle

If, however, you need to transport the TMA Q Seriesâ„¢ instrument yourself by vehicle, follow these instructions to pack up the instrument safely. It is very important to properly protect this precision instrument to guard against potential problems when the unit is unpacked and used again. These instructions are not needed when moving the instrument on a cart within the same building.

WARNING:  Have an assistant help you pack this unit.  Do not attempt to do this alone.

Installing the Shipping Material

The TMA Q400 must be packed carefully to prevent damage to delicate parts.  To pack the instrument  follow these steps:

CAUTION:  Only qualified service personnel should perform steps 7 and 8. The other steps may be completed by the customer.  

WARNING:  Whenever you remove the balance enclosure, make sure that the power is off and the instrument is unplugged.

  1. Turn off the instrument's power switch, which is located on the back of the instrument and remove the power cord.
  2. Disconnect all other cables and lines attached to the back of the instrument. Place the cables and lines in a box to be shipped with the instrument.
  3. Use a 3/32-inch hex wrench to remove the screws, two on each side, that hold the balance enclosure in place.
  4. Slide the balance enclosure out and remove.
  5. Remove the 4-40 nylon screw from the weight tray and pull it out of the front opening of the balance housing. See the figure to the right.
  6. Unplug the thermocouple connection. Reach in from the left side and slide the foam support in under the counterweight.  See the figure below.

  1. Carefully slide the clamping bars horizontally left or right to install them on the suspension. Using a 7/64-inch wrench, install the eight (8) screws on the front and rear clamping bars of the suspension shipping clamp. Refer to the figure below for guidance.

 

  1. Carefully slide in first the top and then the bottom clamping bars of the suspension shipping clamp. Install the long screws using the 7/64-inch hex wrench. Refer to the figure above.
  2. Manually raise the furnace and rotate it clockwise to move it off to the side.
  3. Take the stage shield off by pulling it straight up. (Refer to the figure below for identification of parts for steps 10 through 13.)

  1. Unscrew the large stage nut and remove.
  2. Twist the stage retainer ring (with key slots) counterclockwise and pull it up off the three posts.
  3. Place the shipping foam inside the stage opening and reverse steps 11, 10, and 9 to replace the parts of the stage again.
  4. Place the foam sheet on top of the stage shield.
  5. Rotate the furnace back into position over the stage and lower it.
  6. Unscrew the black cap on top of the furnace post.
  7. Slip the cardboard cylinder over the furnace post, then replace the cap.
  8. Replace the weight tray in of the front opening of the balance housing. Replace the 4-40 nylon screw.
  9. Slide the balance enclosure into position.
  10. Use a 3/32-inch hex wrench to install the screws, two on each side, that hold the balance enclosure in place.
  11. If you are shipping the instrument by commercial carrier, remove the voltage configuration unit. Click here  Installing a Voltage Configuration Unit for the steps to reverse.
  12. If you have retained the shipping pallet, follow the next steps to mount the instrument. Have an assistant hold one side up while you remove the two mounting feet on one side. Then move to the other side and remove the mounting feet.
  13. Place the pallet on the floor. Using two people, lift the instrument onto the pallet.
  14. Use a 7/16-inch socket wrench to install the five hold-down bolts and washers on the bottom of the shipping pallet.
  15. If desired, you can wrap the instrument in a large plastic bag as shown below. Place the original box over the instrument, if it is available.  The instrument is now ready for transport.
