Installing/Removing the Dual Surface Film Tension Clamp

 

The procedures that follow describe the installation and removal of the dual surface film tension clamp (PN 985016.901), which is an optional DMA clamp fixture. For more information on this clamp, see Dual Surface Film Tension Clamp.

Installing the Clamp

To install the film tension clamp on the DMA Q800 instrument, follow these steps:

  1. Place the fixed clamp on the instrument first, aligning the screw holes with the mounting posts.

  2. Seat the fixed clamp firmly on all four mounting posts. Tighten the screws with the hex wrench. Do not over tighten the attachment screws.

  3. Press the CLAMP key on the instrument touch screen to release the drive shaft (drive shaft should move freely up and down).

  4. Loosen and remove the clamping screw (with washers) from the moveable clamp. Open the clamp face to the fully open position.

  5. Slide the hex wrench through the open clamp face and loosen the setscrew (the setscrew should NOT project out through the bottom of the clamp).

  6. Slide the dovetail of the moveable clamp into the dovetail holder of the drive shaft.

  7. Temporarily install the offset gauge (shown to the right) into the fixed clamp as shown in the figure below.  Position the offset gauge (normally used for calibration) in the gauge block towards the front of the clamp.  Tighten the clamping screw to hold the gauge block in the fixed clamp.

  1. Raise the moveable clamp up (towards the fixed clamp) and use your finger to gently press on the back of the moveable clamp so the front face of the clamp is touching the back face of the offset gauge. (See the figure below.) This will ensure that the moveable clamp is properly aligned with the fixed clamp.  

  1. Once the clamp faces are properly aligned, use a hex wrench to tighten the setscrew in the moveable clamp and hold it in position in the drive shaft (see the figure below).  Do not over tighten the setscrew.

  1. Remove the offset gauge.

  2. Replace the clamping screw (with washers) in the moveable clamp.

  3. Make sure the appropriate clamp type and mode are selected on the controller.

  4. Calibrate the probe (see Calibrating the DMA).

  5. Position the thermocouple so that it is close to, but not touching, the sample.

Removing the Clamp

To remove the film tension clamp from the DMA, follow these steps:

  1. Loosen, but do not remove, the four hex screws holding the fixed clamp on the clamp mounting posts.

  2. Loosen the setscrew holding the moveable clamp to the drive shaft.

  3. Slide the dovetail out to remove the moveable clamp.

  4. Remove the fixed clamp.
