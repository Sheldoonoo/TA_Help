Basic Steps Needed to Run a SDT Experiment

 

The SDT is used to perform simultaneous measurements of TGA and either DSC (most commonly used) or DTA, depending upon the desired experiment.  Throughout this online help documentation you will see reference to DSC-TGA experiments or TGA-DTA experiments.  This nomenclature refers to experiments that save heat flow and weight, or weight and delta T (µV and/or °C), respectively.

All of your SDT experiments will have the following general outline. In some cases not all of these steps will be performed.

  • Calibrating the instrument. See Calibrating the SDT.

  • Selecting the cup type and material. See Choosing a Cup Type.

  • Setting up the primary purge gas flow rate. See Recommended Purge Gases and Flow Rates.

  • Entering experiment information through the TA controller. This includes selecting the mode,  choosing signals to save, then entering sample and instrument information on the Summary Page and the Notes Page.

  • Creating or selecting the experimental procedure using the instrument control software.  See Specifying a Test Procedure.

  • Selecting and taring two empty sample cups on the sample and reference beams.

  • Preparing and loading the sample(s).

  • Closing the furnace.

  • Starting the experiment. See Starting a Run and/or Sequence.

  • Unloading the sample at the end of the experiment.
