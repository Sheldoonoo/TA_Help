Calibrating the Weight

 

Weight calibration is required when you first install the Q5000 IR or Q5000 SA systems. After initial calibration, weight verification should be performed periodically (once a month is recommended) and, if necessary, recalibration should be performed.

Weight calibration can be performed manually (using an empty pan and a calibration weight) or automatically (using the two weight calibration pans).

  • Automatic weight calibration allows you to automatically verify and calibrate weight using the Platinum Automatic Tasks features or by selecting Calibrate/Autoweight. In this case the pan differential is used to calibrate the weight. (NOTE:  The weight calibration pans may only be used when a platinum reference pan is installed for the Q5000 IR instrument or when a quartz reference pan is installed for the Q5000 SA.) For more detailed information click here Automatically Calibrating Weight

  • Manual weight calibration is performed using a known calibration weight. For the Q5000 IR, this is typically 100 mg. For the Q5000 SA, this is typically 50 mg. This procedure involves weighing a pan with and without the calibration weight to calibrate the 0 to 100  mg weight range.  This procedure is conducted through the controller software using the Calibrate/Weight menu.  For more detailed information click here Manually Calibrating the Weight

Automatically Calibrating Weight

The Q5000 provides an auto weight calibration procedure. This procedure utilizes calibration pans that are found in the Q5000 accessory kit.

NOTE: Auto weight calibration can also be scheduled to run automatically using the Platinum features. See: Using the Schedule Page

Follow the instructions below:

  1. Obtain the Autoweight Calibration pans from the accessory kit.

  2. Place Pan #1 (Baseline Pan) on the tray. (Typically pan position #24 is used for the Q5000 IR and position #9  for the Q5000 SA.)

  3. Place Pan #2 (Weighted Pan) on the tray. (Typically pan position #25 is used for the Q5000 IR and position #10 for the Q5000 SA.)

  4. Click the Platinum button on the lower left-hand side of the Instrument Control window. The available tasks/features are displayed in the panel above the buttons.

  5. Select Weight Calibration from the list of Automatic Tasks.

  6. Select the Weight Calibration tab, if it is not shown.

  7. Enter the sample pan number position of the Baseline Pan (e.g., #24 or #9)from the list.

  8. Enter the sample pan number position of the 100 mg Pan (e.g., #25 or #10) from the list.

  9. Enter the Pan Differential weight (obtained from the paper enclosed in the accessory kit).

  10. Choose your Settings:

    • If you want to determine if your current calibration is acceptable, choose the Validation (Validation is defined as a periodic check of instrument performance to ensure that the last calibration is still valid. This can be performed with a different sample of the same standard used to calibration/verification.) radio button. Check the Perform Calibration if Validation Fails box to automatically perform the weight calibration in the event the validation test does not fall within the acceptable validation criteria.

    • If you wish to perform a full weight calibration, choose the Calibration (Calibration is defined as the adjustment of an instrument so that it provides correct theoretical value for a specific measurement on a well-characterized [certified] standard.) radio button. Check the Perform Verification after Calibration box to automatically verify (Verification is defined as a double-check of calibration, performed immediately after calibration, usually on the same sample to verify that the instrument has accepted the adjustments made during calibration.) the new calibration, if desired.

    • Enter the desired Validation Criteria.

  11. Click Start Now to begin the calibration immediately or click the Scheduling Page to schedule the calibration for a later time. (If desired, you can set up the Messaging option for this task as well.) When initiated, each pan is measured and the calibration is determined using the differential pan weight. No interaction is required once the calibration is started and the calibration is usually completed in less than three minutes.

  12. View the calibration report to observe the updated calibration values.

Manually Calibrating the Weight

Select Calibrate/Weight from the menu or click on located on the tool bar to perform weight calibration.

The weight calibration functions guide you through the calibration procedure step-by-step. You will need to obtain the following items for this procedure:

  • two (2) empty, clean sample pans (The same type of pan must be installed on the sample and tare sides.)

  • calibration weights

  • brass tweezers

NOTE: Always handle the calibration weights with brass tweezers, not with your fingers. The oils and salt from your skin can change the calibration weight.

Scroll through the steps for more detailed information on weight calibration or, if you want help for a particular step in the calibration, select the step number below:

  • Electronic Balance Tare

  • Zero the Weight Range

  • Calibrate the Weight Range

  • Complete the Weight Calibration

Electronic Balance Tare

Follow the instructions appropriate to the model instrument you are using:

  1. Q5000 IR:  Loosen the three thumbscrews on the tare cap, which is located on the underside of the balance chamber. Remove the cap and put it aside to reinstall later. See the figure to the right.

Q5000 SA:  Open the humidity chamber by using the Furnace/Open command on the Control touch screen.

  1. Hang an empty sample pan of the same type and size as your experimental sample pan on the tare hook (left side). This is your tare pan.

  2. Q5000 IR: Replace the tare cap and tighten the thumbscrews once more. This will prevent air currents from affecting the weight measurement.

  3. Place an empty sample pan that you plan to use in your experiment on the sample platform.

  4. Press the Load Pan button to load the pan onto the sample hook and close the furnace. Closing the furnace prevent air currents from affecting the weight reading.

  5. Press Next to begin the electronic tare. The Zero Weight Range window is displayed.

Zero the Weight Range

The instrument is measuring the weight range. The tare weight is stored by the instrument for this range.

  1. Select Next to go on with the calibration procedure.

  2. Once the weight is stabilized, select Accept. (Press Back to stop the weight stabilization and return to the beginning of this step, if desired.)

Once complete, the calibration procedure will automatically advance to the next step.

Calibrate the Weight Range

This step in the procedure calibrates the 100 mg weight range for the Q5000. (Typically 100 mg is used for the Q5000 IR and 50 mg is used for the Q5000 SA due to the shape of the quartz hemispherical pans.)

  1. Press Unload Pan to unload the sample pan.

  2. Obtain the standard weight from the accessory Kit.

  3. Place the weight in the sample pan.

  4. Press Load Pan to load the pan onto the sample hook and close the furnace. Closing the furnace prevent air currents from affecting the weight reading.

  5. Enter the exact mass of the standard, then select Next. The system will begin measuring the combined weight of the standard and the pan.

  6. Once the weight is stabilized, select Accept. (Press Back to stop the weight stabilization and return to the beginning of this step, if desired.)

Complete the Weight Calibration

When the weight has stabilized, follow these steps to complete the weight calibration:

  1. Press Unload Pan to unload the sample pan.

  2. Remove the calibration weight. Weight calibration has been successfully completed.

  3. Click Finish to exit the calibration wizard.

  4. View the calibration report.
