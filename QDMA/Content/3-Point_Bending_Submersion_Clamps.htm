3-Point Bending Submersion Clamps

 

This type of clamp is most useful for stiff, highly elastic materials such as metals, ceramics, highly filled thermosetting polymers, and highly filled and crystalline thermoplastic polymers. When used for oscillatory experiments, 3-point bending clamps require a preload force. The 3-point bending clamp is therefore not useful for DMA samples that will not support a preload force, such as elastomers. Samples can be heated up to 80°C using the 3-point bending submersion clamp.

The 3-point bending submersion clamp is used with the 5, 10 or 15-mm base of the standard 3-point bending clamp and the specialized tank and yoke assembly.  

 

WARNING:  Proper clamp selection within the instrument control software (e.g., three-point bending submersion) is required to ensure that the 80°C upper temperature range cannot be exceeded.

WARNING:  Flammable or volatile fluids should not be used as the submersion fluid.

Preparing Samples

Sample preparation is one of the most important factors in achieving accurate and reproducible modulus values. Each type of clamp requires a specific method for sample preparation.

Ideally, samples should be molded, machined, or otherwise fashioned into a rectangular shape for use with either of the 3-point bending clamps. The 3-point bending clamps are very sensitive to any sample malformation, therefore, the rectangle must be well formed, without any twists or buckles.

  • Thickness:  The thickness of the rectangle should be 1/10 to 1/32 of the span (length) of the chosen 3-point bending sample supports.  The maximum thickness is 7 mm.  The thickness should be as uniform as possible across the sample, to within 0.02 mm or less. The cube of the sample thickness is used in the modulus calculation; therefore, a 3% error in thickness becomes a 10% error in the calculated modulus.

  • Width:  The width of the rectangle should be 5 to 15 mm.  The width and thickness dimensions should be uniform across the sample to within 0.02 mm.  

  • Length:  Cut the sample 5 mm longer than the span of the chosen 3-point bending clamp sample supports (15, 10 or 5 mm).  Make certain the sample does not touch the walls of the submersion chamber.

Other sample shapes such as cylinders and tubes can be used in the 3-point bending clamps.

Mounting Samples

To mount the rectangular sample on the 3-point bending clamps, follow these steps:

  1. Press the DRIVE Float/Lock  key on the DMA to allow the clamp to float freely.  

  2. Manually lift the moveable clamp and slide the sample in from the side onto the two supports.  

  3. Make sure that the sample does not touch the bath side walls.

  4. Align the sample so that it lays perpendicular to the cross member of the support.

  5. Adjust the thermocouple so that it is 1 mm below and 1 mm to the side of the sample. When using the small 3-point bending clamps, the thermocouple should be as close as possible to the sample. The tip of the thermocouple must be completely submerged in the fluid.

  6. Carefully fill the fluid container (cup). It is recommended that the fluid be loaded into cup using a squeeze bottle, small funnel, pipette, or other device that will direct flow of fluid directly into cup without spilling or splashing the fluid.

CAUTION:  OVERFILLING THE CUP OR SPILLING DURING FILLING can result in fluid getting down into the air bearing, which could cause permanent damage to the instrument.

Running an Experiment

When you use any of the 3-point bending clamps for experiments, you will need to set up the experimental parameters using the Instrument Control software.

When one of the small 3-point bending clamps is used, you must make sure that both the fixed and moveable clamps are installed.  The furnace will hit the clamp when it is closed, unless both parts are installed.

Following these general guidelines when using a 3-point bending clamp:

  1. Set up the instrument parameters. Note that force track and preload force parameters must be entered. The recommended values are 150 to 200 % for force track and 0.005 to 1 N for preload force.

  2. If Force Track is not selected, then the constant preload force is used throughout the experiment. Otherwise the preload force is adjusted to maintain the proper force track force ratio.

  3. Press the CLAMP key on the DMA instrument to lower the clamp.  This will apply the preload force selected in step 1.

  4. Press the MEASURE key on the DMA.  This will start the motor in the selected operation mode and activate the force track parameter (if selected in step 1).

  5. View the signals.  Pay special attention to these signals:

    • Amplitude:  This signal should achieve and maintain the value programmed.  If running a multistrain experiment, the amplitude will cycle through the values programmed.

    • Stiffness:  The stiffness should be within the instrument’s measurable range of 100 N/m to 10,000,000 N/m.  

    • Drive Force:  The drive force should be between 0.0001 and 18 N.  If the drive force is not within this range, either increase the programmed amplitude to increase the drive force, or increase the stiffness of the sample by changing the physical dimensions of the sample.  

    • Preload Force:  If force track is used, the preload force will automatically be adjusted to remain a set percentage greater than the force required to drive the sample at the programmed amplitude.  If force track is not used, the preload force should read the value set in the instrument parameters.  For more information regarding preload force, see Understanding the DMA Forces.

If the initial conditions are acceptable, (i.e., smooth oscillation and good modulus values), start the experiment. See Starting a Run and/or Sequence.

If the values are not acceptable, then change conditions appropriately.

Removing Samples

When the experiment has run to completion, remove the sample from the 3-point bending submersion clamp as follows:

  1. Wait for the sample to return to room temperature before you attempt to remove it.

  2. Press the FURNACE key to raise the furnace.

  3. Press the CLAMP key twice to raise the moveable clamp.

  4. Press the DRIVE Float/Lock key to lock the moveable clamp in the upper position.

  5. Using tweezers, remove the sample. If any sample residue remains stuck to the clamp, remove it by scraping it off with a razor blade or similar tool. If further cleaning is needed, see Cleaning the Clamps.

  6. Using a pipette or syringe to avoid spilling fluid on the instrument, remove the remaining fluid from fluid tank and wipe the inside of the tank dry.

CAUTION:  Be careful not to scratch the contact surface of the clamps. In addition, take care not to drip fluid on the DMA instrument.
