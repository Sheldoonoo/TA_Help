Loading/Unloading a Sample

 

Samples should only be loaded into clean, tared pans to ensure proper weight measurement. The amount of sample to load is dependent on numerous factors:  sample form, density, amount of weight loss/gain expected, and pan capacity. In general, for an unknown sample, start with a sample size of approximately 20 mg.

Instructions on loading samples in sample pans are presented here.

Loading Pans

Loading Open Pans

After taring the sample pan, load the sample as follows:

  1. Q5000 IR Only:  If you do not plan to use sealed aluminum pans, you can retract the punching mechanism. Use a Phillips screwdriver to loosen the screw on top of the punching mechanism. Slide the mechanism out of the way.

  2. Place the sample in the sample pan and position the pan on the sample tray in its original position. (This may be done with the tray on or off the instrument.) The bail of the sample pan should align with the line or groove on the tray so that the sample pan can be picked up by the sample hang-down wire.

NOTE:  Always use brass tweezers to handle the sample pans.

  1. Replace the sample tray on the Autosampler, if needed, and place the plastic cover over the samples, if desired.

Preweigh Samples

You can preweigh the samples prior to the start of the experiment, if desired, in order to obtain a record of the weight. A preweight is valuable for highly volatile materials. This function is performed before you start an experiment. This weight is stored within the data file record. For more information click here Preweighing Your Samples.

 

NOTE (Q5000 SA Only):  Some materials readily build up static electricity charges that can create problems during loading. This phenomenon is most likely to occur in experiments where the ambient humidity is low. Metal-coated quartz pans, which eliminate this problem by completing a grounding path to the balance housing, are supplied as the standard pans used with the Q5000 SA.

 

Loading Sealed Pans

After taring the sample pan, load the sample as follows:

  1. Make sure the punch mechanism is correctly positioned and calibrated. See the figure to the right. This is important for consistently successful punching of sealed pans.

  2. Follow the procedure to load the sample and seal the sample pans using the sample encapsulating press. See Preparing Sealed TGA Pans for detailed instructions.

NOTE:  Always use brass tweezers to handle the sample pans.

  1. Reposition the bail/pan on the sample tray in its original pan position. (This may be done with the tray on or off the instrument.) The tab on the bail should align with the groove in the tray, so that the sample pan can be picked up by the sample hang-down wire. Check that the bail wire is at a right angle (90Â°) to the bail base. This will avoid failure to connect with the hang-down wire hook or interference with the punch during the pan punching process.

  2. Replace the sample tray on the Autosampler, if needed, and place the plastic cover over the samples, if desired.

Unloading a Pan

Unloading a sample can be affected by several different parameters. The following scenarios cover these factors:

Automatically Unloading a Pan

At the end of a run the pan will automatically unload unless the following situations apply. A pan will stay loaded if...

  • ...it is the last or only pan in a sequence and you have set the Post Test conditions to leave the FurnaceClosed. (Leaving the furnace closed also overrides the Air Cool option.)

  • ...you have selected Air Cool on the Post Test window. In this case the furnace will lower when the temperature reached 500Â°C, but the pan will not be unloaded until the Return to Temp method-end condition is reached.

  • ...the Autosampler is disabled and you have selected Leave Furnace Closed on the Tools/Instrument Preferences/Autosampler page.

Manually Unloading a Pan

To manually unload a sample pan, choose one of the following functions:

  • Instrument Control Program:  Select Control/Sample Unload from the menu then click Apply.

  • Instrument Touch Screen:  Select the Control options then select Unload, pick the applicable pan number to unload and press Apply.
