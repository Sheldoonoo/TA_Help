Cleaning the Instrument

 

Different techniques must be used when cleaning various parts of the Q5000 instruments. Choose from the list below for detailed information.

Cleaning the Touch Screen

You can clean the TGA touch screen as often as you like.  The touch screen should be cleaned with a household liquid glass cleaner and soft cloth.  Wet the cloth, not the touch screen with the glass cleaner, and then wipe off the touch screen and surrounding surfaces.

CAUTION:  Do not use harsh chemicals, abrasive cleansers, steel wool, or any rough materials to clean the touch screen as you may scratch the surface and degrade its properties.

Cleaning the IR Furnace

NOTE:  Some materials readily build up static electricity changes that can create problems during loading. The Q5000 IR is designed with a unique hang-down wire assembly that provides a direct path to ground when metal pans are used. Hence, no static effects will be seen with those pans. When using ceramic, uncoated quartz, or platinum-HT pans, however, it may be necessary to periodically discharge static build-up using an "anti-static pistol." Cleaning the furnace tube will also help discharge static buildup.

WARNING:  Do not touch the furnace sample tube with your bare fingers.  Skin oils may cause devitrification of the quartz glass, resulting in severely reduced sample tube life.  Do not insert metallic instruments inside the sample tube to scrape or chip contaminants from the sample tube as breakage may result.

CAUTION:  When cleaning the furnace DO NOT disturb the hang-down wires, the baffles on the sample tube located directly above the furnace as damage may result.

  1. Press the FURNACE key to lower the furnace completely.

  2. Load an empty platinum or ceramic pan, depending on the corresponding tare side pan. (Aluminum pans CANNOT be used due to the high temperatures.)

  3. Raise the furnace completely.

  4. Heat the TGA to 1000°C in air purge and allow to cool.

CAUTION:  Allow the furnace to cool completely before continuing with the next step.

  1. Unload the sample pan.

  1. Remove the lower furnace assembly by unscrewing the thumbscrew as shown in the figure above.

  2. Remove the lower furnace assembly from the furnace by pulling it straight down until the entire assembly is clear of the instrument.  DO NOT TWIST THE LOWER FURNACE.

  1. Unscrew the knurled nut at the other end of the lower furnace assembly (shown in the figure below) to release it from the instrument cabinet.

  2. Disconnect the tubing from the air cool inlet.

  3. Check for any residue inside the heat-absorbing tube. Remove any residue by turning the assembly upside down or gently blowing the residue out. Place the assembly aside for reinstallation later.

CAUTION: When using any solvent, as directed in the next step, it is recommended that you wear protective rubber gloves.

  1. Place a small cup under the furnace. Rinse the furnace tube using a solvent (such as alcohol) to remove debris.  The solvent will drain out of the bottom of the tube into the cup.

  2. Using a soft bristle brush (we recommend a flexible bottle brush), insert the brush up the furnace from the underside. Gently slide the brush up and down to clean out the inside of the furnace tube, allowing the handle to bend freely.

  3. Rinse the furnace tube with the solvent again, catching the debris and solvent in a cup once again.

  4. Replace the lower furnace assembly as follows:  Orient the lower furnace so that the air cool inlet points to the left  you slide it up. See the figure above left for the parts of the lower furnace assembly. Raise the lower furnace until it goes all the way inside until only the nut shows on the outside. The O-ring will hold it in place.

  5. Turn the thumbscrew enough to hold the assembly in place.

  6. Loosen the outer nut and remove the furnace exhaust tube from the left-hand side of the furnace. See the figure to the right.

  7. Look into the furnace outlet port on the side of the instrument. The holes in the heat-absorbing tube should align with the inlet and outlet ports on the furnace housing allowing light to pass through. Rotate the lower furnace assembly until the proper alignment has been achieved.

  8. Fully tighten the thumbscrew.

  9. Replace the furnace exhaust tube.

  10. Insert the lower furnace cable connector at the opposite end to the panel-mounted connector. Tighten the knurled nut.

  11. Connect the 1/8-inch Teflon TFE tubing from Port B on the front of the instrument to the Air Cool Inlet as shown in the figure on the lower right.

  12. Purge the system with nitrogen for one hour.

  13. Heat the furnace to 900°C to remove any remaining solvent.
