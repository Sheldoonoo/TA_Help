Do's and Don'ts

 

Before you operate your rheometer, following the guidelines outlined below to ensure safe and efficient operation.

Do...

  • Ensure the air supply is always very clean at a stable pressure . A coarse filter and desiccant dryer system plus double filter regulator should be used.
  • Look out for water collection in the filter bowls before and during use of the instrument. Drain the filter bowls whenever necessary.
  • Look for visible signs of dirt on the elements in the filter regulator before use. Replace whenever necessary.
  • Replace the air-bearing clamp when the air is ON, and always ensure it is in place when the instrument is moved or when the air is switched off.
  • Refit the air connector plug supplied with the instrument whenever the airline is disconnected.
  • Disconnect the air from the rheometer and then 'blast' air through the airline whenever starting up after any long idle period. (Not necessary every morning, unless you know your air supply tends to accumulate water overnight.)
  • Connect and switch on the air supply before switching the instrument on.
  • Switch on the water supply to the Peltier plate before switching the instrument on.
  • Use good laboratory practice when using the instrument. Wear safety glasses and protective clothing where necessary.

Don't...

  • Operate the instrument without the correct air supply.
  • Remove the air connector plug until ready to attach purged air supply.
  • Unnecessarily touch the air-bearing spindle unless the air is on. This includes attaching and removing geometries.
  • Use the rheometer head as a lifting point.
  • Operate the instrument without a water supply, if you have a Peltier System.
  • Disconnect or connect any cables, leads etc. while the power is on.
