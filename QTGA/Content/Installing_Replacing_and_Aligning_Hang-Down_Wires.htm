Installing/Replacing the Sample Hang-Down Wire (Current Model)

During installation or replacement, take care not to bend the hang-down wires or damage the hang-down loops.

  1. Turn on the instrument.

  2. Press the FURNACE key to lower the furnace.

  3. Using the ball driver supplied in your TGA accessory kit, loosen the six captive screws securing the balance chamber faceplate to the instrument and remove the faceplate.

  4. Loosen and remove the thumbscrew holding the balance cover on the sample (left) side of the balance mechanism and take off the cover.

  5. Locate the sample hang-down wire in your TGA Accessory Kit.

  6. Hold the wire in your hand so that the doubly bent top hook is pointing to the left and the bottom hook is pointing to the right. See the figure below.

  1. Carefully insert the bottom of the hang-down wire into the top of the furnace far enough so that you can insert the top of the wire into the thermocouple tube without bending the wire.

  2. Thread the hang-down wire up through the thermocouple tube into the balance chamber, and hook the top of the wire over the top of the tube.

NOTE:  To make the hang-down loops easier to see, we suggest sliding a piece of white paper into the balance chamber behind each loop before you hook the hang-down wire into it. (Do not forget to remove the paper when finished.)

  1. Grasp the top hook of the hang-down wire with brass tweezers. Being careful to keep the top hook pointing to the left, pass the double bend through the hang-down loop so the wire is hanging from the loop.

Once the sample hang-down wire is mounted, it needs to be aligned inside the furnace and adjusted relative to the sample platform. See Aligning the Sample Hang-Down Wire (Current Model) for information.

Installing/Replacing the Tare Hang-Down Wire (Current Model)

  1. Turn on the instrument.

  2. Press the FURNACE key to lower the furnace.

  3. Using the ball driver supplied in your TGA accessory kit, loosen the six captive screws securing the balance chamber faceplate to the instrument and remove the faceplate.

  4. Loosen and remove the thumbscrew holding the balance cover on the sample (left) side of the balance mechanism and take off the cover.

  5. Loosen and remove the thumbscrew holding the balance cover on the tare (right) side of the balance mechanism and take off the cover.

  6. Locate the tare hang-down wire in your accessory kit.

  7. Hold the wire in your hand so that the tip of the bottom hook (the single bend hook) can be inserted through the kapton loop hole from the right side as shown in the figure to the right.  Insert the bottom hook through the hole. For the tare hang-down wire alignment is not critical; therefore, the bottom (double bend) hook can be facing in either direction.

  8. Using two sets of brass tweezers, as shown in the figure below, carefully rotate the hook to turn it upside down.

  1. Lower the hook until the double bend hook just reaches the kapton loop hole. Release the hook.

  2. Gently raise and lower the wire until the double bend hook rests on the bottom of the kapton hole.

  3. Select a sample pan of the same size and type that you will use in your experiments and hang it from the tare hang-down wire.

The tare hang-down wire does not require alignment.

Aligning the Sample Hang-Down Wire (Current Model)

To avoid weight signal noise, the TGA instrument must be level so that the sample pan and hang-down wire hang inside the furnace and thermocouple tube without touching them. The angle at which the pan hangs is very sensitive to slight irregularities in bench-top surfaces, so it is important that you select a sturdy table or bench for your TGA.

Once you have your TGA in a satisfactory location, adjust the top and bottom of the sample hang-down wire and level the instrument using the following procedures.

Back to top

To align the top of the sample hang-down wire:

  1. Place an empty sample pan on the sample platform.

  2. Load the sample pan. The TGA will automatically lower the furnace (if necessary), move the sample platform over to the furnace, and load the pan onto the balance. If the pan will not automatically load, place pan manually (using brass tweezers) on the sample hang-down wire and continue with the procedure. Use the instrument control Sample Platform Adjust procedure to correct loading after completing sample hang-down wire alignment.

  3. Check to see whether the top end of the sample hang-down wire is hanging freely and roughly centered within the top of the thermocouple tube inside the balance chamber.

  4. If the wire is not roughly centered inside the thermocouple tube, turn the balance adjustment screw (see figure below) with the 7/64-inch ball driver until the wire is centered. Turning the balance adjustment screw clockwise will move the wire backwards; turning the screw counterclockwise will move the wire to the front.

Back to top

To align the bottom of the hang-down wire:

  1. Press the FURNACE key on the touch screen or keypad  to raise the furnace just to the bottom of the sample pan, and press STOP.

  2. Check the alignment of the sample pan within the furnace. It should hang freely, roughly centered, and should not be touching the sides of the furnace or the thermocouple tube. See the figure below.

  3. If the sample pan is not centered and hanging freely within the furnace, level the TGA instrument by adjusting the feet on the bottom. Turn the feet clockwise to lengthen or counterclockwise to shorten the legs. Continue adjusting until the pan hangs correctly.

  1. Lower the furnace.

  2. Unload and remove the sample pan from the furnace.

  3. Replace the balance chamber faceplate and its 6 screws.

If you had to load the sample pan manually in order to align it in the furnace, you should now adjust the sample platform using the procedure described in Adjusting the Sample Platform.

Installing/Replacing the Sample Hang-Down Wire (with Tare Tube)

During installation or replacement, take care not to bend the hang-down wires or damage the hang-down loops.

  1. Turn on the TGA instrument.

  2. Using the ball driver supplied in your TGA accessory kit, loosen and remove the six screws securing the balance chamber faceplate to the instrument and remove the faceplate.

  3. Loosen and remove the thumbscrew holding the balance cover on the sample (left) side of the balance mechanism and take off the cover (see the figure below).

  1. Press the FURNACE key on the keypad or touch screen  to lower the furnace.

  2. If replacing a sample wire:  Remove the old wire by grasping the bottom of the hang-down wire and lifting it up slightly. Then carefully thread the hang-down loop through top of the double-bent hook to unhook the sample wire.

  1. Once the sample hang-down wire has been unhooked, thread the wire down through the thermocouple tube and into the furnace. Once the wire has cleared the thermocouple, remove it.

  1. Locate the new sample hang-down wire in your TGA accessory kit.

  2. Hold the wire in your hand so that the doubly bent top hook is pointing to the left and the bottom hook is pointing to the right.

  3. Carefully insert the bottom of the hang-down wire into the top of the furnace far enough so that you can insert the top of the wire into the thermocouple tube without bending the wire (see the figure below).

  1. Thread the hang-down wire up through the thermocouple tube into the balance chamber, and hook the top of the wire over the top of the tube (see the figure above). To make the hang-down loops easier to see, we suggest sliding a piece of white paper into the balance chamber behind each loop before you hook the hang-down wire into it. (Do not forget to remove the paper when finished.)

  2. Grasp the top hook of the hang-down wire with brass tweezers. Being careful to keep the top hook pointing to the left, pass the double bend through the hang-down loop so the wire is hanging from the loop.

  3. Replace the balance cover and the faceplate.

Once the sample hang-down wire is mounted, it needs to be aligned inside the furnace and adjusted relative to the sample platform. See Aligning the Sample Hang-Down Wire for information.

Installing/Replacing the Tare Hang-Down Wire (with Tare Tube)

  1. Turn on the TGA instrument.

  2. Using the ball driver supplied in your TGA accessory kit, loosen and remove the six screws securing the balance chamber faceplate to the instrument and remove the faceplate.

  3. Loosen and remove the thumbscrew holding the balance cover on the tare (right) side of the balance mechanism and take off the cover.

  4. Press the FURNACE key on the keypad or touch screen  to lower the furnace.

  5. Unscrew and remove the tare tube.

  6. If replacing the tare wire:  Remove the sample pan from the tare hang-down wire. Grasp the bottom of the hang-down wire and lift it up slightly. Carefully thread the hang-down loop through the top of the hang-down hook to unhook the wire.

  7. Locate the new tare hang-down wire in your accessory kit.

  8. Hold the wire in your hand so that the doubly bent top hook is pointing to the left and the bottom hook is pointing to the right.

  9. Using brass tweezers, insert the tare hang-down wire into the balance chamber on the tare side and down through the hole above the tare tube connection, taking care not to bend the wire.

  1. Being careful to keep the top hook pointing to the left, pass the double bend through the hang-down loop so the wire is hanging from the loop.

  2. Select the sample pan you will use in your experiments, and load one of the same size and type onto the tare hang-down wire.

  3. Replace the tare tube and finger-tighten it to compress the O-ring seal.

  4. Replace the balance cover and faceplate.
