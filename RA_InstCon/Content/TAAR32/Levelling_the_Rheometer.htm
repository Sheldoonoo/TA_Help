Levelling the Rheometer

 

Optimum performance depends upon the instrument being level and in a sturdy position to avoid the possibility of rocking. This is relatively simple to check by placing a bubble spirit level on the Peltier plate.

If the instrument is not level, it can made so by screwing the adjustable feet at each corner of the instrument either in or out, as necessary, checking the spirit level after each adjustment. Use a circular spirit level and view the bubble down the hollow shaft of the air bearing (with the draw-rod removed) to avoid parallax. Once level, press each corner of the instrument to check that all four feet are in contact with the laboratory bench. Any movement caused by pressing should be rectified by adjusting the feet, and then rechecking the level of the Peltier plate.

If the spirit level is of the circular type, it should be placed in the middle of the Peltier plate. If the spirit level is of the bar type, place it along a diameter of the Peltier plate, and re-check the level by placing it along another diameter of the Peltier plate at 90° to the first position.
