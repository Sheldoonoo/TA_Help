Starting the TGA Experimental Wizard

 

This is the opening window for the TGA Experimental Wizard that will guide you through the set up of different types of TGA experiments.

Thermogravimetric Analysis (TGA) is a thermal analysis technique designed to measure the amount or rate of weight change in a material as a function of temperature or time. The weight changes measured are generally weight losses associated with phenomena such as decomposition or volatilization and the results provide information about thermal stability and compositional analysis. However, weight gains associated with the adsorption of moisture or a gas (e.g., oxidation) can also be measured.

TA Instruments offers three basic types of TGA experiments as seen on the TGA Experimental Wizard opening window. Most TGA experiments are performed while heating the material at a constant rate from ambient to a temperature above the point of total decomposition. Additional information about the material can sometimes be obtained by performing more sophisticated High Resolution TGA™ (Hi-Res™ TGA) or Modulated TGA™ (MTGA™) experiments. However, when evaluating a material for the first time, a Conventional TGA experiment is recommended. See also:  Available TGA Test Templates

High Resolution TGA and Modulated TGA are optional accessories for the TGA Q500. If these capabilities are not enabled, they will be grayed out (not available for selection).

Click the radio button for the type of experiment desired, then select Next to proceed or select Back to return to the previous window.
