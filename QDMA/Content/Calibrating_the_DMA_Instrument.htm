Calibrating the DMA Instrument

 

Instrument calibration needs to be done when the DMA is moved, the feed hose for the air cool or GCA is removed or installed, or at least once a month. For further information on each of the three main types of instrument calibration, click on the name below.

  • Electronics calibration

  • Force calibration

  • Dynamic calibration

Electronics Calibration

Electronics calibration automatically removes the air to lock the slide (drive shaft) and applies a static force to the motor as a calibration reference. This calibration is performed at least once a month to calibrate the electronics on the analog board. The instrument then uses a series of frequencies to perform the calibration.

Follow the steps below:

  1. Select Calibrate/Instrument/Electronics from the menu.

  2. Remove both the fixed and moveable clamps.

  3. Press the DRIVE key until the drive shaft can move up and down freely.

  4. Install the shipping bracket. See Installing/Removing the Shipping Bracket for instructions.

  5. Press the FURNACE button to close the furnace.

  6. Click the Calibrate button.

  7. Remove the shipping bracket when the calibration is complete.

  8. Select Next to view the Calibration Report.

  9. Perform the Force Calibration procedure after the Electronics calibration has been completed.

Technical Explanation of Electronic Calibration

This calibration is performed to determine the phase shift and gain factors that the drive electronics produce as a function of drive frequency.  The end products of the calibration are polynomial fits of phase shift versus frequency and of gain versus frequency.

The calibration is mathematically performed as follows:

  1. Follow the instructions to collect the drive signal phase shift and gain at an array of frequencies.

  2. Fit a least-squares polynomial to the phase shift points.

  3. Calculate the SRSS (square root of the sum of the squares) of the discrepancies between the phase shift points and the polynomial phase shift curve.

  4. Fit a least-squares polynomial to the gain points.

  5. Calculate the SRSS of the discrepancies between the gain points and the polynomial gain curve.

  6. Sum the phase and gain SRSS’s to get the reported residual value.

The residual value therefore indicates the relative “goodness of fit” of the electronic calibration.

Force Calibration (Balance and Weight)

Force calibration is used to adjust the force exerted by the clamp on the sample and the force registered by the instrument as the experiment proceeds. This calibration involves two steps—balance and weight calibration.  

Force Calibration  - Step 1 (Force/Balance)

Force/Balance calibration measures the amount of force needed to hold the position of the drive shaft.

Follow the steps below to perform the force/balance calibration:

  1. Select Calibrate/Instrument/Force from the menu.

  2. Remove both the fixed and moveable clamps.

  3. Press the FURNACE key to close the DMA furnace.

  4. Select Calibrate.  The instrument will be calibrated automatically.  The furnace will open when the calibration has completed.  (It normally takes approximately five minutes to complete the force/balance calibration.)

  5. Select the Next button to proceed to the force/weight calibration.

Force Calibration - Step 2 (Weight)

Force/Weight calibration measures the amount of force needed to hold the position of the drive shaft when weight has been added.

Follow the steps below to perform the force/weight calibration:

  1. Make sure that you have just completed the force/balance calibration step 1, as directed.

  2. Place the 100-gram weight on top of the drive shaft dovetail.  Make sure that the weight is centered on the dovetail and that the thermocouples do not touch the weight.  

  3. Press the FURNACE key to close the DMA furnace.

  4. Select Calibration.  The instrument will be calibrated automatically. The furnace will open when the calibration has completed.  (It normally takes approximately five minutes to complete the force/balance calibration.)

  5. Remove the weight.

  6. Click the Next button to view the Calibration Report summary.

  7. Perform the Dynamic calibration after the force/weight calibration has been completed.

Technical Explanation of Force Calibration

This calibration is performed to determine the static force gain as a function of slide position.  The end product of the calibration is a cubic spline representing static force gain versus slide position.

The calibration is mathematically performed as follows:

  1. Follow directions in the software and load the slide with a known weight (usually 100g). You will collect raw force and position data continuously while ramping static force to move the slide over the entire range of travel.

  2. Generate position/force data points at even position intervals via a linear fit of the force data in a 5 second window (50 points) centered about each position.

  3. Fit a cubic spline through these data points.

  4. Calculate the SRSS of the discrepancies between the raw data and the cubic spline.  This is reported as the Force Calibration residual.

The residual value therefore indicates the relative “goodness of fit” of the static force calibration.
