Replacing the Q5000 IR Lower Furnace Assembly

 

Weight changes in TGA are typically plotted versus sample temperature as measured by a thermocouple located close to the sample inside the lower furnace assembly.  This lower furnace assembly is exposed to sample off-gases and to contamination if the sample sputters or foams during decomposition. The lower furnace assembly will need to be replaced, if it becomes contaminated and cannot be cleaned.  Embrittlement due to long term cycling up to 1200Â°C will also require replacement.

To replace the sample lower furnace assembly:

  1. Close the furnace completely.

  2. Unscrew the knurled nut at the other end of the lower furnace assembly (shown in the figure below) to release it from the instrument cabinet.

  1. Disconnect the tube from the air cool inlet.

  1. Remove the lower furnace assembly by unscrewing the thumbscrew as shown in the figure to the right.

  2. Remove the lower furnace assembly from the furnace by pulling it straight down until the entire assembly is clear of the instrument.  DO NOT TWIST THE LOWER FURNACE.

  3. Locate the lower furnace assembly in the accessory kit.

  4. Carefully remove the lower furnace assembly from the plastic shipping tube.

  1. Orient the lower furnace assembly so that the air cool inlet points to the left as you slide it up into the bottom of the furnace. See the figure below for the parts of the lower furnace assembly. Raise the lower furnace assembly until it goes all the way inside until only the thumbscrew shows on the outside.  The O-ring will hold it in place.

  1. Turn the thumbscrew enough to hold the assembly in place.

  2. Loosen the outer nut and remove the furnace exhaust tube from the left-hand side of the furnace. See the figure below.

  1. Look into the furnace outlet port on the side of the instrument. The holes in the heat-absorbing tube should align with the inlet and outlet ports on the furnace housing allowing light to pass through. Rotate the lower furnace assembly until the proper alignment has been achieved.

  2. Fully tighten the thumbscrew.

  3. Replace the furnace exhaust tube.

  4. Insert the lower furnace assembly cable connector into the connector below and to the right of the furnace as shown below. Tighten the knurled nut.

  1. Connect the 1/8-inch Teflon TFE tubing from Port B on the front of the instrument to the Air Cool Inlet as shown in the figure to the right. (The figure also shows the sample purge line connected. See Connecting Purge Gas Lines for details.)
