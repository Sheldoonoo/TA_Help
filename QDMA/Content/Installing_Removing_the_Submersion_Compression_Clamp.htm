Installing/Removing the Submersion Compression Clamp

 

The procedures that follow explain the installation and removal of the submersion compression clamp (shown to the right), which is an optional DMA clamp fixture. The submersion compression clamp is used to test low to medium modulus samples while they are submerged in an ambient temperature fluid.

Installing the Clamp

To install the submersion compression clamp on the DMA instrument, follow these steps:

  1. Remove any existing clamp assembly as instructed in the help for that clamp.

  2. Select the appropriate clamp type and mode on the Summary Page.

  3. Press the CLAMP key to raise the drive shaft.

  4. Press the DRIVE Float/Lock key to lock the drive shaft in the upper position.

  5. Verify that the submersion sample thermocouple (extra long thermocouple) has been installed. If the extra long thermocouple is not currently installed, call TA Instruments Service to schedule the installation.

  6. Bend the reference thermocouple so that it is out of the way.

  7. Using the hex wrench, loosen the setscrew and slide the dovetail of the yoke into the dovetail holder on the drive shaft. Tighten the setscrew. (See the figure below.)

  1. Install the top bar and compression disk assembly onto the yoke. Leave the hex screws slightly loose. This assembly makes up the moveable clamp. See the figure below.

  1. Select the Submersion Compression clamp on the Summary Page.

  2. Perform the clamp mass calibration. Click here for more information Calibrating DMA Clamps.

  3. Press the CLAMP key to lower the drive shaft.

  4. Press the DRIVE Float/Lock key to lock the clamp.

  5. Remove the top bar and compression disk (moveable clamp).

  6. Angle the tank and slide it up under the bent sample thermocouple. Position the tank on the four mounting posts. Use a hex wrench to tighten the four captive screws. Make sure that the thermocouple is in the tank, but not touching the sides or bottom of the tank. See the figure to the right.

  7. Press the DRIVE Float/Lock key to release (float) the clamp.

  8. Replace the top bar and compression disk and use a hex wrench to tighten the two hex screws.

  9. Verify that the thermocouples do not interfere with the slide motion of the clamp. Adjust the thermocouples, if needed.

  10. Calibrate the clamp for clamp zero and clamp compliance. Click here for more information Calibrating DMA Clamps.

The clamp is now ready to be used. See the figure to the right for a view of the assembled clamp. For information on mounting samples, click here Mounting samples.

Removing the Clamp

If another sample is not to be tested, remove the clamp using these steps.

  1. Press the DRIVE Float/Lock key to lock the drive shaft.

  2. Use a hex wrench to loosen the screws in the top bar.

  3. Carefully remove the top bar with the compression disk connected and wipe excess fluid from the compression disk.  (It is important to wipe the fluid from the compression disk so that it will not interfere with subsequent tests.)

  4. Using a pipette or syringe to avoid spilling fluid on the instrument, remove the remaining fluid from fluid tank and remove the sample.

  5. Loosen the four screws and remove the tank from the mounting posts.

  6. Wipe out the inside of the tank to remove any remaining fluid.

  7. Loosen the dovetail screw and remove the yoke.

  8. Clean and dry the fluid tank and compression disk before storing them.
