Installing/Removing the Submersion Film/Fiber Clamp

 

The submersion film/fiber tension clamp requires the clamp mass calibration to be performed prior to complete installation of the clamp. The following instructions will outline the installation, calibration, sample loading (needed for calibration), and removal of the submersion film/fiber clamp.

Installing and Calibrating the Clamp

To install and calibrate the submersion film/fiber tension clamp on the DMA instrument, follow these steps:

  1. Remove any existing clamp assembly as instructed in the help for that clamp.

  2. Select the appropriate clamp type and mode on the Summary Page.

  3. Press the CLAMP key to raise the drive shaft.

  4. Press the DRIVE Float/Lock key to lock the drive shaft in the upper position.  If the extra long thermocouple is not currently installed, call TA Instruments Service to schedule the installation.

  5. Verify that the submersion sample thermocouple (extra long thermocouple) has been installed. If the extra long thermocouple is not currently installed, call TA Instruments Service to schedule the installation.

  6. Bend the reference thermocouple so that it is out of the way.

  7. Position the yoke with the flat side to the back. Using the hex wrench, loosen the setscrew and slide the dovetail of the yoke into the dovetail holder on the drive shaft. Tighten the setscrew. (See the figure to the right.)

  8. Use a hex wrench to loosely install the upper clamp's two captive hex screws. (See the figure below.)

Removing the Clamp

If another sample is not to be tested, follow these instructions to remove the clamp (see the installation instructions above for detailed figures):

  1. If a sample is still mounted, attach the Sample-Loading Assembly as follows:

    1. Press the DRIVE Float/Lock key on the instrument touch screen to release (float) the clamp.

    2. Attach the crossbar loading fixture.

    3. Press the DRIVE Float/Lock key on the instrument to lock the drive shaft.

  1. Use a syringe or pipette to remove the fluid from the fluid tank. Avoid spilling fluid on the instrument.  

  2. Loosen the two captive screws from the yoke and four captive screws from the tank enough to remove the Sample Loading Assembly as one piece. Carefully remove the Sample-Loading Assembly, letting excess fluid drip into the cup.  

  3. Wipe the inside of the tank dry to remove any remaining fluid.

  4. Loosen the two captive screws holding the tank to the stage enough to allow the tank to be angled out from under the bent thermocouple.

  5. Loosen the four hex screws on the mounting posts and remove the stage.

  6. Loosen the dovetail setscrew and remove the yoke.
