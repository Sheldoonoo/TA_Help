Message 984

Furnace impedance indicates that at least one bulb needs to be replaced in your furnace.  Please replace for optimal performance.  

Problem:

One or more of the halogen bulbs in your Q5000 IR instrument has burned out and needs to be replaced. Verify this by performing the Auto Diagnostic IR Bulb Check routine.

Solution:

If the error persists, follow the instructions below to replace the bulbs.

NOTE:  If you regularly perform tests at temperatures above 1000 °C, replace all four bulbs as soon as possible to prevent possible overheating of the remaining functioning bulbs.If your normal testing takes place at temperatures well below 1000 °C, replace the bulbs at your earliest opportunity.

Items Needed (IR Bulb Replacement Kit, PN 957367.901):

  • Hex (Allen) wrenches:  7/64-inch and 5/64-inch
  • Four (4) Halogen Double-Ended Quartz Bulbs 250W/120 volts
  • Gloves (You cannot touch the halogen bulbs with bare hands due to the oils in the skin.)

CAUTION:  Bulbs operate at very high temperatures and must be used properly to avoid the risk of fire. DO NOT touch the bulbs with your bare hands. Wear gloves when handling.

Procedure to Replace Bulbs

Follow these steps to replace the bulbs:

  1. Unload the pan from the furnace.
  2. Lower the furnace.
  3. Using the 7/64-inch hex wrench, loosen but do not remove the upper screw holding the furnace in place. See the figure to the right.
  4. Loosen the two lower screws holding the furnace. See the figure below.

Back to top

  1. Pull the furnace forward carefully, disconnecting the pins at the rear from the arm lift. Allow the furnace to rest on the cabinet in the angled out position as seen in the figure below.

Back to top

  1. Using a 5/64 hex wrench, remove the three screws on the left side of the furnace. See the figure above.
  2. Remove the screw on the rear of the furnace shown in the figure directly below. (The furnace has been removed from the instrument to show the screw.)

Back to top

  1. Remove the two screws on the right side of the furnace shown in the figure below.

Back to top

  1. Pull off the top of the furnace to reveal the inside as seen in the figure below.
  2. Loosen the lamp-holder screw indicated in the figure below with a Phillips screwdriver and remove the lamp holder.

Back to top

  1. Remove the 5/64-inch top shield shoulder screw and lift off the top shield from the lamp as shown in the figure to the right.
  2. Carefully remove the halogen bulb. Slide a new bulb in with the glass nub facing toward the center of the furnace as shown in the figure below. (The lamp can only be inserted in this manner.) DO NOT force it.

Back to top

  1. Replace the top shield and reinstall the shoulder screw. Make sure the shoulder passes through the hole in the top shield and that the top shield is not pressed against the surface; the shield should float loosely.
  2. Place the lamp holder in place over the new bulb and tighten the screw. The spring-loaded lamp should move freely up and down and the contact should be centered on the lamp contact.
  3. Repeat steps 10 through 14 for the remaining halogen bulbs.
  4. Place the furnace top cover back on and perform steps 7 and 6 (in that order) to reinstall the six screws holding on the furnace top cover.
  5. Make sure that the furnace cleats are oriented with the flat side toward the opening in the center as shown in the figure to the right.
  6. Align the furnace connector with the furnace lift connector then plug in the connectors.
  7. Hold the furnace in position while the three screws with cleats are secured again using the 7/64-inch hex wrench. See steps 3 and 4 for details.
  8. Run the Auto Diagnostics test to check the bulbs for proper installation.
  9. The procedure is now complete.
