Choosing a Cup Type

 

TA Instruments offers two types of SDT cups for use in your experiments. Use the following guidelines to select a sample cup material that meets the requirements of your experiment.

Sample Cup Sizes

  • alumina ceramic 40 µL,  90 µL (recommended for DSC-TGA studies)

  • platinum 40 µL, 110 µL (recommended for TGA-DTA studies)

General Guidelines:

  • For DSC-TGA experiments, the ceramic (alumina) 90 µL cups must be used. These cups are compatible (i.e., do not affect the results) with most high temperature materials. In addition, the sapphire DSC heat flow calibration standard has been specifically cut to fit the 90 µL cups.

  • For TGA-DTA experiments, the ceramic (40 µL or 90 µL) or platinum (40 µL or 110 µL) cups are acceptable alternatives. Platinum is generally preferred for TGA-DTA work because it is easy to clean and does not react with most organics and polymers. Ceramic is a better choice for inorganics or corrosive materials.

  • Platinum and ceramic cups are reusable.  To clean cups between experiments, use a Bunsen burner or a propane torch, or run the cup through a hot thermal program in the SDT to burn out any residue.

  • Use the same type and size cup for both your reference and sample during experiments.

WARNING: If platinum cups are used at high temperatures (i.e., above 1300°C), they can stick to the platinum liner in the sample and reference platforms, which can cause damage to the liner when the cup is removed.  This may result in the need to replace the sample and reference beam assembly. To prevent this from occurring, you can (1) use a ceramic cup for high temperature work, or (2) place some fine grain alumina powder between the platinum cup and liner before you start the experiment.

NOTE:  Alumina absorbs moisture. Before using alumina, you will need to dry it. Store alumina powder in a desiccator.

NOTE:  Always use tweezers to handle SDT cups.
