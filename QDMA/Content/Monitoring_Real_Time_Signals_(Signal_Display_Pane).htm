Monitoring Real-Time Signals

 

Real-time instrument signals may be monitored from either the instrument touch screen or from the controller Signal Display pane. If you choose to use the touch screen, you can access either the Status or Display options. See also: Display Menu Touch Screen.

Controller Signal Display Pane

The Signal Display pane (shown to the right) is a customizable instrument control window that allows the user to select the real time signals they want displayed during an experiment. These same signals are also available for plotting in the RealTime Plot window.

See also:  Overview of the Instrument Control Window

 

Signal Display Pop-up Menu

The TA Instruments DMA displays a certain set of information on the Signal Display window. Using the pop-up menu (accessed by right clicking), you can customize the signal display by selecting Customize Signal Display.  When the Customize Signal Display window appears, select or deselect the signals then click OK to close the signal selection window and display the new signals. You can reorganize the order of the signals by clicking on a signal name to display a drop-down list of signals to select from.

DMA Instrument Signals

Listed below are all of the possible real-time DMA signals. The available signals are dependent on instrument mode and options installed. These signals are available for display within the Signal Display pane, as RealTime plot signals, and they are displayed on the instrument touch screen, if available. A subset of these signals are available as stored signals within the data file.

Back to top

For a detailed explanation of the signals shown on the window, see the table below:

 

Signal

Description

Amplitude

The measured amplitude of the sample's oscillation.

Creep Compliance, µm2/N or 1/Pa

The ratio of time-dependent strain to stress in the creep phase of a creep recovery test.

Damping

Equivalent to "stiffness," but calculated on the 90 degree out-of-phase (viscous) component of the sample response rather than the in-phase (elastic) response.

Decay Time

The time in a creep or stress relaxation experiment over which the relaxation of creep or stress is measured.

Displacement

Displacement is the distance that the moveable clamp has traveled with respect to a defined zero position(usually the position at the beginning of the experiment). In DMA multifrequency and multistrain modes, the clamp displacement is averaged over each oscillation period.

Drive Force

The drive force signal is the amount of oscillating force, in Newtons, currently being produced in the drive coil.

Force

The force signal is the amount of oscillating force, in Newtons, currently being exerted by the clamp on the sample.

Frequency

The frequency, in Hz, that the instrument is currently applying to the sample.

GCA Cooling

The percentage of the maximum cooling that the GCA can provide.

GCA Liquid Level

The level, in percent, of the liquid nitrogen remaining in the tank.

GCA Pressure

The internal pressure of the Gas Cooling Accessory.

Heater Power, Watts

The amount of electrical power being applied to the heater.

Length

Back to top

Distance between the fixed and moveable clamp grip areas.

Loss Modulus, MPa

 

The calculated ability of a material to dissipate mechanical energy by converting it into heat.

Method Time, min

The total time that the current method has been running.

Oscillation Amplitude, mm

The sample dynamic amplitude

Position, mm

 

The current position of the drive shaft (slide).

Recovery Compliance

The ratio of time-dependent strain to stress in the recovery phase of a creep recovery test.

Relaxation Modulus, Pa

 

Also called stress relaxation modulus, this is the ratio of time-dependent stress to strain in a stress relaxation experiment.

Remaining Run Time, min

The estimated total time remaining until completion of experiment.

Segment Time, min

The total time that the current segment has been running.

Set Point Temperature, °C

The temperature that the DMA furnace is requested to attain.

Static Force, N

The amount of constant force, in Newtons, exerted by the DMA clamp on the sample.

Stiffness

 

Back to top

In DMA, the sample stiffness is defined as the force applied to the sample divided by the amplitude of deformation. The stiffness of a material is dependent on its geometry (physical dimensions).

Storage Modulus, MPa

The calculated ability of a material to store mechanical energy as determined by its stiffness or rigidity.

Strain, %

The measurement of deformation, relative to a reference configuration of length, area, or volume. Also called relative deformation.

Strain Recovery, %

The percentage of strain recovered in the recovery phase of a creep recovery test.

Stress, Pa

A force per unit area.

Tan Delta

In DMA, Tan Delta is the ratio of loss modulus to storage modulus (E''/E' or G''/G'). Tan Delta is a useful index of material viscoelasticity since it is a ratio of viscous and elastic moduli.

Temperature, °C

The current temperature of the sample.
