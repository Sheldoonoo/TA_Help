Displaying the DMA Waveform

 

Right click inside the RealTime Plot pane and select Show DMA Waveform from the pop-up menu to access this feature. (To return to the standard RealTime Plot, right click inside the RealTime Plot pan and uncheck Show DMA Waveform from the pop-up menu.)

DMA Waveform provides a “virtual oscilloscope” to allow operators to monitor the stress (force) and strain (displacement) waveforms as they are being generated during oscillation experiment. This feature can be used to monitor the quality of the data being generated, and to look for signs of distortion in the sine waves that may indicate non-linear behavior in the sample or clipping, which could indicate loss of contact between sample and tensioning clamp. This feature is only available for DMA instruments equipped with Platinum functionality.

The DMA Waveform is applicable to the following Multifrequency Tests:

  • Setting Up a Controlled Strain Isothermal Temperature/Frequency Sweep Experiment

  • Setting Up a Controlled Strain Temperature Step/Frequency Sweep Experiment

  • Setting Up a Controller Strain Temperature Ramp/Frequency Sweep Experiment

  • Setting Up a Controlled Stress Isothermal Temperature/Frequency Sweep Experiment

  • Setting Up a Controlled Stress Temperature Step/Frequency Sweep Experiment

  • Setting Up a Controller Stress Temperature Ramp/Frequency Sweep Experiment
