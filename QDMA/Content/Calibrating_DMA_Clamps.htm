Calibrating DMA Clamps

 

Select Calibrate/Clamp to access the Clamp Calibration functions when you install a clamp for the first time or change from one clamp to another on the DMA. The following steps are performed for total clamp calibration, depending upon the clamp used:

NOTE:  If you change clamps, the geometry will be reset to UNKNOWN. You must then select a sample geometry (shape) on the Summary Page.

  • Calibration Wizard Introduction

  • Step 1 Clamp Mass

  • Step 2 Clamp Zero

(Without Offset Gauge) For film or fiber tension clamp, penetration clamp, or  compression clamp

(With Offset Gauge) For film or fiber tension clamp, penetration clamp, or  compression clamp

  • Step 3 Clamp Compliance

(With Steel Sample) For single or dual cantilever clamp, 3-point bending clamp, or a film tension clamp using a rigid precision steel sample with known dimensions

(Without Steel Sample) For penetration or compression clamps

(With Piano Wire) For fiber tension clamps (optional)

(With Steel Flat Washer) For the submersion compression clamps

  • Step 4 Calibration Report

See also:  DMA Clamp Compliance Values

Calibration Wizard Introduction

Follow these steps:

NOTE: For Fiber Tension Clamps:  If you wish to perform clamp compliance calibration, you will need to enable that feature by selecting it on the Tools/Instrument Preferences/DMA Page before using the Calibration Wizard as directed below.

  1. Select Calibrate/Clamp from the menu. The opening window of the DMA Clamp Calibration Wizard will be displayed.

  2. Select the appropriate clamp type from the drop-down list.

  3. Install (mount) the desired clamp.

  4. Select the desired type of clamp calibration that you want to perform from the drop-down list.

  5. Click the Next button to proceed. You will then perform the clamp calibration, following the instructions displayed. The steps will lead you through the clamp mass, clamp zero, or compliance calibration procedures, depending on the clamp and calibration type(s) you have selected.

Clamp Calibration - Step 1 (Clamp Mass)

Clamp Mass calibration is performed to allow the instrument to compensate for the mass of a specific clamp.  It is the first type of calibration to be performed when you either install a clamp for the very first time, or change from one clamp to another.

You can run a check to see if your clamp mass needs calibration by performing a small test.  Press the DRIVE key on the touch screen to release (float) the clamp.  Manually position the clamp in the middle of its range of travel and release it.  The clamp should maintain its position or slowly drift up or down.  If the clamp rapidly sinks or rises, the clamp mass requires calibration as described below:

  1. Clear the clamp area on the instrument. (Do not mount a sample.) If you are using a submersion type clamp, skip to step 3.

  2. Press the FURNACE key to close the DMA furnace. (Do not close the furnace when submersion type clamp is installed.)

  3. Press the Calibrate button to begin the clamp mass calibration step. The furnace will open when the clamp mass calibration has completed.  (It normally takes approximately five minutes to complete the clamp mass calibration.)

  4. Select the Next button to proceed when the calibration step is complete.

NOTE:  If the clamp continues to rapidly sink or rise instead of floating, after the clamp mass has been calibrated, you will need to perform a position calibration.

Technical Explanation of Clamp Mass Calibration

In non-oscillating modes it is necessary to know the clamp weight to apply the correct static force to the sample.  In oscillating modes it is necessary to know the clamp mass to adjust the slide/movable clamp mass part of the DMA dynamic model when the clamp is changed.  Both of these calibrations are taken care of by comparing the static force required to float the slide with the clamp installed versus the static force required to float the slide alone.  Only the movable part of the clamp figures into this calibration as the assumption is made that the fixed part of the clamp is small (from a mass standpoint) compared to the mass of the frame.

Clamp Calibration - Step 2 (Clamp Zero)

Clamp Zero calibration is needed to determine the point of zero sample length for automated sample length (tension) or thickness (compression and penetration) measurement.  You will need to perform a clamp zero calibration when you install a film or fiber tension clamp, a penetration clamp, or a compression clamp.  You do not need to use it when you install a single or dual cantilever clamp, a shear sandwich clamp, or a 3-point bending clamp (step 2 is not performed).

NOTE:  You can also perform the clamp zero calibration by pressing the ZERO CLAMP key on the instrument touch screen.

  1. Clear the clamp area on the instrument. (Do not mount a sample.) If you are using a submersion type clamp, skip to step 3.

  2. Press the FURNACE key to close the DMA furnace. (Do not close the furnace when submersion type clamp is installed.)

  3. Press the Calibrate button to begin the clamp zero calibration. The clamp faces will be pressed together to determine the zero length point.

  4. Select the Next button to proceed when the calibration step is complete.

Clamp Calibration - Step 2 (Clamp Zero with Offset Gauge)

Clamp Zero calibration is needed to determine the point of zero sample length for automated sample length (tension) or thickness (compression and penetration) measurement.  You will need to perform a clamp zero calibration when you install a film or fiber tension clamp, a penetration clamp, or a compression clamp.  You donot need to use it when you install a single or dual cantilever clamp, a shear sandwich clamp, or a 3-point bending clamp (step 2 is not performed).

NOTE:  You can also perform the clamp zero calibration by pressing the ZERO CLAMP key on the instrument touch screen.

For Film/Fiber Tension Clamps:

To zero the clamp for a film or fiber tension type of clamp, follow these steps:

  1. Measure the offset gauge, found in the accessory kit, with calipers to determine its length.

  2. Enter the length of the offset gauge (range of 5 to 30 mm) in the appropriate field.

  3. Mount the offset gauge in the fixed clamp. If you are using a submersion type clamp, skip to step 4.

  4. Press the FURNACE key to close the DMA furnace. (Do not close the furnace when a submersion type clamp is installed.)

  5. Press the Calibrate button to begin the calibration step. The zero length point for the sample is determined to be the underside of the fixed clamp. The furnace will open when the clamp zero calibration has been completed.  

  6. Remove the offset gauge when the furnace opens.

  7. Press Next button to proceed to the next window.

For Compression or Penetration Clamps:

  1. If your clamp needs a gauge block, enter its size in the gauge block length field. (These clamps normally do not use a gauge block, so this entry can be left unchanged at 0.0.) If you are using a submersion type clamp, skip to step 3.

  2. Press the FURNACE key to close the DMA furnace. (Do not close the furnace when a submersion type clamp is installed.)

  3. Select Calibrate. The clamp faces will be pressed together to determine the zero length point. The furnace will open when the clamp zero calibration has completed.

  4. Press Next to proceed to the next window.

Technical Explanation of Clamp Zero Calibration

Clamp offset calibration relates slide position to sample length.  The calibration is performed by measuring position for a known sample length.  Both the fixed and movable parts of the clamp figure into this calibration.

Calibrate Clamp Compliance - Step 3 (with Sample)

Compliance calibration is used to measure the flexibility of a clamp and calibrates the instrument to that flexibility.  

  • Compliance calibration is performed when you install a single or dual cantilever clamp, 3-point bending clamp, or a film tension clamp using a rigid precision steel sample with known dimensions.

  • It is also performed without a sample when you install a penetration clamp or compression clamp.

  • Clamp compliance calibration, which uses a piano wire sample, is optional for the fiber tension clamp.

  • Clamp compliance calibration, which uses a steel washer, is performed when you install a submersion compression clamp.

  • Compliance calibration is not performed when you install a shear sandwich (i.e., step 3 of clamp calibration is not displayed).

The procedure used for compliance calibration is slightly different depending on the type of clamp installed.

For Cantilever, Film Tension, or 3-Point Bending Clamps:

To calibrate the compliance for a cantilever, film tension, or 3-point bending type of clamp, follow these steps:

  1. Measure the precision steel compliance sample, provided in the accessory kit, with calipers to determine its thickness and width.

  2. Enter the compliance sample dimensions (in this order - length, width, and thickness). Note:  For Dual cantilever clamps use length = 35 mm.

  3. Mount the compliance sample and measure the sample length. Use a torque of 8 to 10 in-lbs for dual/single cantilever clamps. Use a torque of 2 to 3 in-lbs for film tension clamps when mounting the sample. If you are using a submersion type clamp, skip to step 5.

  4. Press the FURNACE key to close the DMA furnace. (Do not close the furnace when a submersion type clamp is installed.)

  5. Select the Calibrate button. The furnace will open when the compliance calibration has completed.

  6. Press Next to proceed to the next window.

For Fiber Tension Clamps:

To calibrate the compliance for a fiber tension type of clamp, follow these steps:

  1. Make sure the sample shape is set to "Cylindrical" on the Experiment View/Summary Page.

  2. Enable the fiber tension clamp compliance option located on the Tools/Instrument Preferences/DMA Page. If this option is not enabled, then a default compliance factor will be used.

  3. Using a caliper, measure the length and diameters of the precision piano wire sample, provided in the accessory kit.

  4. Enter the compliance sample dimensions (in this order - length, diameter).

  5. Mount the compliance sample and measure the sample length.

  6. Press the FURNACE key to close the DMA furnace.

  7. Select the Calibrate button. The furnace will open when the compliance calibration has completed.

  8. Press Next to proceed to the next window.

For Submersion Compression Clamps:

To calibrate the compliance for a submersion compression type of clamp, follow these steps:

  1. Place the compliance sample (0.037-inch flat steel washer) in the center of the fluid tank.

  2. Select the Calibrate button to start the clamp compliance.

  3. Press Next to proceed to the next window.

Clamp Calibration Compliance - Step 3 (without Steel Sample)

Compliance calibration is used to measure the flexibility of a clamp and calibrates the instrument to that flexibility.  Compliance calibration is performed when you install a single or dual cantilever clamp, 3-point bending clamp, or a film tension clamp using a rigid precision steel sample with known dimensions. It is also performed without a sample when you install a penetration, or compression clamp as described here. Compliance calibration is not performed when you install a shear sandwich or fiber tension clamp (e.g., step 3 of clamp calibration is not displayed).

The procedure used for compliance calibration is slightly different depending on the type of clamp installed.

For Compression or Penetration Clamps:

  1. Press the FURNACE key to close the DMA furnace.

  2. Select the Calibrate button to calibrate the clamp compliance.

  3. Press Next to proceed to the next window.

Technical Explanation of Clamp Compliance Calibration

The slide/movable clamp are one body and the frame/fixed clamp are another body.  When the clamp is changed it is necessary to update the stiffness terms for these two bodies to reflect the stiffness of the clamp assembly currently installed.

This calibration is performed as follows:

  1. A 10 Newton force is applied downwards and the compliance of the clamp/sample combination is measured.

  2. The force is reversed and the clamp/sample compliance is measured again

  3. The compliance (inverse of stiffness) of the sample is calculated based on the operator-entered dimensions and the known modulus of steel.  Note that this calculation is extremely sensitive to sample thickness for bending clamps, so an accurate measurement of thickness is critical.

  4. The compliance of the clamp is set to the average of the two measurements minus the calculated compliance of the sample.

Clamp Calibration - Step 4 (Report)

After you have completed the clamp calibration procedures, the results are put into a report. Use the Calibration Report option to view or print this report.

Select OK when you have finished reviewing the report.
