Preparing the SDT for Moving

 

When you want to move the SDT Q600 to another location, the steps you take to protect the instrument depend on how much handling will occur. Basically we have divided these instructions into two types of movesâ€”room-to-room or building-to-building. Follow the instructions based on the type of move involved.

Moving the SDT from Room to Room

CAUTION:  When packing the balance, follow these procedures carefully to prevent damage to the two balance beams.

Some mechanical parts must be locked in position in order to protect the inner mechanisms when you place the instrument on a cart and move it to another room. Follow these procedures to pack and prepare your instrument for moving:

  1. Using both hands, grasp the balance dress cover, and work it back and forth slightly as you pull it up. When you have enough clearance, place your fingers under the cover, as shown in the figure to the right. Then pull out on the right bottom edge of the dress cover to clear the balance housing lid as you slide the dress cover up and off.

  2. Use a 3/32-inch hex wrench to remove the six (6) screws holding the balance housing lid in place.

  3. Place one hand on each side of the balance housing lid, and carefully lift the right side of the lid, as shown in the figure below.

Back to top

  1. Pivot the right side of the lid upwards first to clear the inner mechanisms of the balance assembly. Then lift up the left side of the lid, and remove the lid entirely.

  2. Locate the two screws on the front (sample) beam stop that are holding the tare beam. Refer to the figure to the right.

  3. Using a 3/32-inch hex wrench, turn the inner screw clockwise a few turns to lower it onto the tare beam.

  4. Turn the outer screw clockwise to tighten the beam stop assembly. This screw should be tightened until the tare beam has no movement in either direction.

  5. Repeat steps 5 through 7 for the back (reference) beam stop.

  6. Replace the balance housing lid, and reinstall the six (6) screws to hold it in place.

  7. Replace the balance dress cover.

  8. Close the furnace by selecting the SHIP key on the touch screen. A verification message is displayed to confirm this selection. This prevents accidental damage to the beams. Select Yes to override this safety feature and allow the furnace to be closed when the beams are locked down.

  9. Turn the power off.

  10. Unplug the instrument and disconnect the cables. The instrument is ready to be moved to another room.

Moving the SDT from Building to Building

CAUTION:  When packing the balance, follow these procedures carefully to prevent damage to the two balance beams.

Some mechanical parts must be locked in position in order to protect the inner mechanisms. Follow these procedures to pack and prepare your instrument for moving to another building either by cart or vehicle:

  1. Using both hands, grasp the balance dress cover, and work it back and forth slightly as you pull it up. When you have enough clearance, place your fingers under the cover, as shown in the figure to the right. Then pull out on the right bottom edge of the dress cover to clear the balance housing lid as you slide the dress cover up and off.

  2. Use a 3/32-inch hex wrench to remove the six (6) screws holding the balance housing lid in place.

  3. Place one hand on each side of the balance housing lid, and carefully lift the right side of the lid, as shown in the figure below.

Back to top

  1. Pivot the right side of the lid upwards first to clear the inner mechanisms of the balance assembly. Then lift up the left side of the lid, and remove the lid entirely.

  2. Locate the two screws on the front (sample) beam stop that are holding the tare beam. Refer to the figure to the right.

  3. Using a 3/32-inch hex wrench, turn the inner screw clockwise a few turns to lower it onto the tare beam.

  4. Turn the outer screw clockwise to tighten the beam stop assembly. This screw should be tightened until the tare beam has no movement in either direction.

  5. Repeat steps 5 through 7 for the back (reference) beam stop.

  6. Turn on the SDT power switch.

  7. Press the FURNACE key on the instrument touch screen to start the furnace in motion. Stop the furnace by pressing the FURNACE button again (or using the STOP button) when the furnace has opened 5 to 8 cm (2 to 3 inches). This allows enough room to place the shipping foam around the balance beams.

  8. Carefully grasp the tops of the foam with both hands, as shown in the figure to the right, and separate the two top portions of the Y-shaped foam.

  9. Push the back portion of the foam gently under the beams, and position the foam very carefully around the beams to avoid breaking the sample and reference beams as shown in the figure below.

Back to top

  1. Replace the balance housing lid and reinstall the six (6) screws to hold it in place.

  2. Replace the balance dress cover.

  3. Close the furnace by selecting the SHIP key on the touch screen. A verification message is displayed to confirm this selection. This prevents accidental damage to the beams. Select Yes to override this safety feature and allow the furnace to be closed when the beams are locked down.

  4. Unhook the end of the rubber protective cover (bellows) from the metal plate on the left side of the instrument and push it across to the right.

  5. Use a flathead screwdriver to completely remove two (2) of the screws and their washers on the left side of the furnace and use a 10/32 hex wrench to remove the hex screw in the slide rail.

  6. Locate the shipping bracket (removed when the instrument was unpacked) and position it on the left side of the furnace (see the figure below). This bracket is used to hold the furnace in place during shipping.

  1. Install the bracket replacing the two screws and washers in the same holes on the top of the shipping bracket and place the hex screw in the bottom slot into the slide rail.

  2. Turn the power off.

  3. Unplug the instrument and disconnect the cables. The instrument is ready to be relocated to another building.
