Sample Tensile Stress Equation

Sample tensile stress is calculated using the following equation:

ST = F / A

where:

ST  =   stress at temperature T (Pa)

 F   =   force (N)

 A   =   cross-sectional area (m2).

 

The film/fiber accessory kit contains:

  • Film/fiber probe
  • Film/fiber stage
  • Vial of cleaved aluminum balls
  • Stainless steel clamps
  • Clamp block
  • Screwdriver
  • Auto Measure gauge.

Operating Procedures

Film or fiber samples, held in tension during operation, are generally longer than expansion or penetration samples; therefore, the small temperature gradient normally present in the furnace is more significant. A faster heating rate serves to accent this gradient. For the best reproducibility, when creating a method, use a Ramp segment of 5 °C/min or less for your heating rate. (See the instrument control software online documentation for details.)

When comparing run data, make sure that all comparison scans are made with the thermocouple in the same position and at the same heating rate.

Installing the Probe and Stage

Because the configuration of the film/fiber accessory is different from that of the other types of probes, you need to perform the following general steps to install the special film/fiber probe and stage.

Removing the Existing Probe

NOTE:  This procedure is the same for the TMA Q400 and TMA 2940 Film/Fiber probes.

  1. Raise the furnace, and rotate it clockwise to move it off to the side.
  2. Grasp the top of the probe with one hand, and locate the probe-locking lever (behind the door that covers the weight tray) with your other hand.
  3. Unscrew the locking lever by turning it counterclockwise approximately one turn.
  4. Lift the probe gently, and twist slightly to aid its removal from the stage opening.

Back to top

Removing the Stage

NOTE:  This procedure is the same for the TMA Q400 and TMA 2940 Film/Fiber probes.

  1. Remove the stage shield from the base of the stage by lifting it straight up. (This is a friction fit.)
  2. Take off the spring clip that holds the sample thermocouple in place.
  3. Move the thermocouple off to the side of the stage.
  4. Turn the stage nut counterclockwise to remove it.
  5. Twist the stage retainer ring (with key slots) counterclockwise, pull it up off the three posts, and slide it up off the stage.
  6. Take the wave washer up off the stage flange and remove it.
  7. Remove the stage from the stage opening.

Changing the Selected Probe

NOTE:  This procedure is the same for the TMA Q400 and TMA 2940 Film/Fiber probes.

The film/fiber probe should be specified on the Summary Page prior to installation to prevent damage to the probe currently installed.

Installing the Film/Fiber Probe

NOTE:  This procedure is the same for the TMA Q400 and TMA 2940 Film/Fiber probes.

The special configuration of the film/fiber accessory requires the installation of the probe before the stage.

  1. Insert the core end of the probe carefully into the opening in the top plate.
  2. Open the weight tray door, and locate the probe-locking lever.
  3. Loosen the probe-locking lever and hold it in the up position.  Continue lowering the probe until you can feel it exerting downward pressure on the locking lever.
  4. Align the slot in the top of the probe front to back, and tighten the probe-locking lever by turning it clockwise.
  5. Close the weight tray door.

Installing the Film/Fiber Stage

NOTE:  This procedure is the same for the TMA Q400 and TMA 2940 Film/Fiber probes.

  1. Lower the film/fiber stage down carefully over the probe onto the base.
  2. Place the wave washer over the top of the stage and probe assembly, and lower it onto the flange.
  3. Slide the stage retainer ring carefully over the top of the stage and probe assembly so that it rests on top of the wave washer. Push down and turn clockwise to tighten the retainer ring.
  4. Carefully align the stage slot with the slot in the top of the probe, and position the stage opening to the front as seen in the figure to the right (QTMA probe shown).
  5. Replace the large stage nut, and turn it clockwise to insert it.
  6. Attach the thermocouple to the stage as follows:
  1. Position the tip of the thermocouple at approximately one half the length of the samples you will be measuring. The tip should be angled toward the sample location.

NOTE:  After the thermocouple is installed, its tip may interfere with the zero length operation. Temporarily move the thermocouple when performing this operation.

  1. Hold the thermocouple against the stage assembly, and put on the spring clip to hold the thermocouple in place.
  1. Replace the stage shield on the base of the stage.
  2. Move the furnace back into position and lower it.

Calculating the Offset

When you use the film/fiber accessory, analysis must compensate for the thickness of the stage platform and the upper portion of the probe. Perform the “Auto zero offset” procedure to compensate for the thickness. (See the instrument control software online documentation for further instructions on performing this operation.)

NOTE:  This procedure is the same for the TMA Q400 and TMA 2940 Film/Fiber probes.

The Auto Measure Offset does not need to be changed unless you:

  • Reset the instrument parameters
  • Use a new film/fiber probe and stage assembly.

Change the Auto Measure Offset as follows:

  1. Record the value listed as Auto Measure Offset from the TA controller.
  2. Use calipers to measure the gap in the Auto Measure Offset Gauge, and record this value as the Actual Size.
  3. Open the probe and insert the gauge as shown in the figure to the right.
  4. Press the Measure Length key on the touch screen. Record this value as the Displayed Size.
  5. Calculate the new offset using the following equation:

Offset new = (Actual Size – Displayed Size) + Offset current

  1. Enter the new Auto Measure Offset using the instrument control functions.
  2. Remove the Auto Measure Gauge, and proceed with your experiment.

Film/Fiber Probe Calibration

NOTE:  This procedure is the same for the TMA Q400 and TMA 2940 Film/Fiber probes.

You should perform probe, temperature, and cell constant calibration for the film/fiber accessory as you would normally calibrate any of the standard probes. Metal wires crimped in the aluminum balls should be used for temperature calibration. High-quality aluminum foil clamped in the steel clamps should be used for cell constant calibration. See Calibrating the TMA for details.

Preparing Film and Fiber Samples

The method used to prepare and load your sample varies according to the sample type. Two methods available are for the film/fiber accessory:  aluminum balls and stainless steel clamps. See the appropriate section below.

Regardless of the method chosen, use the following guidelines when preparing and loading samples:

  • Maintain a minimum length to width ratio of at least 16:1
  • Use a dynamic force of 0.05 to 0.2N, recommend starting with 0.1N
  • Use a static force of 0.2 to 0.4N
  • Use a frequency of 0.05 to 0.5Hz, recommend starting with 0.5Hz

Using the Aluminum Balls

NOTE:  This procedure is the same for the TMA Q400 and TMA 2940 Film/Fiber probes.

The aluminum balls are generally used with fiber samples as follows:

  1. Measure and cut the fiber sample slightly longer than desired length, typically 12 to 25 mm (0.5 to 1.0 inch).
  2. Place two cleaved aluminum balls 12 to 25 mm (0.5 to 1.0 inch) apart.

  1. Lay the fiber across the balls, and press the fiber into the cleavage of each ball as shown above.
  2. Crimp the two balls closed. The balls should not shift or slip when tested by hand.
  3. Cut off any excess fiber extending beyond the aluminum balls.
  4. Place one ball over the slot in the stage with the fiber hanging down through the slot, and allow the other ball to hang freely.
  5. Position the second ball below the slot in the film/fiber probe so that the fiber is suspended between the two balls, as shown the figure to the right (TMA 2940 probe shown here).
  6. Measure the sample length as follows:  Before you begin the experiment, it is important to take an initial measurement of the sample. To do this, simply press the MEASURE key on the instrument touch screen or select the button on the instrument control tool bar.
  7. If the TMA is operating in any of the enhanced modes (any mode other than standard and calibration) enter the additional sample shape dimensions. See: Selecting a TMA Operating Mode.
  8. Position the thermocouple close to, but not touching, the sample.

Using the Stainless Steel Clamps - TMA Q400 Probe

The stainless steel clamps may be used with either film or fiber samples as follows:

  1. Measure and cut the sample to the desired length, typically 12 to 25 mm (0.5 to 1.0 in). (Films should have a uniform width.)
  2. Separate the jaws of the upper clamp (the clamp with two tabs).
  3. Place the upper clamp into the sample block so that the clamp fits into the notch on one end. See the figure to the right.
  4. Lay the film across the clamp, position the other jaw across the sample, place the screws into their openings and tighten the screws uniformly.
  5. Lift up the free end of the film, and slide the lower clamp onto the end, positioning the clamp ridge toward the center of the sample.
  6. Move the clamp into the desired notch position (for either a 8-, 16-, or 24-mm sample).
  7. Tighten the screws uniformly so that the sample is flat in the block and is positioned perpendicular to the clamps.
  8. Remove the clamps and sample from the block, and trim off the excess material.
  9. Place one clamp on edge over the slot in the film/fiber stage and slide the tabs into the slot so that the film hangs down into the slot.
  10. Position the second clamp below the slot in the film/fiber probe so that the film is suspended between the two clamps. Slide the clamp into the probe slot so that the lower clamp ridge is facing up, as shown in the figure to the right.
  11. Measure the sample length as follows:  Before you begin the experiment, it is important to take an initial measurement of the sample. To do this, simply press the MEASURE key on the instrument touch screen or select the button on the instrument control tool bar.
  12. If the TMA is operating in any of the enhanced modes (any mode other than standard and calibration) enter the additional sample shape dimensions. See: Selecting a TMA Operating Mode.
    • Standard Mode:  The TMA Q400 stainless steel film/fiber clamp can accommodate samples up to 0.5 mm in thickness in the Standard mode using the standard clamp screws. For samples with thicknesses between 0.5 and 1.0 mm, install the longer clamp screws to accommodate the thicker sample.
