Making the SDT Y-Adjustments

 

To enhance the DTA baseline between ambient and approximately 500°C, you may find it necessary to adjust the y-axis setscrew. The setscrew should be moved if the DTA signal changes direction twice before reaching a peak at approximately 500 to 800°C. In other words, the DTA signal must approach 500°C in one direction. The effect of the setscrew on delta (D) T is approximately 1°C per turn of the setscrew. Use the following guidelines to help you determine which way to turn the setscrew:

  • If the DT has an initial positive slope, then reverses to a negative slope at 500°C, turn the setscrew clockwise.

  • If the DT has an initial negative slope, then reverses to a positive slope at 500°C, turn the setscrew counterclockwise.

Perform the procedures described below to adjust the y-axis setscrew on the SDT.

  1. Use a flathead screwdriver to remove the y-adjustment sealing screw located on the balance assembly just below the arm rest (see figure below). This leaves the y-adjustment slot uncovered.

  1. Remove the balance dress cover, then remove the six screws holding the balance housing lid using a 3/32 Allen wrench. Follow the procedures described in "Unpacking the Balance" to remove the lid.

  2. Loosen the two shoulder screws on the meter mount baseplate using a 3/32 Allen wrench (see the figure below for location of the shoulder screws).

  3. Use a .050 Allen wrench on the y-adjustment Allen screw, and turn the wrench either clockwise to move the meter movement back or counterclockwise to move the meter movement towards the front.

  4. Retighten the shoulder screws.

  5. Replace the balance housing lid and the six screws that hold it in place.

  6. Run a DTA-specific experiment, and check the baseline for acceptability.

  7. Replace the y-adjustment sealing screw and the balance dress cover when the procedure is completed.
