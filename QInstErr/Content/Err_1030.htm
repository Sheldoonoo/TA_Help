Message 1030

GCA tank empty. Cannot start GCA autodiagnostic routine.

Problem:

The GCA tank must be filled with liquid nitrogen before it can be used for cooling experiments with the DMA, and it has registered as empty. The Autodiagnostic routine cannot be run until it is full.

 

Solution:

There are two methods, listed below, that you can use to fill the GCA, depending on your laboratory setup. For more information refer to the QDMA online help.

  • If you have the available space and are able to keep a bulk storage reservoir near the instrument, you can use the autofill feature.  This allows you to automatically refill the GCA with liquid nitrogen from your bulk storage reservoir, when the GCA is not actively cooling.
  • If you must take the GCA to the bulk storage reservoir for refilling, you will need to use the manual method to fill the GCA tank with liquid nitrogen.
