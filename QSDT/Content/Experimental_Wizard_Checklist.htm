Experimental Wizard Checklist

 

Additional comments on proper experimental setup.

Purge Gas:

The SDT is operated with a single purge gas flow.  Since this purge gas flows through the balance housing before entering the furnace/sample area, the choice of purge gas in limited to gases which are not “corrosive,” including nitrogen, argon, helium, air and oxygen.  The typical purge gas flow rate is 100 cc/minute.  (An initial rate of up to 1000 cc/minute may be used to prepurge the system before starting the experiment.)  The purge gas can be switched during the experiment (mostly commonly from nitrogen or other inert gas to oxygen or other “reactive” gas).

Taring:

The sample cup should be tared before the start of each experiment.  Taring ensures that the measured weight reflects only the sample weight.  Place two clean, empty matching cups on the beam sensors.   Click the Tare button.  You may also tare by pressing the TARE button located on the instrument touch screen.  It is not necessary to remove the inert reference material contained in the reference cup, if used, before taring.

Sample Loading:

The optimum sample size depends on the magnitude of the transitions being investigated:

  • 2 to 20 mg samples are used for DSC-TGA experiments

  • 20 to 30 mg samples are used for TGA-DTA experiments.  

Types of Cups to Use:

  • The 90mL alumina cups should be used for DSC-TGA studies.  An empty cup is recommended as the reference for DSC-TGA.  

  • Both alumina (40mL or 90 mL) and platinum (40 mL or 110 mL) cups can be used for TGA-DTA studies.  

Alumina is generally used because it does not react with or catalyze most high temperature samples.  Alumina cups are also less expensive (i.e., more disposable).  Dry aluminum oxide is used in most TGA-DTA experiment as an inert reference material to reduce baseline drift due to heat capacity effects.  Use a weight roughly equivalent to the sample.  (Aluminum oxide should be kept dry by storing it in a dessicator.)

Sample Cup Placement:

Sample cup placement on the SDT beam’s platinum sensor is important for good TGA-DTA results, but it is critical for optimum DSC-TGA results because the reproducibility of that placement directly affects the TGA weight signal used to normalize DSC heat flow.  Position the sample and reference cups as close as possible to the beam side of the sensor.  Mark each cup and make sure that you position the cups so that its horizontal and rotational position are the same for each experiment.

Close/Prepurge the Furnace:

After the sample is in place close the furnace by pressing the FURNACE key located on the instrument touch screen.  Prepurge the furnace area if desired, before starting the experiment.

Starting the Experiment:

After you have completed the steps needed in the wizard, you can:

  • Press the START RUN button to close the wizard and begin this run immediately.

or

  • Select Finish to close the wizard, storing the information you have entered. You may start this experiment later by selecting Control/Start, pressing the START on the tool bar, or pressing the START button located on the instrument touch screen or keypad.
