Setting Up a Stress Sweep Experiment

 

Multistress (Stress Sweep) experiments are performed over a range of stress or force at a constant frequency at isothermal temperature. These experiments provide information on the effect of stress on material properties and can be used to look at sample resilience and identify the linear viscoelastic region (LVR).

If you want to set up a DMA Stress Sweep procedure, you can choose one of two techniques—the DMA Wizard or the Experimental View - Procedure Page. See the instructions below for each technique.

When you perform a DMA Stress Sweep experiment, the following parameters need to be chosen:

  • For Tensioning Type Clamps Only:  Enter the Preload Force. Then, choose one of two methods (a or b) to maintain a positive force on the sample:  

    Preload Force is the force applied to the sample prior to initiating the experiment.  

    • Preload force is not available when you select a nontensioning clamp (i.e., single/dual cantilever, shear sandwich).  

    • Preload force is required with the tensioning clamps (i.e., 3-point bending, compression, and tension film/fiber) when running multifrequency and multistrain experiments in order to sustain oscillation (ensure that force is applied to the sample throughout the complete oscillation).  Typical preload forces for those experiments are 0.01 to 0.5 N.

     

  1. Check the Force Track box and enter the desired ratio of static and dynamic forces, if you want the total force that is applied to the sample to be automatically adjusted during the experiment.

    Selecting Force Track will cause the instrument to automatically adjust the force as the sample changes modulus, in order to minimize permanent sample deformation. This parameter is used for 3-point bending, compression, penetration, and film/fiber tension clamps. These clamps allow sample deformation in one direction only.

    Force track automatically adjusts the total force (preload and dynamic) applied to the sample during multifrequency and multistrain experiments.  

    • Force track is only used when one of the tensioning clamps (i.e., 3-point bend, compression, tension film and tension fiber) is selected. It is not needed for nontensioning clamps (i.e., single/dual cantilever, shear sandwich).

    • Force track essentially determines the dynamic force required to oscillate at the desired amplitude and frequency; it applies an additional static force that is a constant multiple of that dynamic force. This is necessary to avoid sample buckling in tension clamps and loss of contact with the sample for compression and 3-point bending clamps.

    • Recommended force track values are 120% for thin films and low denier fibers (tension film and tension fiber clamps), 150% for thermoplastics (3-point bending clamps), and 125% for general applications.

    • As the temperature increases, the modulus of the material drops (provided that there is no cross-linking reaction). Since the static force is adjusted in proportion to the dynamic force, the sample does not creep or undergo permanent deformation during the experiment.

    After checking the Force Track box, enter the desired value in percent.

  2. Uncheck the Force Track box, if you want the to use a constant force (the Preload Force) on the sample.

NOTE:  If there is a need to maintain a constant force on the sample throughout the experiment, enter that force in the Preload Force field and uncheck Force Track.

  • Frequency:  A single frequency is used throughout the experiment.  1 Hz is recommended.

  • Initial Amplitude:  This parameter is used to evaluate the stiffness of the sample in order to establish the limits of stress that the instrument will be able to apply at a certain frequency. This should be a small number, in the range of 2 to 5 µm.

  • Isothermal Temperature & Soak Time:  Stress/force sweeps for determining the linear viscoelastic region are performed isothermally.  Ambient temperature is commonly used.  When evaluating the material at another temperature [achievable temperature range with gas cooling accessory is -145 to 600°C], it is important to ensure thermal equilibrium is reached before initiating the strain sweep.  A five-minute "soak time" is adequate for most materials.

  • Number of Sweeps:  A single stress sweep is sufficient for determination of linear viscoelastic response.  Repetitive sweeps provide information about structural stability in "softer" materials.  Elastomers, for example, can exhibit lower modulus on subsequent stress sweeps if the time between sweeps is to short to rebuild internal structure destroyed during the initial sweep.

  • Stress/Force Table:   The stress that should be used will vary from sample to sample based on the sample stiffness. An initial run may be performed with a large range and then you can select the limits for subsequent experiments. It is easier to choose the range using force values because the force range of the instrument is fixed, whereas the stress depends on the geometry and dimensions of the sample. Recommended force values are:  0.01 to 10 N, logarithmically spaced, 5 points per decade. For more information on stress tables, click here Stress/force table.

  • Additional Conditions:  There are additional experimental conditions that can be changed to optimize specific situations.  These are accessed using Advanced Parameters and Post-Test Conditions.  For most experiments, the system default values shown are recommended.

  • See also:  Guidelines for Running Subambient Experiments.

Using the Experimental View Procedure Page:

  1. Display the Procedure Page. Select the desired experiment from the list of available preprogrammed templates.

  2. Enter the desired parameters. When you have finished setting up this procedure, you can enter more information by selecting the Notes Page and Summary Page for this run.

  3. Click the Apply button when finished to save these changes or the Append button to save the changes to this run and add another run to the end of the sequence.

NOTE:  You can use any test template as the basis for a custom test by selecting the desired test template from the list of tests, then click the Apply button and change the test to Custom. Then you can click on the Procedure Page and then the Editor button to make the changes desired.
