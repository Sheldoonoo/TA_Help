Using the Tzero Press

The following basic instructions can be applied to any set of dies using with the Tzero press. For more details on preparing specific types of sample pans, see the following topics.

  • Preparing Tzero Aluminum Pans and Tzero Low-Mass Aluminum Pans (Black die set)

Follow these basic instructions to use the Tzero sample press:

  1. Select the desired sample pan and matching lid and obtain the matching die set. The die sets have colored rings and the pan/lid boxes have matching colored labels.

NOTE: Always handle the pans and lids carefully with tweezers. Ensure that any and all surfaces that the pans are placed on are clean and free of any possible contaminants.

  1. Weigh the empty pan and lid.

NOTE: Only the Q2000 and Q1000 use the weight of the pan and lid (in Advanced Tzero mode) to improve the resolution and sensitivity of your heat flow measurement. If you carefully measure and enter the empty sample and reference pan weights into the software, the effect of the difference between the sample and reference pan weights will be compensated for and will improve your heat flow measurement. It is highly recommended that you take the extra time to do so. Users of the other Q Series and earlier DSC instruments do not need to perform this step.  

  1. Prepare the sample and place it in the pan. If you are using a powder or granular sample, spread it evenly in the pan.
  2. Weigh the filled sample pan and lid and determine the sample weight. It is important to control the amount of sample you are using. Refer to additional material in the training courses for optimal sample size considerations.
  3. Place the filled sample pan in the lower die and position the matching lid in place.
  4. Move the press handle back and place the matching upper die into the press. The upper die slides in easily and a magnet will hold it in place.

CAUTION:  Make sure that you hold the handle firmly throughout the entire crimping procedure!

  1. Place the lower die with the prepared sample pan into the press. Rotate the die slightly from side to side to ensure that it seats properly.
  2. Pull the handle forward until it stops to seal the lid to the pan.

       

Back to top

  1. Move the handle back again and remove the lower die and pan.

  1. Use the pan removal tool on the front of the press as shown in the figure below to remove the Tzero and Standard Aluminum Hermetic sample pans from the lower dies. The sample is ready to be tested on the instrument. See Loading/Unloading a Sample for more information.

  1. Prepare an empty pan and lid for use as the reference pan. It is important that the same care be taken in preparing the reference pan as in preparing the sample pan.

Preparing Tzero Aluminum Pans and Tzero Low-Mass Aluminum Pans

The Tzero aluminum pans require the use of the black die set shown in the figure to the right.

There is one lower black die and two upper black dies. Only one of these two upper dies is used for a given sample.

  • The upper black die with a flat surface is used for samples that lie flat and evenly in the sample pan. It will maximize the contact of the lid and sample.
  • The upper black die with the cupped surface is used for samples that are irregularly shaped. This die will conform the lid to the irregular sample and minimize distortion of the bottom of the sample pan.

     

 

To prepare a sample in a Tzero aluminum pan, consider the comments above and follow the instructions here Using the Tzero Press.

Preparing Standard Aluminum Hermetic Pans

The standard aluminum hermetic pans require the use of the white die set shown in the figure to the right.

There is one lower white die and two upper white dies. Both upper dies must be used to seal the standard aluminum hermetic pans. Each die has been inscribed with a number indicating the order in which they will be used during the sealing process.

  • The upper white die with a recessed ring is used to form the lid (Die #1).
  • The upper white die with the small raised flat platform is used to finish sealing the lid (Die #2).

Follow these basic instructions to use the Tzero sample press and the Standard Aluminum Hermetic pans:

  1. Select the desired sample pan and matching lid and obtain the matching die set. The die sets have colored rings and the pan/lid boxes have matching colored labels.

NOTE: Always handle the pans and lids carefully with tweezers. Ensure that any and all surfaces that the pans are placed on are clean and free of any possible contaminants.

  1. Weigh the empty pan and lid.

NOTE: Only the Q2000 and Q1000 use the weight of the pan and lid (in Advanced Tzero mode) to improve the resolution and sensitivity of your heat flow measurement. If you carefully measure and enter the empty sample and reference pan weights into the software, the effect of the difference between the sample and reference pan weights will be compensated for and will improve your heat flow measurement. It is highly recommended that you take the extra time to do so. Users of the other Q Series and earlier DSC instruments do not need to perform this step.  

  1. Prepare the sample and carefully place the sample in the pan. If you are using a powder or granular sample, spread it evenly in the pan. Do not allow the sample to spill onto the lip of the pan.
  2. Weigh the filled sample pan and lid and determine the sample weight. It is important to control the amount of sample you are using. Refer to additional material in the training courses for optimal sample size considerations.
  3. Place the filled sample pan in the lower die and position the matching lid in place, concave side up.
  4. Push the press handle back and place the upper white die #1 into the press. The upper die slides in easily and a magnet will hold it in place.
  5. Place the lower die with the prepared sample pan and lid into the press. Rotate the die slightly from side to side to ensure that it seats properly.
  6. Pull the handle forward until it stops to form the edges of the sealing surface prior to finishing the seal.
  7. Push the press handle back and remove the lower die and pan and then the upper die #1.
  8. Install the upper white die #2 into the press.

  1. Replace the lower white die with the "formed" pan into the press. Rotate the die slightly from side to side to ensure that it seats properly.
  2. Pull the handle forward until it stops. This step requires more force than the other die and pan types, this is normal.

CAUTION:  Make sure that you hold the handle firmly throughout the entire crimping procedure!

  1. Push the handle back again and remove the lower die and pan.
  2. Use the pan removal tool on the front of the press to remove the sample pan. The sample is ready to be tested on the instrument. See Loading/Unloading a Sample for more information.

Preparing High Volume Pans

The high volume pans require the use of the yellow die set shown in the figure to the right.

There is one lower yellow die (right) and one upper yellow die (left). Both yellow dies must be used to seal the high volume pans.

Please read the manual before using these pans. Click here to open the Adobe pdf file DSC High Volume Pan Kit Manual.

Follow these basic instructions to use the Tzero sample press and the high volume pans:

  1. Select the desired sample pan, matching lid, and o-ring, and obtain the yellow die set.

NOTE: Always handle the pans and lids carefully with tweezers. Ensure that any and all surfaces that the pans are placed on are clean and free of any possible contaminants.

  1. Weigh the empty pan, o-ring, and lid.
  2. Use tweezers to place the o-ring in the lid and push it all the way to the top of the lid.
  3. Prepare the sample and carefully place the sample in the pan. If you are using a powder or granular sample, spread it evenly in the pan. Do not allow the sample to spill onto the lip of the pan.
  4. Weigh the filled sample pan and lid and determine the sample weight. It is important to control the amount of sample you are using. Refer to additional material in the training courses for optimal sample size considerations.
  5. Place the filled sample pan in the lower die and position the matching lid in place.
  6. Push the press handle back and place the upper yellow die into the press. The upper die slides in easily and a magnet will hold it in place.
  7. Place the lower yellow die with the prepared sample pan and lid into the press. Position the die with the raised platform to the rear of the press as shown in the figure below. Rotate the die slightly from side to side to ensure that it seats properly.

  1. Pull the handle forward until it stops to push the lid onto the pan.

CAUTION:  Make sure that you hold the handle firmly throughout the entire crimping procedure!
