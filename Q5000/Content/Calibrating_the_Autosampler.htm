Calibrating the Autosampler

If the Autosampler fails to pick up a sample pan correctly during an automatic loading procedure, the Autosampler may need to be calibrated. The calibration procedure is performed at installation and should be done periodically thereafter, as needed.

Other possible causes for improper pan pickup include:

  • The instrument is not level. See Aligning the TGA Balance for instructions to solve this problem.

  • The hang-down wire is not straight. Replace the hang-down wire. See Installing/Replacing the Hang-Down Wires for instructions.

  • The pan is not properly positioned on the tray.

  • For pans with bails: A bent bail may cause improper pan pickup. Be sure to use straight bails.

Autosampler calibration requires the calibration of two load positions, upper and lower. To perform the calibration follow the instructions below.

  1. Touch the Calibrate button at the bottom of the touch screen. See Introducing the Touch Screen for more information.

  2. Press the Autosampler button.

  3. Touch the Calibration tab, if it is not already selected.

  4. Select "Calibrate Load Lower" or "Calibrate Load Upper" from the list of options at the top of the page using the arrow buttons. Click on the desired link to obtain information:

    • Calibrate Load Lower

    • Calibrate Load Upper

  5. If you are using sealed aluminum pans, you will also need to calibrate the "Punch Lower" and "Punch Limit" positions. Select these from the list at the top of the page using the arrow buttons. Click on the desired link to obtain information:

    • Calibrate Punch Lower

    • Calibrate Punch Limit

Additional Functions

In addition to the calibration functions, several other functions are available from the Calibration tab:

Name

Description

Reset

Resets the Autosampler.

Load Pan 1

Select "Load Pan 1" from the Calibration tab to initiate loading of the sample pan in position number 1 on the Autosampler tray.

Unload Pan 1

Select "Unload Pan 1" from the Calibration tab to initiate unloading of the sample pan to return to position number 1 on the Autosampler tray.

Pan 1 to Front

Select "Pan 1 to Front" from the Calibration tab to bring the sample pan in position number 1 on the Autosampler tray to the front of the instrument.

Calibrate Park

Allows you to set the park position for the Autosampler tray. Click here for more information: Calibrate Park.

Calibrate Load Lower Position

This calibration is used to adjust the position of the sample tray so that  the tray can move out of the way without interfering with the pan when the pan hangs from the hang-down wire hook.

  1. Place a pan and bail in the number 1 position of the sample tray.

  2. Select "Calibrate Load Lower" from the Calibration page on the touch screen.

  1. Select the GO TO button. The pan will be loaded.

  2. Observe the position of the tray when the pan has loaded. The tray should move down and clear the bottom of the pan without touching it.

  3. Touch the directional arrows to move the tray up/down, left/right, or clockwise/counterclockwise, followed by the GO TO button, until the tray is correctly positioned.

  4. Select SAVE when the tray has been moved to the desired unload (lower load) position.

Calibrate Load Upper Position

This calibration is used to adjust the position of the sample tray as it slides across to load a sample pan.

  1. Place a pan and bail in the number 1 position of the sample tray.

  2. Select "Calibrate Load Upper" from the Calibration page on the touch screen.

  1. Select the GO TO button. The pan will be moved to the load position.

  2. Observe the position of the bail and the hang-down wire hook when the pan stops. The hook should be in the center of the bail's angled handle and should not touch the bail so that the hook will not pull off the pan when the tray moves. See the figure to the right of the Q5000 IR loading a pan as an example.

  3. Touch the directional arrows, followed by the GO TO button, to move the desired position for the tray up/down, left/right, or clockwise/counterclockwise until the tray is correctly positioned.

  4. Select SAVE when the tray has been moved to the desired load (upper) position.

NOTE:  The left/right and rotational positions will be saved to both the Calibrate Load Lower and Calibrate Load Upper windows. Therefore, you will automatically save the values to both places during the calibration procedure.

Calibrate Park

This calibration is used to adjust the position of the sample tray so that when the Autosampler is in park position it is set off to the right and below the home position. This is to allow you to move the tray out of the way when desired by touching PARK AUTOSAMPLER from the Control touch screen.

  1. Select "Calibrate Park" from the Calibration page on the touch screen.

  2. Select the GO TO button. The pan will be moved to the park position.

  3. Observe the position of the tray. It should move to the right and below the home position.

  4. Touch the directional arrows, followed by the GO TO button, to move the tray in/out or clockwise/counterclockwise until the tray is correctly positioned.

  5. Select SAVE when the tray has been calibrated in the appropriate park position.
