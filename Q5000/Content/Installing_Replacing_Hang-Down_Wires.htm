Installing the Tare Hang-Down Wire (Q5000 IR)

During installation or replacement, take care not to bend the hang-down wires or damage the hang-down loops.

  1. Remove the balance housing cover by removing the two screws in the back with a screwdriver. (See the figure to the right.) Retain the screws. Lift the cover off.

  2. Remove the six screws on the inner balance faceplate and remove the plate. Make sure the small o-rings inside the faceplate are not lost. They must be in place when the faceplate is reinstalled.

  3. Turn the instrument power switch to ON, if it is not already powered up.

Installing the Sample Hang-Down Tube (Q5000 IR)

Before you can install the sample hang-down wire, you must install the tube assembly that surrounds the wire. Follow these steps:

  1. Locate the sample hang-down tube in the accessory kit.

  2. Open the furnace.

  3. Cover the furnace opening with a flat object (such as a business card) to prevent anything from falling inside.

  4. Holding the tube portion with tweezers, install the sample hang-down tube by inserting it straight up inside the sample side hole in the cooling plate. Be careful not to bend the baffles.  See the figure to the right.

  5. Adjust the length of the sample tube by pushing it up or pulling it down.  The length from the bottom of the baffle to the cooling plate should be 1.6 in (4.1 cm).

  6. Remove the flat object covering the furnace opening.

  7. With the sample hang-down tube in place, you can proceed to install the sample hang-down wire as directed in the next section.

Installing the Sample Hang-Down Wire (Q5000 IR)

If you have just replaced the tare hang-down wire using the steps above, you have already done steps 1, 2, and 3 and you can proceed to step 4 below. During installation or replacement, take care not to bend the hang-down wires or damage the hang-down loops.

  1. Remove the balance housing cover by removing the two screws in the back with a screwdriver. (See the figure to the right.) Retain the screws. Lift the cover off.

  2. Remove the six screws on the inner balance faceplate and remove the plate. Make sure the small o-rings inside the faceplate are not lost. They must be in place when the faceplate is reinstalled.

  3. Turn the instrument power switch to ON, if it is not already powered up.

CAUTION:  During installation, take care not to bend the hang-down wires or damage the hang-down loops.

  1. Placing a finger under the sample hang-down tube, push the tube up slowly until it stops moving, being careful not to bend the baffles.

  2. Locate the sample hang-down wire and the sample hang-down installation tool (shown to the right), found in the accessory kit.

  3. Optional: At this point you may wish to place a small piece of white paper carefully behind the hang-down loop in order to see it better later in this procedure.

  4. Hold the hang-down wire with brass tweezers, being careful not to bend it. Position the hang-down wire so that the double bend hook is at the top.  

  5. Lower the hang-down wire into the opening in the sample hang-down installation tool.

  6. Lower the furnace to its fully open position.

  7. Carefully angling the tool as shown in the figure below, lower the bottom into the furnace opening until you have enough clearance to hold the tool and wire vertically without bending the wire (very important).

  1. Insert the wire and sample hang-down installation tool vertically up inside the sample tube, being careful not to bend the wire. See the figure below. Continue to raise it slowly, feeding it through the purge control cap until the top of the wire protrudes from the opening in the cap.

  1. Cover the furnace opening with a flat object that will completely block the opening.

  2. Still holding the tool, use the touch screen Calibrate/Autosampler/Motor Test/Furnace Up function to raise the furnace slowly until the bottom of the tool can rest on the flat object covering the opening. The top of the wire should still protrude from the top of the purge control cap.

  3. Position the sample hang-down wire so that the double bend hook is pointed to the left. (The figure below shows the reference side hang-down wire installed. The procedure is the same for this step.) Grasp the hang-down wire with brass tweezers, being careful not to bend it.

  1. Using tweezers, gently hang the double-bend hook (pointing to the left) on the hang-down loop. Maneuver the hook until it is fully inserted through the loop.

  2. Lower the furnace to its fully open position, allowing the tool to drop down with the furnace. The hang-down wire, if properly inserted through the loop will remain in position.

  3. Remove the paper (if used) and the object covering the furnace opening.

  4. Fully remove the tool from the hang-down wire, being careful not to bend the wire.

  5. Adjust the length of the sample tube by pulling it down. The length from the bottom of the baffle to the cooling plate should be 1.6 in (4.1 cm), which can be determined using the supplied gauge. See the figure to the right.

  6. Hang the desired sample pan from the hook. Choose the same type of pan that will be used for your experiments.

  7. Align the balance, if needed. (The balance was aligned at TA Instruments before shipment and may not need realignment.) Click here for more information  Aligning the TGA Balance.

  8. Close the balance housing by replacing the six screws on the inner balance faceplate. Make sure the small o-rings are in position on the inside of the faceplate so that it seats correctly.

  9. Lower the balance housing cover over the balance then install the two screws in the back.

NOTE:  If your instrument has a humidity chamber, the same type of hang-down wire is used for both the tare and sample sides of the balance. The procedure is described in this section.

Installing the Tare or Sample Hang-Down Wires

During installation or replacement, take care not to bend the hang-down wires or damage the hang-down loops.

  1. Turn on the instrument. The humidity chamber will automatically lower.

  2. Remove the screws securing the inner balance chamber faceplate to the instrument and remove the faceplate. Make sure the small o-rings inside the faceplate are not lost. They must be in place when the faceplate is reinstalled. See the figure below.

Back to top

NOTE:  If you are performing this operation due to damaged hang-down wires, remove the closeout disks before continuing.

  1. Locate the hang-down wire and fixture (see the figure below right) in your Accessory Kit.

  2. Lower the hang-down wire into the opening in the fixture and turn the double-loop gently to the left.

  3. Holding the fixture only, angle the fixture and wire so that the bottom goes into the chamber slightly to allow enough space for the top of the wire to clear the humidity chamber without hitting it. See the figure below.

  1. Straighten the fixture so that it is vertical and slowly insert the wire up through the hole in the cooling plate being careful not to bend or bow the hang-down wire. Continue to raise the fixture until the hook protrudes slightly from the purge control cap. See the figure below.

  2. Optional: At this point you may wish to place a small piece of white paper carefully behind the hang-down loop in order to see it better.

  3. Using tweezers, if needed, gently feed the double-bend hook (pointing to the left) through the hole in the hang-down loop. Maneuver the hook until it is fully inserted through the loop. (The figure to the right shows the reference side hang-down wire installed.)

  4. Allow the fixture to drop straight down into the furnace, do not try to angle the fixture as you remove it or the wire may bend.

  5. Carefully remove the fixture and the paper (if used).

  6. Repeat this procedure for the other hang-down wire located on the reference side. (The same length hang-down wire is used for both sides.)

  7. Install the Autosampler sample platform (found in the accessory kit).  There is a guide pin that will help you orient the platform correctly.

  8. Select Autosampler Reset from the touch screen or from the instrument control program.

  9. Align the balance, if needed. (The balance was aligned at TA Instruments before shipment and may not need realignment.) Click here for more information  Aligning the TGA Balance.

  10. Close the balance housing by replacing the six screws on the inner balance faceplate. Make sure the small o-rings are in position on the inside of the faceplate so that it seats correctly.

  11. Lower the balance housing cover over the balance then install the two screws in the back.

  12. Install the humidity close-out disks, if they were removed previously. See the NOTE above. Click here for more information Installing the Humidity Close-Out Disks.
