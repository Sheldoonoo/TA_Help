Preparing Sealed TGA Pans

 

Sealed aluminum pans are available for use with the Q5000 IR and Q5000 SA. They are used with the punching mechanism for opening sealed pans before the experiment. The ability to encapsulate samples prior to placing them in the Autosampler tray and to open them just before loading into the furnace serves to prevent loss of volatiles to the environment or to avoid adsorption of moisture by a hygroscopic material while the material is awaiting analysis.

The TA Instruments Tzero Sample Encapsulating Press (PN 901600.901) or the blue Sample Encapsulating Press (PN 900878.902) can be used to seal samples in the TGA aluminum sample pans.

Taring Pans Before Sealing

Assemble the bail, pan, and lid for each pan that will be sealed. Before placing the sample in the pans and sealing them, inspect the pan lids to make sure there is a complete, circular "score" all the way around the lid. If the scoring is incomplete or nonexistent, discard the pan lid. Place the assembled pieces on the sample tray in the order that they will be used.

Follow the instructions to tare all of the pans. Click here for more information if needed: Taring the Pan(s).

Preparing the Sample and Pan

Sealing the aluminum pans is basically a two-step process. The first pressing forms the sample pan into its shape and the second pressing seals and flattens the rim of the pan closed. Follow the instructions below carefully to assemble the sample and pan.

  1. Remove the pan from its tared spot in the tray and note the tray position.

  2. Remove the pan from its bale.

  3. Carefully remove the lid from the pan.

  4. Carefully place the sample in the bottom of the aluminum pan. Do not allow the sample to spill onto the lip of the pan. See the figure to the right.

  5. Place the lid on the pan with the raised side facing up.

  6. Seal the pan. Follow the directions appropriate to your press:

    • Sealing Pans Using the Tzero Press

    • Sealing Pans Using the Blue Press

Sealing Pans Using the Tzero Press

Follow these basic instructions to use the Tzero sample press to seal TGA pans:

  1. Obtain the purple TGA die set (PN 957540.901).

NOTE: Always handle the pans and lids carefully with tweezers. Ensure that any and all surfaces that the pans are placed on are clean and free of any possible contaminants.

  1. Make sure you have tared the empty pan and lid before loading the sample. See Taring Pans Before Sealing

  2. Prepare the sample and pan as directed. See Preparing the Sample and Pan.

  3. Place the assembled pan in the lower die. See the figure to the right.

  4. Move the press handle back and place the upper purple die #1 into the press. The upper die slides in easily and a magnet will hold it in place.

  

  1. Place the lower die with the prepared sample pan into the press. Rotate the die slightly from side to side to ensure that it seats properly.

  1. Pull the handle forward until it stops to form the edges of the sealing surface prior to finishing the seal.

Configuring the Sample Press for Sealing Aluminum Pans

This procedure only applies to the blue sample press shown in the figure above.

  1. Remove the DSC lower die set as follows:

  1. Raise the cam lever handle, then loosen the thumbscrew on the column of the press, which locks the lower die in place.

  2. Lower the die holder by turning the outer ring clockwise.

  3. Lift the lower die to remove it from the die holder.

  1. Place the lower TGA die (see the figure to the right) into the die holder, with either end facing up.

  2. Lower the cam lever handle.

  1. Raise the bottom die by turning the outer ring counterclockwise until it just touches the upper die.

Using the Blue Press to Seal the TGA Aluminum Pans

NOTE: Always handle the pans and lids carefully with tweezers. Ensure that any and all surfaces that the pans are placed on are clean and free of any possible contaminants.

  1. Make sure you have tared the empty pan and lid before loading the sample. See Taring Pans Before Sealing.

  2. Prepare the sample and pan as directed. See Preparing the Sample and Pan.

  3. Place the assembled pan in the lower die of the sample press.

  4. Place the preforming tool on top of the pan with the Flat Side #1 facing up at the pan end of the tool and the Recessed Side # 1 (also called the "forming side") facingdown. The recessed side of the tool "forms" the sample pan into shape. See the figures below for reference. Hold onto the end of the tool with one hand.

    

  1. Using your other hand, pull the cam lever handle down until it stops.

  2. Raise the lever and remove the preforming tool.

  3. Flip the tool over so that the Flat Side #2 is facing up at the pan end of the tool and Recessed Side #2 is facing down. Slide the preforming tool on top of the pan. (Using the tool in this position will cause the recessed portion of the tool to flatten the seal around the edge of the pan.)

  4. Lower the cam lever handle with a steady motion until the lever hits the stop.

  5. Raise the handle and remove the sealed pan from the press with tweezers.

  6. Place the sealed pan back on the bale and return it to the same spot on the tray from which it was removed. The tab on the sample bail should align with the notch in the sample tray. It is important for successful punching of sealed pans that the pans are placed securely in the bail and not tilted in any way. The sealed pan is now ready to be punched when the experiment starts.

  7. Repeat steps for each sample pan.

  8. Replace the sample tray on the Autosampler, if needed, and place the plastic cover over the samples, if desired.

You can preweigh the samples prior to the start of the experiment, if desired, in order to obtain a record of the weight. A preweight is valuable for highly volatile materials. This function is performed through the controller functions and performed before you start an experiment. This weight is stored within the data file record.
