Transporting the DMA

 

Important:  If you need to ship this instrument, you will need to contact TA Instruments' Service to properly package the instrument for shipment. Improper packaging will result in damage to the instrument.

If, however, you need to transport the DMA Q series instrument yourself by vehicle, follow these instructions to pack up the instrument safely. It is very important to properly protect this precision instrument to guard against potential problems when the unit is unpacked and used again. These instructions are not needed when moving the instrument on a cart within the same building.

WARNING:  Have an assistant help you pack this unit.  Do not attempt to do this alone.

CAUTION:  Follow the directions on the packing instructions carefully when installing the shipping material. You MUST make sure that the four jackscrews located under the four dress cover Phillips screws are tightened properly (step 12) or the instrument may be damaged. When unpacking the instrument again, be sure to loosen the jackscrews as directed in the unpacking instructions, PN 985012.000, which are shipped with the instrument.

  1. Remove any samples and the current clamp from the instrument. The instrument should be powered on and warmed up.

  2. Turn on the air filter regulator valve, which should already be connected, and confirm that the air pressure builds to 60 psi. Disconnect any coolers, but leave the gas lines in place for now.

  3. Press the FURNACE key on the touch screen to raise the furnace.

  4. Press the DRIVE key on the touch screen until it reads "Floating."

  5. Obtain the shipping bracket that was removed when the instrument was unpacked.  

  6. Slide the bracket into the dovetail. Use a 9/16-inch hex (Allen) wrench to tighten the four hex screws holding the shipping bracket in place (see the figure to the right).  

  7. Use a 1/16-inch hex (Allen) wrench to tighten the setscrew in the center of the shipping bracket.

  8. Press the DRIVE key on the touch screen until it reads "Locking."  You should hear the click of the air bearing solenoid.

  9. Replace the blue shipping foam on the handrest, if it is available (see figure below).

Back to top

  1. Press the FURNACE key on the touch screen to lower the furnace.

  2. Obtain the large shipping bolt. Use a 7/16-inch wrench to install the large hex bolt to hold the furnace in place.  See the figure to the right.

  3. Remove the four dress cover screws on the four corners. Tighten the four 1/4-20 jackscrews (standoffs), located under the dress cover, using a 3/16 Allen wrench. Turn the screws clockwise evenly until they reach the bottom.

  4. Turn off the instrument's power switch, which is located on the back of the instrument and remove the power cord.

  5. Disconnect all other cables and lines attached to the back of the instrument. Place the cables and lines in a box to be shipped with the instrument.

  6. If you have retained the plywood pallet, follow the next steps to mount the instrument. Have an assistant hold one side up while you remove the two mounting feet on one side. Then move to the other side and remove the mounting feet.

  7. Place the plywood pallet on the floor. Using two people, lift the instrument onto the pallet.

  8. Use a 7/16-inch socket wrench to install the five hold-down bolts and washers on the bottom of the plywood pallet.

  9. If desired, you can wrap the instrument in a large plastic bag as shown below. Place the original box over the instrument, if it is available.  The instrument is now ready for transport.
