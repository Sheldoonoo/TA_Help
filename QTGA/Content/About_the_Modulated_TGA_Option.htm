About the Modulated TGA™ Option

Introduction

TA Instruments Modulated TGA (MTGA) is an innovative option that is used with the Thermogravimetric Analyzer, TGA Q500 (standard furnace installed).  This option is used to study the same decomposition or volatilization properties as conventional TGA. However, MTGA provides unique capabilities that increase the amount of information obtained from a single TGA experiment, thereby improving the quality of interpretation.

These unique capabilities include:

  • continuous determination of activation energy

  • verification of single kinetic mechanism

  • verification of first-order kinetic model.

When you run experiments, there will be times when you may wish to use the MTGA option to obtain certain types of data from your samples.  This section provides guidelines that will enable you to decide when it will be beneficial to use the MTGA technique.

The Modulated TGA Technique

Traditional TGA is a well-accepted technique for analyzing the thermal stability and  compositional analysis of materials.  It provides information on the temperature at which a material begins to lose weight, either due to volatilization or decomposition.  

  • For mixtures or blends of materials with differing thermal stabilities, TGA provides information on the amount of the respective components present. (For an example, see ASTM Method E1131).  

  • Traditional TGA may also be used for the study of the kinetics of volatilization or decomposition, using a number of experimental approaches, including the multiple heating rate method. (For an example, see ASTM Method E1641).

MTGA is an enhancement of TGA that provides the same information as TGA, plus new information that permits unique insights into the behavior of the weight loss reaction.

Specifically, MTGA provides an alternative way to obtain kinetic information about one or more weight losses, in a shorter period of time than the multiple heating rate approach.  

In addition, MTGA provides continuous determined values for activation energy throughout the weight loss reaction, not just at specific reaction levels.  The ability to obtain activation energy continuously allows you to follow changes in the activation energy during the reaction, as a function of temperature or conversion.  The calculation of activation energy is “model free”—no knowledge about the form of the kinetic equation is required.  The assumption of a first-order kinetic model (a reasonable assumption for many decomposition reactions), permits the calculation of natural logarithm of the pre-exponential factor in the same manner as the continuous  determination of activation energy.

MTGA should be used where a rapid, single experiment determination of kinetic parameters is desired, or where information concerning these parameters is needed as a function of temperature or conversion. See also:  Selecting a Modulated TGA Procedure.

Selecting a Modulated TGA Procedure

Conventional TGA can be used to study the kinetics of volatilization or decomposition processes using a number of experimental approaches including the multiple heating rate approach specified in ASTM (American Society for Testing & Materials) Method E 1641. Modulated TGA allows similar kinetic information to be obtained from a single experiment by applying a modulated heating profile to the material, which in effect is comparable to applying multiple constant heating rates simultaneously. Hence, Modulated TGA saves time.

In addition, Modulated TGA provides a continuous determination of activation energy throughout the weight loss, not just a specific reaction levels. This determination of activation energy is "model free"—i.e., no knowledge about the form of kinetic equation is required. The assumption of a first-order kinetic model also permits calculation of the natural logarithm of the pre-exponential factor.

  • If High Resolution TGA capability is available, it is recommended that it be used in combination with Modulated TGA. In this case, select Modulated TGA with Hi-Res.

  • If High Resolution TGA capability is not available, select Modulated TGA Standard.
