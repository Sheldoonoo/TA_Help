Installing a New TMA Thermocouple

 

Occasionally, you may need to replace a broken or worn-out part of the TMA. Any replacements needed, other than those discussed in this document, must be supplied and installed by qualified TA Instruments service personnel. Call (302) 427-4050 for service.

Replacing the Thermocouple

You may find that the need arises for a new thermocouple (PN 944344.902) as a result of normal wear and tear, accidental breakage, contamination of the thermocouple, etc. See also:  TMA Replacement Parts, TA Instruments Offices.

Removing the Existing Thermocouple

To remove the thermocouple, you will need to perform the following steps:

WARNING:  Whenever you remove the balance enclosure, make sure that the power is off and the instrument is unplugged.

  1. Raise the furnace and rotate it clockwise to move it off to the side.
  2. Remove the stage shield by lifting it straight up. (This is a friction fit.)
  3. Remove the spring clip holding the thermocouple in place.
  4. Obtain the hex wrench from the accessory kit and remove the screws, two on each side that hold the balance enclosure in place.
  5. Slide the balance enclosure out and remove it.
  6. Thread the thermocouple carefully down through the opening in the platform, and unplug it.

Installing a New Thermocouple

Replace the sample thermocouple, following the remainder of the instructions.

  1. Insert the two-pin thermocouple connector into the fitting as shown in the figure to the right.
  2. Carefully route the thermocouple through the guide to prevent interference with the balance mechanism.  (See the yellow-lined route in the figure below.)  A service loop is recommended to take up excess length.

  1. Carefully thread the thermocouple, tip first, up through  the hole in the top of the balance enclosure.
  2. Slide the balance enclosure into position.
  3. Replace the screws, two on each side, that hold the balance enclosure in place.
  4. Reposition the thermocouple so that the tip of the thermocouple bends at a 90Â° angle and lies flat against the sample platform.  It should be close to, but not touching the sample.
  5. Replace the spring clip to hold the thermocouple in place.
  6. Place the stage shield on the stage, aligning the slot in the bottom over the thermocouple wire.
