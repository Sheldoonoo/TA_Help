Connecting a Spectrometer

 

The Q5000 IR furnace allows you to connect a spectrometer, such as an FTIR or mass spectrometer to the instrument. This topic provides information needed to connect either type of spectrometer.

To connect any spectrometer, you will need to use a transfer line (supplied by the spectrometer manufacturer) to transport the gas evolved from the sample to the spectrometer. See Transfer Line Requirements for information.

Transfer Line Requirements

To connect any spectrometer, you will need to use a transfer line (supplied by the spectrometer manufacturer) to transport the evolved gas from the sample to the spectrometer.

Click one of the following topics for more information:

  • Mass Spectrometer Transfer Line Requirements

  • FTIR Transfer Line Requirements

Mass Spectrometer Transfer Line Requirements

The transfer line should have the following characteristics:

  • The dimension specifications should be as follows:

    • Mass Spectrometer Transfer Line = maximum diameter of 1.3 inches.

    • Mass Spectrometer Capillary Tube = maximum diameter of 0.023 inches.

  • The Mass Spectrometer transfer line should be made of heat-resistant alloy capable of resisting corrosion by the evolved gas and oxidation at temperatures up to 1200Â°C.

  • The mass spectrometer transfer line must pass through the exhaust gas fitting and a purge gas outlet in the quartz tube (with an internal diameter of 0.15 inches). It should end at a point just short of the inside diameter of the quartz tube to ensure that the evolved gases do not condense before entering the transfer line and to avoid contacting sample pans when the furnace moves.

  • The transfer line must be long enough to allow flexible movement. It must accommodate movement of the furnace up and down 9.5 cm (3.75 inches) to open and close for sample loading and unloading. (If the transfer line is not long enough, it must be disconnected and reconnected each time the furnace is opened and closed.)

  • If you are connecting to a mass spectrometer, an interface kit, PN 957349.901, is available for use with the Q5000 IR. This kit may be used with any mass spectrometer, if the specifications for the capillary interface and transfer line are compatible.

FTIR Transfer Line Requirements

The transfer line should have the following characteristics:

  • The FTIR transfer line should be 1/8-inch O.D. to attach to the Swagelok fitting on the adapter.

NOTE:  The Swagelok ferrules should be pre-swaged onto the 1/8-inch O.D. FTIR transfer line, prior to connecting the transfer line to the FTIR Adapter.

  • The FTIR line should be made of heat-resistant alloy (to 200 Â°C) capable of resisting corrosion by the evolved gas and oxidation. The transfer line should be able to withstand temperatures up to 200 Â°C at the interface to the adapter.

  • The FTIR transfer line must be long enough to allow flexible movement. It must accommodate movement of the furnace up and down 9.5 cm (3 and 3/4 inches) to open and close for sample loading and unloading. (If the transfer line is not long enough, it must be disconnected and reconnected each time the furnace is opened and closed.)

  • If you are connecting to an FTIR spectrometer, an interface kit, PN 957468.901, is available for use with the Q5000 IR. This kit may be used with any FTIR spectrometer, if the specifications for the transfer tube are compatible.

Connecting to a Spectrometer Without an Interface Kit

To connect your spectrometer directly to the furnace, without an interface kit, follow these steps:

  1. Install a SwagelokÂ® nut, drive ring, and ferrule on the correct length of transfer line, leaving more than two inches of transfer line projecting beyond the ferrule.

  2. Swage the ferrule, then cut the end of the transfer tube off so that two inches of the tube projects beyond the ferrule.

NOTE: Extending the transfer line more than two inches beyond the Swagelok fitting may cause the TGA to operate improperly.

  1. Make sure that the end of the transfer line is straight and free of oxide deposits before you insert it into the exhaust gas connection.

  2. Insert the transfer line and tighten the Swagelok nut onto the furnace adapter outlet to seal the connector. When you tighten the Swagelok nut, use a 3/8-inch wrench on the exhaust fitting flats to prevent them from turning.

WARNING: If the transfer line is not straight, or has heavy oxide deposits on it, the sample tube may be broken as the line is inserted.

Installing the Mass Spectrometer Interface

To connect your mass spectrometer to the Q5000 IR furnace you will need to remove the furnace exhaust tube assembly and then install the spectrometer interface assembly.  Follow the instructions below:

Installation Requirements for the Mass Spectrometer Interface

To install a mass spectrometer interface you will need the following:

  • 5/64 hex key (Allen wrench)

  • Phillips screwdriver

  • TA Instruments' Mass Spectrometer Upgrade Kit, PN 957349.901

  • Mass spectrometer with a transfer line that meets the requirements above. Click here  Transfer Line Requirements for more information.

Inserting the Transfer Line

NOTE:  Make sure that the end of the capillary is straight and free of oxide deposits before you insert it into the exhaust gas connection.

The white tube (see the figure to the right) should sit inside the adapter's recessed end (see the figure above right).

Use a mirror to look inside the top of the furnace to ensure that the capillary does not extend into the furnace or it may interfere with the sample pan. (You may need to obtain the assistance of a second person for this operation. One person can look down into the furnace and the other person can adjust the position of the capillary to slide it in or out as needed.)

NOTE:  Make sure that the end of the tube is straight and free of oxide deposits before you insert it into the exhaust gas connection.
