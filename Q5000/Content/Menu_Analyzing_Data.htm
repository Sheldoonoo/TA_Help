Analyzing Data

 

When you have finished running experiments on your instrument you will have a collection of data files. To analyze the information contained in these data files use the TA Instruments Universal Analysis program. See the Universal Analysis Getting Started Guide or the online help for further information.

 

Plotting Q5000 SA Results

When plotting Q5000 SA results, either Time or Relative Humidity (%) are almost always used as the X-axis signals. The Sample Sensor RH (%) or Reference Sensor RH (%) provide an indication of the humidity in the area of the sample and reference pans during an experiment, but are generally not used as the X-axis because those values are not as accurate at the Relative Humidity (%) signal.

In addition to direct plots of the raw experimental signals, the Universal Analysis program provides many other useful ways to treat/view the results. For example, the "initial sample weight" can be used for weight change measurements or the "weight after initial drying" can be used. Analyses such as BET and GAB are also available.
