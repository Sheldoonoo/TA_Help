Tensioning vs Nontensioning Clamps

 

There are two classes of clamps for the DMAâ€”tensioning and nontensioning.  The 3-point bend, tension/film, tension/fiber, compression and penetration clamps are tensioning clamps, while the single/dual cantilever and shear sandwich clamps are nontensioning.  

  • In oscillation experiments, strictly speaking, there is a positive force that is applied during the positive part of the sine wave.  In the case of 3-point bending clamps, for example, this would bend the sample by a certain degree.  (Positive force is applied in the downward direction in this clamping system.)  But when the negative part of the sine wave is applied, the negative force (in the upward direction) would make the movable clamp leave the surface of the sample. In case of the tension film clamps, the negative part of the sine wave would buckle the sample. These are undesirable.

  • This can be prevented if a "pre-tension" force is applied to pre-stretch the film/fiber sample or pre-bend the 3-point bending beam beyond the limit of the oscillation amplitude. If the oscillation is applied to the sample in this state, there will always be a positive force on the sample, even during the negative part of the sine wave.

Two methods are available for maintaining a positive force on the sample:  constant force and force track.  

  • When you use constant force, the preload force on the sample remains steady throughout the experiment.  

  • When you select force track, the total force (preload and dynamic) that is applied to the sample during multifrequency and multistrain experiments, using tensioning clamps, is automatically adjusted.  Recommended force track values are 120% for thin films and low denier fibers (using the film and fiber tension clamps), 150% for thermoplastics (using 3-point bend clamps), and 125% for general applications.  

Force track is typically preferred over constant force, since the likelihood of permanently deforming (e.g., stretching) the specimen is greatly reduced by reducing the force as the specimen modulus decreases.

You can select both parameters using the DMA Instrument Control software.
