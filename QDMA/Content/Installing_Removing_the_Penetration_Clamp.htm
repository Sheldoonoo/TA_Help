Installing/Removing the Penetration Clamp

 

The procedures that follow explain the installation and removal of the penetration clamp, which is an optional DMA clamp fixture. The penetration clamp is only used for TMA penetration analysis on the DMA. Information such as glass transition or melting points can be obtained.

See also:  Penetration Clamp

Installing the Penetration Clamp

To install the penetration clamp on the DMA instrument, follow these steps:

  1. Screw the penetration piece into the moveable clamp before installing it onto the instrument.

  2. Slide the moveable clamp (with the penetration piece in place) onto the instrument. Align the dovetail with the holder and tighten the setscrew.

  3. Press CLAMP key on the instrument touch screen to raise the moveable clamp.

  4. Seat the fixed clamp firmly onto all four of the mounting posts.  Then tighten the screws with a hex wrench. Do not over tighten the attachment screws.  Be careful not to damage the thermocouple.

  5. Make sure that the appropriate clamp type and mode are selected on the controller.

  6. Calibrate the clamp (see Calibrating the DMA.)

  7. Position the thermocouple so that it is close to, but not touching the sample.

Removing the Penetration Clamp

To remove the penetration clamp from the DMA, follow these steps:

  1. Press the CLAMP key on the instrument to raise the moveable clamp.

  2. Loosen the four hex screws holding the fixed clamp, then remove the fixed clamp.

  3. Loosen the setscrew holding the moveable clamp on the drive shaft.

  4. Slide the moveable clamp off the dovetail holder.
