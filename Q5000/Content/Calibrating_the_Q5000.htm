Calibrating the Q5000 - Overview

 

To ensure that your Q5000 is properly installed and providing accurate experimental results, you (or your TA Instruments service representative) should calibrate the instrument when you first install it, and then verify the results on suitable standard samples. To ensure that your results continue to be accurate over time, you should validate your instrument results periodically using certified (or well-characterized) samples.

Q5000 IR Calibration

The following calibration procedures are applicable to the TGA Q5000 IR instrument:

  • Balance Conditioning

  • Calibrating the Autosampler

  • Calibrating the Weight

  • Calibrating Temperature

  • Calibrating the Q5000 IR Mass Flow Controllers

  • Calibrating the Touch Screen

Q5000 SA Calibration/Verification

To obtain accurate experimental results you should calibrate the instrument when you first install it.  For the best results, however, you should validate performance periodically and recalibrate, if necessary.

The following calibrations are performed on the Q5000 SA:

  • Weight calibration. See Calibrating the Weight

  • Temperature calibration. See Calibrating the TGA Q5000 SA Temperature

  • Humidity (flow) calibration. See Verifying the Q5000 SA Humidity and Calibrating the Q5000 SA Mass Flow Controllers

  • Autosampler calibration. See Calibrating the Autosampler

  • Touch screen calibration. See Calibrating the Touch Screen

  • Balance Conditioning

NOTE:  You can perform all of these calibrations except temperature and humidity calibrations. See the following sections for information. Calibration of temperature requires a high accuracy external meter and must be performed by TA Instruments Service personnel. Humidity (flow) calibration also requires specialized external devices and must be performed at the factory. Therefore, if humidity calibration issues are detected, the Mass Flow Controller (MFC) assembly must be replaced.
