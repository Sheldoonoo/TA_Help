Power Law Equation

 

Since most materials are non-Newtonian, non-linear models are needed to describe the change in viscosity as a function of shear. The power law equation is the simplest of the available models. The viscosity (either Newtonian or apparent) is replaced by a consistency coefficient, K.

Based upon the index, n, the power law model describes three basic types of flow:

n=1 Newtonian behavior

n<1 Shear thinning (or Pseudoplastic)

n>1 Shear thickening

Example material types:

Most materials are shear thinning and can be represented by the power law over a limited range of shear. Dispersions with a high volume fraction may exhibit shear-thickening behaviour. Often, a volumetric increase may also take place and this is referred to as dilatancy.

Over a sufficiently wide shear range, the power law may not accurately describe the materials behavior and more complex models must then be used.

A more complex model is the Sisko model.
