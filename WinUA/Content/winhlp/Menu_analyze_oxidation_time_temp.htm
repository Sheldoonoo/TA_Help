Oxidation Time/Temp

Use the Oxidation Time/Temp option to analyze DSC data from oxidation induction time (OIT) and oxidation temperature (OT) experiments.

 

  • Oxidation induction time (OIT) is a DSC test method that measures the time to oxidation onset of a sample held at an isothermal temperature and in an oxidizing atmosphere.

  • Oxidation temperature (OT) is a DSC test method that measures the oxidation onset temperature of a temperature-programmed sample in an oxidizing atmosphere.

 

Follow these steps to perform either analysis:

 

1. Select Oxidation Time/Temp from the Analyze menu.

  • If you choose OIT as your analysis type, the Oxidation Time Window is displayed.

  • If you choose OT as your analysis type, the Oxidation Temperature Window is displayed.

2. Enter the signal threshold to indicate the point at which the deviation from the baseline begins. This is used to account for background noise in the curve.

3. For OIT analyses: Enter the oxidation gas start, which it the time when you started the use of oxygen in your experiment.

4. Click the OK button. Markers will be displayed on the graph.

5. Right click to display the Analyze pop-up menu.

6. Select Point 1 to activate the first marker.

7. Position the marker at the beginning of the region of oxidation onset.

8. Right click to display the Analyze pop-up menu. Select Point 2 to activate the next marker.

9. Position the marker at the end of the region of oxidation.

10. Right click to display the Analyze pop-up menu. Select Accept Limits. The program calculates and displays the oxidation results.
