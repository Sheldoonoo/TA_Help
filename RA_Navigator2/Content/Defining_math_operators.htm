Defining Math Operators

The mathematical operators provided by Navigator 2 (+ - * / 1n exp pow) enable almost any operations in discrete mathematics to be performed. Operations such as taking a root or performing a trigonometric (cos, sin, tan, etc.) or hyperbolic (sinh, cosh, etc.) function are very simple, and the relevant equations are in mathematics textbooks. See also: Variables

Example 1

Taking a root, for example, can be done in at least two ways in Navigator 2. To obtain the cube root of 4

v90 = log (4)
v90 = v90/3
v90 = exp (v90)

Now v90 is 1.587, as it should be.

or

  • Enter the following:

v91 = 1/3
v91 = pow 4, v91)

Now v91 is 1.587.

Example 2

To take the cosine of x, for example cos 32, you would enter as follows:

v90 = v32*0.017453
v92 = pow (v90,2)
v92 = v92/2
v94 = pow (v90,4)
v94 = v94/24
v96 = pow (v90,6)
v96 = v96/720
v98 = pow(v90,8)
v98 = v98/40320
v99 = 1 - v92 + v94 + v96 + v98

Now v99 is 0.8480 as it should be.

To reduce the number of variables used, you could consider using a loop.

You do not, of course, do this every time, but instead write it once and save it as a call script.

Probably the most relevant purpose of this feature of Navigator is that it allows any kind of statistical analysis to be performed. For example, you can simply obtain the mean and standard deviation for a number of measurements, or you can exclude the "silly" values by using the conditionals.

You can also consult mathematics textbooks for simple algorithms for curve fitting (by least squares, for example) and then incorporate the model of your choice into Navigator 2.
