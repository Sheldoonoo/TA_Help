Calibrating the Punching Mechanism

 

When using sealed aluminum pans, the punching operation is dependent on several calibration positions.

To perform the calibration follow the instructions below.

  1. Touch the Calibrate button at the bottom of the touch screen. See Introducing the Touch Screen for more information.

  2. Press the Autosampler button.

  3. Touch the Calibration tab, if it is not already selected.

  1. Select "Calibrate Punch Lower" or "Calibrate Punch Limit" from the list of options at the top of the page using the arrow buttons. Click on the desired link to obtain information:

    • Calibrate Punch Lower

    • Calibrate Punch Limit

Calibrate Punch Lower Position

This calibration is used to adjust the position of the sample tray so that the slit on top of the punching mechanism lines up with the top of the sample pan bail for correct punching.

  1. Use a Phillips screwdriver to loosen the screw on top of the punching mechanism. Slide the mechanism out of the way.

  2. Place the punch alignment fixture in the number 1 position of the sample tray.

  3. Select "Calibrate Punch Lower" from the Calibration page on the touch screen.

  4. Select the GO TO button. The fixture will be moved to the punch position.

  5. Slide the punching mechanism forward until it just touches the fixture.

  6. Use the left/right arrows, followed by the GO TO button,  on the Calibration page touch screen to move the tray until the line on the top of the fixture aligns with the slit in the top of the punching mechanism as shown in the figure to the right.

  7. Use the up/down arrows, followed by the GO TO button, on the touch screen to move the tray up until the punching mechanism touches the fixture's lower alignment platform as shown in the figure below. The punching mechanism should not be loose when you lightly touch the top of it. It should rest solidly on the fixture.

NOTE: The tray will automatically move away from the punching mechanism if they touch with too much force. You will need to repeat the up/down adjustment in steps 7 until the tray stops moving.

  1. Select Save on the touch screen.

  2. Select the Motor Test page on the touch screen. Touch the U/D Load Down button.

  3. Tighten the screw on top of the punching mechanism.

  4. Remove the punch alignment fixture.

Calibrate Punch Limit

This calibration is used to adjust the position of the sample tray so that tray moves allowing the punching mechanism to punch open the sealed pan to the desired amount. Moving the tray up higher during the punching operation will open the pan more. Moving the tray down slightly during the punching operation will open the pan to a lesser degree.

  1. Place a pan and bail in the number 1 position of the sample tray.

  2. Select "Calibrate Punch Limit" from the Calibration page on the touch screen.

  3. Select the GO TO button. The pan will be punched.

  4. Observe the degree to which the pan is opened. See the figure above right for an example of a pan that has been approximately 50 percent opened. It should move up and allow the punching mechanism to push down on the lid enough to open the pan as desired.

  5. Touch the directional arrows,followed by the GO TO button, to move the tray up/down depending on whether you want your pans opened further or to a lesser degree.

  6. Place a fresh, unpunched pan in the number 1 position on the sample tray.

  7. Repeat steps 3, 4, and 5 until the pan has been punched the way you want it.

  8. Select SAVE when the tray has been calibrated to the appropriate limit.
