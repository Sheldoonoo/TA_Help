Experimental Checklist

 

Additional comments on proper experimental setup.

Purge Gas:

The TGA is operated with two purge gas flows—one through the balance housing and one through the furnace/sample area. The former purge, which is always an inert gas like nitrogen, is used to protect the balance assembly from back diffusion of gaseous decomposition products from the furnace area. The latter is the actual purge gas "interacting" with the sample material. This gas can be either inert or reactive and is often switched during the experiment. Depending on the type of furnace installed on the instrument, the following flow rates are recommended:

  • TGA Standard Furnace:  40 mL/minute for the balance and 60 ml/minute for the sample area.

  • TGA EGA Furnace:  10 mL/minute for the balance and 90 ml/minute for the sample area.

Sample Loading:

Three types of sample pans are available: platinum (50 and 100µL), alumina (100, 250, 500 µL) and aluminum (100 µL). Platinum pans are used for most experiments. Typical sample weights for TGA are 20-50 mg. The best sample weight, and the size of the pan required to hold it, will depend on the density of the sample material, the energetics of the decomposition/volatilization process being evaluated, and the tendency for the material to foam during heating.

When comparing TGA results between materials, it is important to keep sample surface area, as well as size, consistent.

NOTE:  If aluminum pans are used, the experimental temperature must not exceed 600°C.

Taring the Pan:

When you are ready to proceed, make sure that you place a clean, empty sample pan on the tray platform, then click the Tare button on the window. If you have an Autosampler, Tare will open the Tare Utility window allowing you to tare the corresponding pan number or the entire tray.

When the taring process has been completed, load your sample into the pan. Make sure that the pan is properly aligned with the groove in the platform.

Starting the Experiment:

After you have completed the steps needed in the wizard, you can:

  • Press the START RUN button to close the wizard and begin this run immediately.

or

  • Select Finish to close the wizard, storing the information you have entered. You may start this experiment later by selecting Control/Start, pressing the START on the tool bar, or pressing the START button located on the instrument touch screen or keypad.
