Setting Up a Strain Sweep Experiment

 

Multistrain (Strain Sweep) experiments are performed over a range of amplitudes (strains) at constant frequency and isothermal temperature. These experiments provide information on the effect of amplitude on material properties. One of the most important applications of this experiment is to determine the linear viscoelastic region (LVR).

If you want to set up a DMA Strain Sweep procedure, you can choose one of two techniques—the DMA Wizard or the Experimental View - Procedure Page. See the instructions below for each technique.

When you perform a DMA Strain Sweep experiment, the following parameters need to be chosen:

  • Frequency:  A single frequency is used throughout the experiment.  1 Hz is recommended.

  • Preload force: This is the force applied to the sample prior to initiating the experiment.  

    • Preload force is not available when you select a nontensioning clamp (i.e., single/dual cantilever, shear sandwich).  

    • Preload force is required with the tensioning clamps (i.e., 3-point bending, compression, and tension film/fiber) when running multifrequency and multistrain experiments in order to sustain oscillation (ensure that force is applied to the sample throughout the complete oscillation).  Typical preload forces for those experiments are 0.01 to 0.5 N.

  • Force Track:  Force track automatically adjusts the total force (preload and dynamic) applied to the sample during multifrequency and multistrain experiments.  

    • Force track is only used when one of the tensioning clamps (i.e., 3-point bend, compression, tension film and tension fiber) is selected. It is not needed for nontensioning clamps (i.e., single/dual cantilever, shear sandwich).

    • Force track essentially determines the dynamic force required to oscillate at the desired amplitude and frequency; it applies an additional static force that is a constant multiple of that dynamic force. This is necessary to avoid sample buckling in tension clamps and loss of contact with the sample for compression and 3-point bending clamps.

    • Recommended force track values are 120% for thin films and low denier fibers (tension film and tension fiber clamps), 150% for thermoplastics (3-point bending clamps), and 125% for general applications.

  • Isothermal Temperature & Soak Time:  Amplitude sweeps for determining the linear viscoelastic region are performed isothermally.  Ambient temperature is commonly used.  When evaluating the material at another temperature [achievable temperature range with gas cooling accessory is -145 to 600°C], it is important to ensure thermal equilibrium is reached before initiating the strain sweep.  A five-minute "soak time" is adequate for most materials.

  • Number of Sweeps:  A single strain sweep is sufficient for determination of linear viscoelastic response.  Repetitive sweeps provide information about structural stability in "softer" materials.  Elastomers, for example, can exhibit lower modulus on subsequent strain sweeps if the time between sweeps is to short to rebuild internal structure destroyed during the initial sweep.

  • Amplitude Table:  All Multistrain mode tests require that you select one or more amplitudes. Up to 50 amplitudes over the range 0.5 to 10,000 mm are possible.  In practice, however, the range of acceptable amplitudes is determined by the stiffness of the sample and the maximum drive force of the DMA (18 N).  Initial recommendation:  Sweep from the lowest (0.5) to 5000 µm at 5 points/decade in log scale. For more information on amplitudes, click here  Amplitude Table.

  • Additional Conditions:  There are additional experimental conditions that can be changed to optimize specific situations.  These are accessed using Advanced Parameters and Post-Test Conditions.  For most experiments, the system default values shown are recommended.

  • See also:  Guidelines for Running Subambient Experiments.

Using the Experimental View Procedure Page:

  1. Display the Procedure Page. Select the desired experiment from the list of available preprogrammed templates.

  2. Enter the desired parameters. When you have finished setting up this procedure, you can enter more information by selecting the Notes Page and Summary Page for this run.

  3. Click the Apply button when finished to save these changes or the Append button to save the changes to this run and add another run to the end of the sequence.

NOTE:  You can use any test template as the basis for a custom test by selecting the desired test template from the list of tests, then click the Apply button and change the test to Custom. Then you can click on the Procedure Page and then the Editor button to make the changes desired.
