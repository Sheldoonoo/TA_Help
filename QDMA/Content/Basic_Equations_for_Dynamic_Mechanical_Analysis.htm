During dynamic testing, an oscillatory (sinusoidal) strain (or stress) is applied to the material and the resulting stress (or strain) developed in the material is measured.  For an ideal solid material, which obeys Hooke’s law, the resulting stress will be proportional to the amplitude of the applied strain.  The strain will be in phase with the stress, i.e., the phase shift (phase angle d) between stress and strain will be 0° (see A in the figure below).  For an ideal fluid, which obeys Newton’s law, the stress will be proportional to the strain rate.  The stress signal will lead the strain signal by 90° (see the figure below).

Most real-world materials exhibit mechanical responses that are a mixture of viscous and elastic behavior.  Thus, they are Viscoelastic.  For a viscoelastic material, the phase angle will lie somewhere between 0° and 90° (as seen in the figure below).

 

As the modulus is defined as the ratio of stress to strain (stress/strain), the resultant stress generated in a viscoelastic material—also referred to as the complex stress (s* or t*)—can be used to calculate the complex modulus E* or G*.  The complex modulus is a measure of the material's resistance to deformation.  It encompasses both elastic and viscous responses. The power of dynamic testing is that, by using the measured phase angle, the stress can be deconvoluted into two parts:

  • An elastic stress (s’ or t’), that is in phase with the strain, and

  • A viscous stress (s” or t”), that is in phase with the strain rate (90° out of phase with the strain).

Back to top

The elastic modulus, or storage modulus (E’ or G’) and the viscous modulus, or loss modulus (E” or G”), can then be calculated directly from the elastic and viscous stress respectively.  A summary of the calculations are as follows:

Complex Modulus:

E* = s*/e

or

E* = E’ + iE”

(Tension, Compression or Bending Clamps)

 

G* = t*/g

or

G* = G’+ iG”

(Shear)

Back to top

Storage Modulus:

E’ = s’/e

or

E’ = E* cos d

(Tension, Compression or Bending)

 

G’ = t’/g

or

G’ = G* cos d

(Shear)

Back to top

Loss Modulus:

E” = s”/e

or

E’ = E* sin d

(Tension, Compression or Bending)

 

G” = t”/g

or

G” = G* sin d

(Shear)
