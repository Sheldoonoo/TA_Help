Replacing the Q5000 SA Humidity Sensor

 

The relative humidity (RH) is controlled by a pair of very precise Mass Flow Controllers (MFCs). Sensors located near the sample and reference provide ongoing verification that the relative humidity achieved is what was requested. Failure of one or both of these sensors does not jeopardize the performance of the instrument.

In the event you need to replace a sensor, follow the instructions below. These instructions may be used to replace the humidity sensor on either the sample (right) or reference (left) side of the Q5000 SA humidity chamber.  

CAUTION:  It is recommended that an ESD grounding strap be worn during this procedure to lower the risk of static electricity damage to the sensor.

Follow the steps below:

  1. Shut down the instrument and turn the power switch to the OFF position.

  2. Loosen the two quarter-turn fasteners on the side of the humidity chamber cover shown to the right. Remove the cover.

  3. Pull open the flap in the insulation to expose the humidity sensor cover plate shown below.

  1. Loosen the four captive screws holding the cover plate on and remove the plate.

  2. Grasp the small tab and pull the sensor carrier board straight out as seen in the exploded diagram below.

  1. Pull the humidity sensor straight off the sensor carrier board being careful not to bend the pins. See the figure below. Discard the defective humidity sensor.

  1. Place the new humidity sensor on the sensor carrier board with the black sensor housing facing away from the four gold pins on the carrier board as shown in the figure above.  Do not force it.

  2. Replace the sensor carrier board onto the sensor base board inside the chamber. The humidity sensor will be suspended in the hole above the sensor base board.  See the figure above.

  3. Replace the cover plate and tighten the four screws. Close the black flap in the insulation.

  4. Turn the power switch on the instrument to ON.

  5. Attach the humidity chamber cover and tighten the quarter-turn fasteners once again. The replacement procedure has been completed.
