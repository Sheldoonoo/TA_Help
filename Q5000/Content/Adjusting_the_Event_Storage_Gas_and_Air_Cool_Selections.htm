Adjusting the Event, Storage, Gas, and Air Cool Selections

 

During an active experiment the data storage, event, gas, and air cool states are controlled by the method parameters and/or method segments. However, you may manually change the state of these selections using the Control menu.

To display the status of these items, select Tools/User Preferences/Status Page and select the desired items to display on the status line. This will allow you to monitor the changes in these items.

Select from the following topics for more information:

  • Changing the Event State

  • Changing the Data Storage State

  • Changing Between Gas #1 and Gas #2

  • Changing the Air Cool State

Changing the Event State

Use Event on the Control menu to activate (on) or deactivate (off) an external device connected to the instrument with the event cable. This control overrides the Event segment in the method.

If the selected Event state is currently active, no action will be seen.

Changing the Data Storage State

Data storage is typically controlled by the method segments; however, you can use Control/Store to manually turn data storage on and off during a running method. This control overrides the Data Storage segment in the method.

If the selected Data Storage option is currently active, no action will be seen.

Changing Between Gas #1 and Gas #2

Q5000 IR Purge Gases

Use Control/Gas/Gas #1/#2 to switch between the primary and secondary purge gases used in the experiment. A purge gas is a gas, often inert (e.g., nitrogen), that flows through the cell during the experiment. For example, oxidative stability experiments use nitrogen for the primary gas and oxygen for the secondary gas.

  • Gas #1 refers to the primary purge gas connected to the instrument. Gas #1 is controlled by the mass flow controller.

  • Gas #2 refers to any secondary purge gas connected to the instrument. Gas #2 is controlled by the mass flow controller.

  • With Mass Flow Controllers a gas may also be switched during an experiment using the Gas Selection method segment.

If the selected Gas state is currently active, no action will be seen.

NOTE: If you are using laboratory purge gas, rather than bottle purge gas, you will need to install an external drier.

CAUTION: Use of corrosive gases will shorten the life of the instrument. The MFC is not designed to work with corrosive gases.

WARNING: Use of an explosive gas as a purge gas is dangerous and is not recommended for this instrument.

Q5000 SA Purge Gas

NOTE:  The Q5000 SA uses only nitrogen as the purge gas for the balance housing and for creating the humidified gas that flows to the sample and reference chambers. Hence, the Q5000 SA only has a Gas 1 port on the back of the instrument for connection to the nitrogen source. In addition, the purge gas cannot be switched during the experiment. See also: Q5000 SA Purge Gases and Flow Rates

Changing the Air Cool State

Q5000 IR Only

Use Air Cool to open (on) or close (off) the internal solenoid valve connected to the Air Cool connector on the exterior of the instrument. Air Cool may be turned on during an experiment, but will not activate until the temperature reaches 500Â°C. The air cool state may also be changed when the experiment has finished.
