Submersion Film/Fiber Clamp

 

This clamp is used to evaluate thin films or fibers while they are submerged in a fluid. The clamp is primarily designed to evaluate polymeric materials in aqueous or buffer solutions at temperatures up to 80°C. Because of the volume of fluid (and its associated heat capacity), these submersion measurements are usually performed under isothermal conditions.

 

This section describes the use of this clamp with film samples. Similar procedures are used for evaluating fiber samples. Samples that are in the stiffness range of 100 N/m to 10,000,000 N/m can be evaluated. See Running an Experiment, step 4.

 

 

WARNING:  Proper clamp selection within the instrument control software (e.g., submersion film/fiber and submersion compression) is required to ensure that the 80°C upper temperature range cannot be exceeded.

WARNING:  Flammable or volatile fluids should not be used as the submersion fluid.

CAUTION: The Submersion Film/Fiber Tension clamp is designed primarily for isothermal experiments in the temperature range of subambient to 80°C. Heating ramp experiments can also be performed at <2.0°C/minute. The clamp is designed for aqueous or buffer solutions and should not be used with fluids whose vapor pressure results in volatility at the temperature of usage.

Preparing Samples

The clamp can hold samples with a 15 mm fixed length, a width up to 6.5 mm, and a thickness up to 2 mm. When preparing samples make sure that your samples do not exceed these measures. Cut sample fibers and films to those specifications, then mount the sample as directed before adding the submersion fluid to the fluid container.

Operating Range of the Submersion Film/Fiber Clamp

Geometry Factor Equation

The geometry factor, GF, in the equation below can help you determine the optimum size for the sample material or determine if a sample of a particular size can be measured.  

GF = L/A

where:

A = cross-sectional area = width x thickness (mm2)

L = sample length, which is fixed to 15 mm, so...

GF (1/mm)   =   15/A

for the submersion film tension Clamp

The sample modulus is calculated by multiplying the measured sample stiffness, Ks, by the geometry factor, GF.

 Modulus (MPa) = (Ks x GF)/1000

Modulus (MPa) = [Ks x (15/A)]/1000

where the measurable stiffness range of the instrument is a fixed quantity between 100 to 10,000,000 N/m.  

Note we can solve the modulus equation above for stiffness as follows:

Ks (N/m) = (Modulus/GF) x 1000

Ks (N/m) = [(Modulus x A)/15] x 1000

Since the modulus of the material is an intrinsic property—i.e., independent of the sample dimensions—it can be seen that the sample dimensions must be selected to yield stiffness values within the allowable measurable range of the instrument.

Mounting Samples

After the submersion film/fiber tension clamp has been installed and calibrated (see Installing/Removing the Submersion Film/Fiber Clamp), you can mount the sample using the following steps:

NOTE:  The mounting of film samples is critical to achieving accurate and reproducible results.  

  1. Measure and note the width and thickness dimensions of test sample (or appropriate dimensions if other geometries are used).  

  2. Load sample into the upper and lower clamps of the Sample Loading Assembly. Slide the sample in from the bottom of the Sample Loading Assembly. Refer to the two figures below.

  1. Using the torque wrench, tighten the top and bottom sample clamp screws to the appropriate clamping torque [1 to 2 in-lbs. (10 to 20 cm-N) for a rigid sample].

  2. Press the DRIVE Float/Lock key on the instrument touch screen to release (float) the clamp.

  3. Insert the Sample-Loading Assembly into the cup of the fluid tank.  

  4. Tighten the two captive screws onto the yoke and four captive screws onto the tank to install the Sample Loading Assembly.

  5. Press the DRIVE Float/Lock key on the instrument touch screen to lock the clamp into position.  

  6. Unscrew the four hex screws to remove the crossbar loading fixture (shown alone in the figure to the right). This is the way the clamp will be assembled while the experiment is run.  (See the figure below for the clamp configuration.) The sample is now loaded and ready for testing.  The sample length will be set to 15 mm after loading.

  1. Carefully fill the fluid container (cup). It is recommended that the fluid be loaded into cup using a squeeze bottle, small funnel, pipette, or other device that will direct flow of fluid directly into cup without spilling or splashing the fluid.

CAUTION:  OVERFILLING THE CUP OR SPILLING DURING FILLING can result in fluid getting down into the air bearing, which could cause permanent damage to the instrument.

Running an Experiment

When you use the submersion film tension clamp for experiments, you will need to set up the experimental parameters using the instrument control software.

Follow these general guidelines when using the submersion film clamp:

  1. Use the Summary Page to select the clamp type, mode, etc.

  2. Set up the instrument parameters for the type of experiment desired. See Available DMA Test Templates. Note that the amplitude range of the DMA is 0.5 to 10,000 microns. The upper amplitude range is limited to 2,000 microns when using the submersion film clamp. This amplitude range is a physical limit that is limited by distance between the yoke and the bottom of the fluid container. This clamp is a tensioning clamp; therefore, preload force and force track values must be selected. The recommended values for preload force values are 0.005 to 1 N. The recommended values for force track are 120 to 150% (when appropriate).

NOTE:  Temperature ramp experiments may be performed using the submersion film/fiber clamps, but only at low rates (2.0 °C/min or lower). For better thermal equilibration it may be best to perform temperature sweeps (measurements at different isothermal temperatures).

  1. Press the MEASURE key on the DMA.  This will apply the preload force and start the motor in the desired operation mode, but the readings will not be saved to a file.

  2. View the signals.  When running a dynamic experiment (multifrequency and multistrain mode) pay special attention to these signals:

    • Amplitude:  This signal should achieve and maintain the value programmed.  If running a multistrain experiment, the amplitude will cycle through the values programmed.

    • Stiffness:  The stiffness should be within the instrument’s measurable range of 100 N/m to 10,000,000 N/m.  If it is not, refer to “Operating Range of Submersion Film/Fiber Clamp” to make appropriate adjustments.

    • Drive Force:  The drive force should be between 0.0001 and 18 N.  If the drive force is not within this range, either increase the programmed amplitude to increase the drive force, or increase the stiffness of the sample by changing the physical dimensions of the sample.  Since the length is fixed in the submersion film clamp, the width and thickness will need to be increased in order to increase the sample stiffness.

    • Force Track:  If force track is used, we recommend a minimum setting of 200% to get reliable modulus values.

    • Preload Force:  If force track is used, the preload force will automatically be adjusted to remain a set percentage greater than the force required to drive the sample at the programmed amplitude.  If force track is not used, the preload force should read the value set in the instrument parameters.  For more information regarding preload force, see Understanding the DMA Forces.

If the initial conditions are acceptable, (i.e., smooth oscillation and good modulus values), start the experiment. See Starting a Run and/or Sequence.

If the values are not acceptable, then change conditions appropriately.

Removing Samples

After you have finished your experiment, you can remove the sample from the submersion film clamp as follows:

  1. Press the DRIVE Float/Lock key on the instrument touch screen to release (float) the clamp.

  2. Attach the crossbar loading fixture using its four captive hex screws.

  3. Press the DRIVE Float/Lock key on the instrument to lock the drive shaft.

  4. Remove the Sample Loading Assembly from the rest of the clamp as follows:

    1. Loosen the two captive screws from the yoke and four captive screws from the tank enough to remove the Sample Loading Assembly as one piece. (See the figure below.)

  1. Carefully remove the Sample-Loading Assembly, letting excess fluid drip into the cup.  

  2. Wipe excess fluid from Sample-Loading Assembly.

  3. Loosen the clamp setscrews and slide the sample out from the bottom. Load a new sample, if desired.
