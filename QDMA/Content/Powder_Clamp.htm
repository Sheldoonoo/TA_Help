Intended Use

The powder clamp is intended for characterizing primary and secondary transitions in powder materials.  As in any DMA experiment, storage modulus, loss modulus, and tan delta information are collected.  Transitions are most easily observed as peaks in tan delta or loss modulus.  The peak temperature of transitions obtained in DMA experiments will depend on testing conditions such as frequency and heating rate, and testing conditions should be reported along with transition information.  The magnitude of the storage and loss moduli data generated using the powder clamp is qualitative only. Put another way, the modulus numbers generated by the software do not represent the true modulus of the powder material, thus quantitative sample comparisons should never be made using the modulus data.

Maximum Use Temperature

Never heat powder samples above a melt transition or decomposition. This may cause significant problems when removing the upper plate from the lower tray after testing. In addition, it can lead to contamination of the clamps, making cleaning difficult. If the melting or decomposition temperature of the powder is unknown, it should first be determined using calorimetry (DSC) or thermogravimetry (TGA) respectively. Set the upper testing temperature 10°C to 20°C below the melting/decomposition temperature.

Using the Powder Clamp

Follow these steps to install and use the powder clamp.

  1. Install and calibrate the 35-mm dual cantilever clamp. See Installing/Removing the Single/Dual Cantilever Clamp and Calibrating DMA Clamps for details. After calibrating the clamp compliance, leave the steel compliance sample in and proceed to the next step.

  2. Lock the shaft of the instrument before removing the steel compliance sample. This will keep the lower clamp faces of the cantilever clamp level, facilitating loading of the powder clamp. It is highly recommended that aluminum foil be used to cover the lower clamps before loading the holder. This will prevent the clamp surfaces from being contaminated should a sample flow out of the sides of the holder.

  3. Prior to loading the sample into the lower tray of powder clamp, sprinkle a small amount of dry calcined alumina onto the base of the tray. See the figure to the right. This material is included with the powder clamp.  Use the material sparingly. It is not necessary to form a complete layer of alumina powder on the tray. This material acts as a release agent, preventing the upper plate and lower tray from sticking together.

  4. Load the powder sample into the lower tray using a small spatula. Do not overfill the powder clamp. Try to fill the lower tray only to the top of the sides of the lower tray. It is easier to locate the upper plate in a slightly under filled clamp.

  5. Sprinkle a small amount of alumina on top of the powder sample to prevent sticking to the upper plate.

Suggested Testing Parameters and Method

The suggested oscillation amplitude is 20 µm and a 1 Hz frequency.

A typical method for subambient operation is as follows:

  • Motor Drive: Off

  • Initial Temperature: -80°C (for example)

  • Motor Drive: On

  • Isothermal: 5 min (for example)

  • Ramp: 2°C/min to 140°C (for example)

Cleaning and Maintenance

Thoroughly clean the powder clamp after use, making sure the cantilever clamp and powder clamp surfaces are free of contamination. Contamination may lead to transitions in the data that and may confuse analysis.

The powder clamp is designed to be re-useable but caution is required. Use of the alumina as described above will help prevent sticking in most instances.

Guidelines for handling the powder clamp:

  • If the upper plate should stick, use pliers to grip either side of the middle raised block on the upper tray and pull up carefully. In most instances, this will free the upper plate.

  • If this is not effective, heat the holder on a hot plate and then remove the upper plate. Once apart, the holder will normally clean up easily with an appropriate solvent.

  • If there are areas of the clamp where sample remains, try scraping with a spatula. Once the bulk of the material has been removed, use a fiberglass brush to remove any remaining residue.
