Installing/Removing the Fiber Tension Clamp

 

The procedures that follow explain the installation and removal of the fiber tension clamp, which is an optional DMA clamp fixture. The fiber tension clamp can be used for individual thin fiber samples or bundles of fibers.

See also:

Fiber Tension Clamp

Installing the Clamp

To install the fiber tension clamp on the DMA instrument, follow these steps (see the figure below for identification of parts):

  1. Remove the pin vises from both the fixed and moveable clamps by unscrewing the vise collars.

  2. Press the CLAMP key to raise the drive shaft.

  3. Press the DRIVE Float/Lock key to lock the drive shaft in the upper position.

  4. Slide the dovetail of the moveable clamp into the dovetail holder of the drive shaft.  Align the dovetail so that it is flush with the dovetail holder.

  5. Use the hex key to tighten the setscrew in the center of the moveable clamp.

  6. Seat the fixed clamp firmly onto all four of the mounting posts.  Then tighten the screws with a hex wrench.  Do not over tighten the attachment screws.

  7. Place the pin vises into the fixed and moveable clamps.

  8. Select the appropriate clamp type and mode using the Instrument Control program.

  9. Calibrate the clamp mass and clamp zero (see Calibrating the DMA.)

Removing the Clamp

To remove the fiber tension clamp, follow these steps:

  1. Loosen, but do not remove, the four hex screws that hold the fixed clamp in place on the mounting posts.

  2. Lift off the fixed clamp.

  3. Remove the knurled vise collar and pin vise inside from the moveable clamp.

  4. Use the hex key to loosen the setscrew in the center of the moveable clamp.

  5. Slide the dovetail of the moveable clamp out of the dovetail holder of the drive shaft.
