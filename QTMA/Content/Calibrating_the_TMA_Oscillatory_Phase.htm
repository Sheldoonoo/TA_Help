Calibrating the TMA Oscillatory Phase

 

The oscillatory phase calibration performs a 0.1N frequency sweep on a 0.020 inch steel wire sample over the range 0.01 Hz to 2.0 Hz and captures the phase angle at each of the 9 frequencies.  The collected data is analyzed by the instrument to produce a calibration quadratic in Radians, Radians per Hz, and Radians per HzÂ².

To perform the calibration:

  1. Select Calibrate/Oscillatory Phase Calibration from the instrument control menu. The first window will be displayed. Follow the directions on the window as follows.
  2. Clear the weight tray and sample stage.
  3. Install the Flexure probe.
  4. Click the Calibrate button to calibrate the probe.
  5. Place the low-friction, three-point bend fixture on the stage (shown in the figure to the right) and lower the probe into its central V-groove (without a sample) to align the fixture with the probe.
  6. Place the phase compliance wire on the groove in the rollers of the low-friction three point bend calibration fixture (see figure to the right).
  7. Click the Zero Probe button to zero the probe.
  8. Verify/enter sample dimensions:  Length is 10.16 mm,  Diameter is 0.508 mm.
  9. Press the FURNACE button to close the furnace.
  10. Click the Calibrate button to begin the calibration and wait about 24 minutes for the calibration to be completed and saved.
