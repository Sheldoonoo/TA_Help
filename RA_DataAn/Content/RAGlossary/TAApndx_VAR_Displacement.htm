Displacement

Description

The angular displacement: other than in an oscillation experiment, the angle through which the rheometer rotor has moved from the last position at which it was zeroed. In an oscillation experiment, the amplitude of the sinusoidal displacement. The strain is calculated from the displacement and the measuring system shear rate factor. The term angular is often omitted, as the motion of the rotor is always circular.

Symbol 

f   (phi)  or f0 for the displacement amplitude
