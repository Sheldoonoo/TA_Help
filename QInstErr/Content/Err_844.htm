Message 844

Switch to gas #2.

This is an informational message to let you know that the gas control has been switched to gas #2. To switch to gas #1 select Control/Gas/1 from the menu.

A purge gas is a gas, often inert (e.g., nitrogen), that flows through the cell during the experiment. For example, oxidative stability experiments use nitrogen for the primary gas and oxygen for the secondary gas.

  • Gas #1 refers to the primary purge gas connected to the instrument. Gas #1 is controlled by the Mass Flow Controller or plumbed directly to the Gas 1 connection.
  • Gas #2 refers to any secondary purge gas connected to the instrument. Gas #2 is controlled by the Mass Flow Controller or plumbed directly to the Gas 2 connection.
